# Projection Types

This document defines **canonical projection types**
derived from the Brain, as specified by the System Momentum Canon.

Projection types describe **structural intent and audience scope**.
They do not introduce authority, validity, or commitment.

---

## Canonical Classification

All projections fall into one of the following canonical types:

- **Public Projection**
- **Operational Projection**
- **Analytical Projection**

This classification is **closed**.
New projection types may not be introduced.

---

## Public Projection

Public projections are intended for **external visibility**.

Characteristics:

- broad audience
- reduced detail
- non-actionable by default
- non-binding
- optimized for perception and interpretation

Public projections must not:

- expose operational controls
- enable governance
- imply institutional commitment

---

## Operational Projection

Operational projections are intended for **system operation and coordination**.

Characteristics:

- constrained audience
- actionable visibility
- tied to operational roles
- non-authoritative

Operational projections may:

- expose system state
- enable coordination
- support execution decisions

Operational projections must not:

- define semantic authority
- create institutional memory
- assert validity

---

## Analytical Projection

Analytical projections are intended for **analysis, reasoning, and inspection**.

Characteristics:

- high fidelity
- exploratory
- non-operational
- non-binding

Analytical projections may:

- combine multiple Brain views
- apply analytical lenses
- support reflective processes

Analytical projections must not:

- drive action directly
- define governance outcomes

---

## OSS as Operational Projection

**OSS (Operational System Surface)** is a subtype of **Operational Projection**.

OSS characteristics:

- exposes live operational state
- supports coordination and execution
- bounded by authority constraints
- revocable and regenerable

OSS does not:

- hold authority
- persist institutional memory
- redefine Brain semantics

---

## Non-Equivalence Rules

- Public ≠ Operational
- Operational ≠ Analytical
- Analytical ≠ Governance
- OSS ≠ Authority

Cross-use of projections outside their canonical intent
constitutes a canonical violation.
