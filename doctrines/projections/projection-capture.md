# Projection Capture

Projection capture is one of the most dangerous
and least visible failure modes in complex systems.

It occurs when a projection stops being treated as a view
and starts being treated as reality.

---

## How Capture Begins

Projection capture does not begin with deception.
It begins with convenience.

A projection is adopted because it is:

- clear
- stable
- actionable
- easy to reference

Over time, it becomes the default interface
through which the system is understood.

At that point, questioning the projection
feels like questioning reality itself.

---

## From View to Truth

Once captured, a projection acquires properties
it was never designed to have.

It becomes:

- a source of justification
- a proxy for truth
- a substitute for verification

Decisions are no longer explained.
They are _pointed to_.

The projection no longer supports reasoning.
It replaces it.

---

## Loss of Corrective Capacity

The most critical effect of projection capture
is the loss of correction.

Signals that do not appear in the projection
cease to exist for decision-makers.

Errors outside the projection frame
are dismissed as noise, anomalies, or irrelevance.

At this stage, the system can no longer learn
from its own failures.

---

## Social Reinforcement

Projection capture is reinforced socially.

People align their behavior
to what the projection rewards.

Contradictory signals are:

- deprioritized
- discouraged
- or punished

Eventually, only projection-compatible behavior survives.
The system appears stable,
precisely because it has stopped seeing instability.

---

## Why Capture Is Hard to Detect

Projection capture rarely looks like failure.

It looks like:

- consistent metrics
- confident reporting
- smooth operation
- reduced disagreement

By the time reality contradicts the projection,
the cost of correction is already high.

Often, it requires external shock
to break the projection frame.

---

## The Only Real Defense

Projection capture cannot be prevented
by better projections.

It can only be mitigated by:

- explicit awareness that projections are views
- tolerance for contradictory signals
- parallel projections that do not converge too early
- cultural permission to question visibility itself

This is not a technical safeguard.
It is an epistemic discipline.

---

## Closing

A system captured by its projections
does not collapse loudly.

It collapses confidently.

By the time the projection fails,
the system no longer remembers
how to see without it.
