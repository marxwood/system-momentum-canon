# Operational Projections — Doctrinal View

Operational Projections support coordination and day-to-day operation.

They expose **current system state**
without conferring authority or commitment.

---

## Purpose

Operational projections exist to:

- coordinate action
- monitor progress
- surface signals
- support execution

They are tools for operators,
not instruments of governance.

---

## What Operational Projections May Contain

- live dashboards
- task states
- queues
- alerts

They may change rapidly
without altering canonical state.

---

## What Operational Projections Must Never Do

- commit decisions
- redefine priorities
- create obligations
- bypass governance
- rewrite memory

If operators act directly on operational signals
without governance,
authority has leaked.

---

## Typical Failure Modes

- managing indicators instead of outcomes
- treating “green” as success
- escalating signals into commitments
- bypassing governance for speed

Operational projections are volatile by design.
Stability must exist elsewhere.
