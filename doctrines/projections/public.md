# Public Projections — Doctrinal View

Public Projections are outward-facing representations of the system.

They exist to communicate, inform, and expose selected aspects
of Brain state to the external world.

They do not carry authority.

---

## Purpose

Public projections exist to:

- communicate positions and states
- provide transparency
- enable public understanding

They are not instruments of governance.

---

## What Public Projections May Contain

- published claims
- official summaries
- curated narratives
- time-delayed or filtered views

Everything shown publicly
has already passed through governance elsewhere.

---

## What Public Projections Must Never Do

- create commitments
- introduce new claims
- decide policy
- override governance
- act as proof of validity

A public projection may be misleading
without the system itself being wrong.

---

## Audience and Risk

Public projections are optimized for:

- legibility
- clarity
- stability

They are not optimized for:

- completeness
- internal nuance
- operational detail

Optimizing public projections for persuasion
instead of truth is a governance failure upstream.

---

## Typical Failure Modes

- treating press as policy
- reacting to public optics
- governing through announcements
- confusing visibility with legitimacy

Public projections reflect decisions.
They must never replace them.
