# Ownership and Accountability

Organizations do not fail because people make mistakes.

They fail because mistakes have no repair owner.

Ownership is the interface between action and repair.

Accountability is the mechanism that enforces ownership.

---

## Ownership is not hierarchy

Ownership is not seniority.

Ownership is the binding between:

- an action surface
- and a repair obligation

A junior person can own an action surface.

A senior person can be unable to own it.

Ownership is not status.

Ownership is responsibility.

---

## Accountability is not punishment

Accountability is not revenge.

Accountability is responsibility tracing.

It answers:

- who declared intent
- who executed
- who approved
- who evaluated
- who knew
- and who ignored evidence

Without this, organizations cannot learn.

They can only scapegoat.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is accountability collapse.

Accountability collapses into:

- blame diffusion
- scapegoating
- immunity
- or fear-based silence

All four outcomes produce capture.

---

## Failure modes

### Blame diffusion

Everyone is responsible.

No one repairs.

### Scapegoating

One person is punished.

The system is unchanged.

Failures repeat.

### Immunity

Certain actors cannot be held accountable.

This is capture.

### Fear-based silence

Accountability becomes humiliation.

People hide evidence.

Evidence collapse produces delusion.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. ownership bindings for intent, execution, and repair
2. accountability ledgers that are inspectable
3. consequences that are bounded and symmetric
4. protection for contradiction and evidence reporting

If accountability becomes fear, correction becomes impossible.
