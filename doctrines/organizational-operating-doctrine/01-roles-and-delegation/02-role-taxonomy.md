# Role Taxonomy

Organizations fail when roles exist as titles.

Correction-capable organizations require roles that exist as interfaces.

This module defines a minimal taxonomy of roles needed for corrigible execution.

It is not an org chart.

It is a responsibility surface map.

---

## Core role types

### Intent Owner

Declares intent.

Owns legitimacy.

Owns consequences.

### Execution Owner

Owns delivery of the action.

Owns constraints during execution.

### Evidence and Provenance Owner

Owns evidence collection.

Owns provenance integrity.

Owns traceability.

### Evaluator

Owns quality gates.

Owns acceptance criteria.

Owns correction triggers.

### Risk and Policy Owner

Owns constraints.

Owns permission boundaries.

Owns risk tiering.

### Incident and Repair Owner

Owns failure response.

Owns containment.

Owns learning loops.

### Dependency Owner

Owns external dependencies.

Owns exit paths.

Owns lock-in awareness.

---

## Why this taxonomy exists

This taxonomy exists because most organizations collapse into:

- “everyone is responsible”
  or
- “the leader is responsible”

Both outcomes destroy correction.

Responsibility must be:

- explicit
- bounded
- and inspectable

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is role collapse into status.

Status-driven roles produce:

- immunity
- blame diffusion
- and theater

The organization appears coordinated.

It becomes uncorrectable.

---

## Failure modes

### Missing ownership

No one owns repair.

Failures repeat.

### Role stacking without boundaries

One actor owns everything.

This creates single-point capture.

### Role theater

Roles exist on paper.

Decisions are still informal.

### Role mismatch

People are held accountable for what they cannot control.

This produces fear and silence.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit assignment of the core role types
2. separation between intent ownership and evaluation ownership
3. traceability between actions and owners
4. escalation paths when role boundaries conflict

If roles cannot be inspected, accountability becomes theater.
