# Escalation and Override

Delegation without escalation produces rogue execution.

Escalation without override produces theater.

Override without accountability produces capture.

This module defines escalation and override as survivability interfaces.

---

## Escalation is not failure

Escalation is not incompetence.

Escalation is the mechanism that prevents:

- local optimization
- and bounded tasks
  from becoming systemic damage

A correction-capable organization treats escalation as normal.

---

## Override is not domination

Override is not hierarchy performance.

Override is the mechanism that stops:

- unsafe execution
- or runaway consequence cascades

Override exists to preserve correction capacity.

Not to preserve ego.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is escalation collapse.

Escalation collapses when:

- people fear punishment
- people fear humiliation
- or people believe escalation is disloyalty

When escalation collapses, the organization becomes blind.

Blind organizations stabilize through coercion or delusion.

---

## Failure modes

### Escalation suppression

People do not escalate.

Failures become incidents.

Incidents become crises.

### Override as immunity

Overrides are not contestable.

This creates executive capture.

### Override as scapegoat

Overrides are used to assign blame.

This destroys trust and contradiction.

### Override without repair

Overrides stop action but do not trigger repair.

The same failure repeats.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. survivable escalation paths
2. override mechanisms with bounded authority
3. auditability of overrides and escalations
4. repair triggers following overrides

If escalation is punished, the organization will learn silence.

If override is immune, the organization will learn capture.
