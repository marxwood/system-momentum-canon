# Delegation Contracts

Delegation is a capability transfer.

A delegation contract is the boundary object that prevents capability transfer from becoming unbounded.

Contracts do not need to be legal.

They need to be inspectable.

---

## What a delegation contract contains

A delegation contract defines:

- intent scope
- permitted actions
- forbidden actions
- evidence requirements
- evaluation gates
- escalation triggers
- and repair ownership

Without these, delegation becomes “trust”.

Trust becomes theater.

Theater becomes capture.

---

## Contracts are not bureaucracy

Contracts are not meant to slow work.

They are meant to prevent:

- rogue execution
- blame diffusion
- and urgency exemptions

A fast organization without contracts becomes fast delusion.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is implicit delegation.

Implicit delegation occurs when:

- authority is social
- approval is informal
- constraints are assumed
- and repair is undefined

Implicit delegation produces:

- unbounded action
- and unbounded blame

---

## Failure modes

### Scope creep

Delegation expands silently.

Capability becomes unbounded.

### Constraint disappearance

Constraints are ignored under urgency.

Urgency becomes capture accelerator.

### Evaluation bypass

Work ships without quality gates.

This produces drift and hidden debt.

### Repair ambiguity

Failures occur with no repair owner.

This produces repeat failure.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit delegation contracts for high-impact work
2. escalation and override triggers inside contracts
3. evidence and provenance requirements
4. repair ownership bindings

If contracts do not exist, delegation becomes power without constraints.
