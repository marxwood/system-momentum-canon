# Human and System Role Boundaries

Organizations delegate to increase capability.

Delegation can be directed to:

- people
- teams
- or systems

The core risk is not delegation itself.

The risk is delegation without boundaries.

Boundaries are what preserve correction.

---

## Boundaries are not about dignity

Boundaries are not about preserving human status.

They are about preventing:

- unbounded action
- blame diffusion
- and capture through capability

A system that can act without ownership becomes power.

Power without constraints becomes capture.

---

## What humans do

Humans are uniquely suited for:

- legitimacy-bearing decisions
- moral responsibility
- and irreversible consequence ownership

Humans are also the primary carriers of:

- accountability
- repair obligations
- and escalation authority

This is not because humans are superior.

It is because organizations are still embedded in human legitimacy structures.

---

## What systems do

Systems are uniquely suited for:

- high-frequency execution
- monitoring and evidence capture
- consistency enforcement
- and bounded automation inside explicit constraints

Systems are not moral agents.

They do not own consequences.

They can produce consequences.

Ownership must remain explicit.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is boundary collapse.

Boundary collapse occurs when:

- systems act as if they own legitimacy
- humans act as if they own immunity
- and delegation becomes “trust us”

Boundary collapse produces:

- rogue execution
- symbolic accountability
- and emergency capture

---

## Failure modes

### Capability without ownership

Actions occur with no repair owner.

This produces permanent drift.

### Human rubber-stamping

Humans approve without understanding.

This converts oversight into theater.

### System scapegoating

Failures are blamed on systems.

This destroys accountability.

### Human immunity

Humans become immune because systems are blamed.

This is capture.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit ownership for every action surface
2. bounded permissions for systems
3. human escalation and override that is operational
4. auditability of system decisions and human approvals

If boundaries collapse, capability becomes capture.
