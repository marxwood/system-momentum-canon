# Governance, Risk, and Evaluation

Organizations collapse when they treat governance as:

- compliance theater
- or control fetish

Governance exists for one purpose:

to preserve correction capacity under power.

Risk exists for one purpose:

to prevent irreversible harm.

Evaluation exists for one purpose:

to detect drift before it becomes collapse.

---

## Governance is not morality

Governance is not virtue.

Governance is constraint engineering.

Constraints exist because capability naturally expands.

Expanded capability naturally becomes capture.

---

## Risk is not paranoia

Risk is not fear.

Risk is consequence mapping under uncertainty.

Organizations that refuse to do risk work do not become brave.

They become reckless.

Recklessness becomes coercion later.

Because reality forces correction.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is governance drift.

Governance drifts into:

- selective enforcement
- immunity
- symbolic policy
- or bureaucracy bloat

All four produce capture.

---

## What this section covers

This section defines:

- policy and permissions as capability boundaries
- risk classes as evaluation tiers
- human-in-the-loop boundaries as legitimacy interfaces
- evaluation and quality gates
- change management and reversibility
- incident response as correction loop

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. constraints that are symmetric (no immunity)
2. risk tiering for autonomy
3. evaluation gates that cannot be bypassed by urgency
4. incident response that produces repair, not scapegoating

If governance cannot constrain power, the organization will stabilize through coercion.
