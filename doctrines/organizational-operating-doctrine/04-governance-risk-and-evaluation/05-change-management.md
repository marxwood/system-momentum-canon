# Change Management

Organizations do not fail because they change.

They fail because change is irreversible.

Irreversible change makes correction impossible.

Change management is the discipline of:

- reversibility
- staging
- and repair

---

## Change is not innovation theater

Change is not a slogan.

Change is a sequence of actions that modifies:

- capability
- incentives
- and constraints

Any change that increases capability without increasing correction capacity is dangerous.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is change as domination.

Change becomes domination when:

- it is imposed without repair paths
- it is justified by urgency
- and dissent is treated as disloyalty

Domination produces silence.

Silence produces drift.

---

## Failure modes

### Big bang change

Large irreversible change is deployed.

Correction becomes impossible.

### Change without rollback

Rollback is absent.

Fear becomes normal.

### Change without repair ownership

Failures occur with no repair plan.

This becomes chaos.

### Change without legitimacy

Change is pushed through authority alone.

This produces factional war.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. staged change
2. reversibility for high-impact changes
3. explicit repair ownership
4. legitimacy interfaces for dissent and contradiction

If change is irreversible, the organization will stabilize through fear.
