# Incident Response

Incidents are not failures of competence.

They are failures of correction capacity.

Incident response is not a firefight.

It is the organization’s reality-contact reflex.

---

## Incident response is not blame

Incident response is not punishment.

Punishment produces silence.

Silence produces repeated incidents.

Incident response exists to:

- stop harm
- preserve evidence
- restore service
- and trigger repair

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is incident theater.

Incident theater occurs when:

- incidents are hidden
- incidents are narrated as heroism
- or incidents are used to purge

All three destroy learning.

---

## Failure modes

### Cover-up

Evidence is destroyed.

Memory collapses.

### Hero narrative

Incidents become status events.

Repair becomes secondary.

### Purge response

Incidents trigger scapegoating.

Systems remain unchanged.

### No repair loop

Incidents end at restoration.

Failures repeat.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. rapid containment of harm
2. evidence preservation
3. auditability of incident decisions
4. post-incident repair obligations

If incidents cannot produce repair, the organization will accumulate hidden instability.
