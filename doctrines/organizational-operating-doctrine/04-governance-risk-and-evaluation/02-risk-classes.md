# Risk Classes

Organizations fail when they treat all work as equal.

Not all work is equal.

Some work is reversible.

Some work is irreversible.

Risk classes exist to preserve correction capacity by:

- tiering autonomy
- tiering review
- and tiering permissions

---

## Risk is consequence, not fear

Risk is not how scary something feels.

Risk is:

- magnitude of potential harm
- and reversibility of harm
- under uncertainty

Organizations that classify risk by emotion produce theater.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is risk flattening.

Risk flattening occurs when:

- everything is “low risk”
- or everything is “high risk”

Both outcomes destroy correction.

---

## Failure modes

### Everything is low risk

Autonomy expands without evaluation.

Drift becomes collapse.

### Everything is high risk

Execution slows.

Shadow work appears.

Shadow work bypasses governance.

### Risk by politics

Risk class is assigned by status.

This becomes immunity.

### Risk by narrative

Risk is justified by stories.

This becomes theater.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit risk tiers for actions and outputs
2. review gates tied to tiers
3. permissions tied to tiers
4. escalation triggers for tier changes

If risk cannot be tiered, autonomy becomes either reckless or paralyzed.
