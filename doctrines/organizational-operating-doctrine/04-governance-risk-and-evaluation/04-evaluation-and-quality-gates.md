# Evaluation and Quality Gates

Organizations fail when evaluation is optional.

Optional evaluation becomes:

- skipped under urgency
- performed selectively
- or replaced by narrative

Narrative is not evaluation.

---

## Gates are not perfection

Quality gates are not meant to eliminate all error.

They are meant to:

- detect drift early
- prevent irreversible harm
- and trigger repair loops

A gate that aims for perfection becomes bureaucracy.

A gate that aims for speed becomes theater.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is gate bypass.

Gate bypass occurs when:

- urgency is treated as legitimacy
- status overrides evaluation
- or evaluation is outsourced to reputation

Reputation is not evidence.

---

## Failure modes

### Shipping as proof

Output is treated as validation.

This produces drift.

### Selective evaluation

Some work is evaluated.

Others are immune.

This is capture.

### Gate overload

Everything requires heavy review.

Shadow work emerges.

### Metric substitution

Evaluation becomes one number.

Gaming becomes inevitable.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit quality gates for high-impact work
2. gates tied to risk classes
3. auditability of bypass events
4. repair triggers when gates fail

If evaluation cannot be enforced, the organization becomes a drift engine.
