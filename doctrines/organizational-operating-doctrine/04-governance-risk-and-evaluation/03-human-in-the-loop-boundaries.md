# Human-in-the-Loop Boundaries

Human involvement is not a moral preference.

It is a legitimacy interface.

Organizations fail when human-in-the-loop is treated as:

- a checkbox
- or a trust ritual

The point is not “a human saw it”.

The point is:

a responsible owner accepted repair obligations.

---

## Human review is not enough

Human review without:

- evidence
- provenance
- and consequence mapping

is only narrative approval.

Narrative approval is theater.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is responsibility laundering.

Responsibility laundering occurs when:

- humans approve without understanding
- or humans approve under urgency
- or humans approve because they cannot refuse

This produces:

- fake accountability
- and real harm

---

## Failure modes

### Rubber-stamp approval

Humans approve because the system asked.

This is legitimacy theater.

### Approval by intimidation

Humans approve because refusal is punished.

This is coercion.

### Approval without repair

Humans approve but do not own repair.

This is blame diffusion.

### Hidden autonomy

Systems act without review.

This is rogue capability.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit boundaries for when human approval is required
2. clear ownership of repair when approval is granted
3. survivable refusal paths (approval must be contestable)
4. auditability of approvals and refusals

If human review is a checkbox, it will be used as cover.
