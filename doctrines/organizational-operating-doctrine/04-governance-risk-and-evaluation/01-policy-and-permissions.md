# Policy and Permissions

Organizations collapse when permissions are social.

Social permissions are invisible.

Invisible permissions are unaccountable.

Unaccountable permissions become capture.

This module treats permissions as the operational form of power.

---

## Policy is a capability boundary

Policy is not a document.

Policy is the boundary that defines:

- what may be done
- by whom
- with what evidence
- under what audit
- and with what repair obligation

A policy that does not bind capability is theater.

---

## Permissions are not trust

Permissions are not “we trust this team”.

Trust is not inspectable.

Permissions must be inspectable.

Inspectable permissions are corrigible.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is permission creep.

Permission creep occurs when:

- access accumulates
- exceptions become permanent
- and urgency becomes justification

Permission creep is how organizations become capturable.

---

## Failure modes

### Social access

People gain access by relationships.

This is factional power.

### Exception permanence

Temporary exceptions never expire.

This becomes unbounded capability.

### Selective enforcement

Some actors are constrained.

Others are immune.

This is capture.

### Policy as paperwork

Policy exists but does not bind tools.

This becomes theater.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit capability boundaries
2. least privilege as default
3. expiration for exceptions
4. symmetric enforcement (no immunity)

If permissions cannot be constrained, power becomes unbounded.
