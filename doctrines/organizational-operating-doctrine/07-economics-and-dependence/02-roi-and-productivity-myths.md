# ROI and Productivity Myths

Organizations become delusional when they treat productivity as obvious.

Productivity is not obvious.

Productivity claims are often:

- narrative
- status signaling
- and procurement justification

This module treats ROI as a contested truth surface.

---

## ROI is not immediate output

ROI is not:

- number of tickets closed
- number of documents produced
- or number of features shipped

Those are output metrics.

Output metrics are easy to game.

ROI is the long-term effect on:

- correction capacity
- dependence
- and survivability under change

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is productivity theater.

Productivity theater occurs when:

- short-term output increases
- long-term repair collapses
- and dependence increases silently

This produces future coercion.

---

## Failure modes

### Headcount substitution fantasy

Organizations believe work disappears.

Work does not disappear.

It shifts.

### “Free capability” illusion

Capability is treated as cheap.

Correction capacity is ignored.

### Measurement capture

Metrics are selected to justify decisions.

Truth becomes narrative.

### Debt blindness

Repair debt grows.

Future cost explodes.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. ROI claims tied to evidence and time horizons
2. measurement of repair capacity, not only output
3. explicit accounting of dependence and lock-in
4. post-decision evaluation of ROI claims

If ROI is treated as obvious, procurement becomes ideology.
