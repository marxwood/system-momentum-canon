# Labor Redesign

Organizations do not adopt new capability without labor redesign.

If labor redesign is ignored, the organization will:

- displace responsibility
- increase resentment
- and create internal hostility

This module treats labor redesign as legitimacy engineering.

---

## Labor redesign is not replacement

Labor redesign is not “removing people”.

It is the re-binding of:

- intent
- execution
- and repair ownership

If repair ownership is removed, correction collapses.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is legitimacy collapse.

Legitimacy collapses when:

- people feel disposable
- responsibility is unclear
- and status is threatened

Legitimacy collapse produces sabotage equilibria.

---

## Failure modes

### Shadow resistance

People comply outwardly.

They sabotage silently.

### Responsibility vacuum

Work is delegated without ownership.

Failures repeat.

### Status humiliation

Roles are degraded.

Truth becomes dangerous.

### Two-class organization

Some people become “operators”.

Others become “subjects”.

This becomes internal authoritarianism.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit redesign of ownership and repair roles
2. dignity preservation during role shifts
3. retraining tied to real responsibility
4. legitimacy interfaces for dissent

If labor redesign is ignored, adoption becomes coercion.
