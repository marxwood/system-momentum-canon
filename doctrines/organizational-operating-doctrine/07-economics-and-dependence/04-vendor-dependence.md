# Vendor Dependence

Vendor dependence is not a procurement issue.

It is a power relationship.

Organizations become capturable when:

- they cannot exit
- they cannot audit
- or they cannot negotiate

Dependence is the mechanism.

Capture is the outcome.

---

## Dependence is not always bad

Dependence can be rational.

The hazard is dependence without exit.

Exit is what makes dependence non-coercive.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is silent lock-in.

Silent lock-in occurs when:

- integration becomes irreversible
- data becomes trapped
- and internal capability is not maintained

When lock-in becomes visible, it is usually too late.

---

## Failure modes

### Data captivity

Data cannot be extracted.

Audit becomes impossible.

### Contractual immunity

Vendors cannot be held accountable.

This produces asymmetric power.

### Capability erosion

Internal skill disappears.

Exit becomes fantasy.

### Vendor-shaped governance

Vendor constraints become organizational law.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. dependence maps for critical vendors
2. exit paths for critical dependencies
3. auditability requirements in procurement
4. redundancy for critical capabilities

If exit is absent, dependence becomes coercion.
