# Moats and Commoditization

Organizations become delusional when they assume advantage is permanent.

Advantage is not permanent.

Capabilities spread.

What remains is:

- coordination
- trust
- and correction capacity

This module treats competitive advantage as a coordination outcome.

---

## Moats are not features

Features are copied.

Tools are copied.

Execution speed is copied.

What is harder to copy is:

- repair discipline
- truth handling
- and survivable contradiction

These are slow.

These are cultural and structural.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is false moat confidence.

False moat confidence occurs when:

- advantage is treated as inherent
- dependence is ignored
- and correction capacity is underfunded

This produces sudden collapse.

---

## Failure modes

### Feature pride

Organizations believe shipping is the moat.

Competitors catch up.

### Tool worship

Organizations believe tools are the moat.

Tools commoditize.

### Reputation as immunity

Reputation is treated as protection.

Reality eventually overrides it.

### Coordination neglect

The real moat decays.

The organization becomes fragile.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit identification of what is commoditizable
2. investment in correction capacity as advantage
3. dependence management as strategy
4. repair discipline as long-term survivability

If the organization cannot correct, it cannot adapt.
