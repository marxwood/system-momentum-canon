# Cost Models

Organizations become irrational when they cannot see cost.

Invisible cost produces:

- fantasy planning
- blame diffusion
- and dependency denial

This module treats cost models as a truth surface.

---

## Cost is not money only

Cost includes:

- compute and tooling
- human time
- opportunity cost
- incident cost
- repair cost
- and coordination cost

Organizations that measure only money will optimize for theater.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is cost laundering.

Cost laundering occurs when:

- costs are moved across budgets
- costs are moved across teams
- or costs are delayed into the future

Delayed cost becomes coercion later.

Because repair becomes unaffordable.

---

## Failure modes

### Tool cost invisibility

Tools are adopted without lifecycle cost.

Dependence becomes permanent.

### Incident cost denial

Incidents are treated as bad luck.

Repair is underfunded.

### Coordination cost denial

Meetings expand.

Work slows.

People burn out.

### Externalized cost

Costs are pushed onto customers or workers.

Trust collapses.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. cost models that include repair and incident cost
2. visibility of dependency lifecycle costs
3. budgeting for correction capacity
4. cost ownership bindings (who pays for what)

If cost cannot be seen, the organization will be captured by its own dependencies.
