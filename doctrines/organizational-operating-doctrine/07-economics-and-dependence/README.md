# Economics and Dependence

Organizations do not collapse because they lack ideas.

They collapse because they become dependent.

Dependence is power.

Power without correction becomes capture.

This section treats economics as constraint substrate.

---

## Economics is not finance

Economics is not budgeting alone.

Economics is the structure of:

- dependence
- incentives
- and exit paths

If an organization cannot exit dependencies, it cannot negotiate.

If it cannot negotiate, it will eventually be coerced.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is hidden dependence.

Hidden dependence occurs when:

- costs are externalized
- lock-in is ignored
- and procurement is treated as administrative

Hidden dependence becomes sudden coercion later.

---

## What this section covers

This section defines:

- cost models and true accounting
- ROI myths and second-order effects
- labor redesign and resentment management
- vendor dependence and lock-in
- moats and commoditization
- security as economics

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. dependence maps (internal and external)
2. exit paths for critical dependencies
3. incentives that do not punish repair
4. security treated as economic survival, not compliance

If economics is ignored, governance becomes fantasy.
