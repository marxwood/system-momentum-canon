# Scale Translation

Organizations fail when mechanisms do not translate across scale.

A mechanism that works in a team may fail in a company.

A mechanism that works in a company may fail in a network of companies.

This section treats translation as discipline.

---

## Translation is not copying

Translation is not replicating structures.

Translation is preserving invariants under new constraints.

The invariant here is:

correction capacity under power.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is scale illusion.

Scale illusion occurs when:

- mechanisms are copied without adaptation
- and failure is blamed on people
- rather than on broken translation

Scale illusion produces bureaucracy or chaos.

---

## What this section covers

This section defines:

- team-to-org translation
- org-to-ecosystem translation
- cross-cultural and cross-site translation
- scaling without ideology
- governance of doctrine change

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit translation work when scaling
2. evaluation of mechanisms under new constraints
3. avoidance of universalist assumptions
4. governance of doctrine evolution without drift

If translation is ignored, scaling becomes accidental capture.
