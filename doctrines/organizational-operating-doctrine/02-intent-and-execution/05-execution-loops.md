# Execution Loops

Organizations do not execute through single actions.

They execute through loops.

A loop is the minimal unit of corrigible work.

It binds:

- action
- evidence
- evaluation
- and repair

Without loops, organizations produce output without correction.

Output without correction becomes drift.

---

## Loops are not bureaucracy

Loops are not process for its own sake.

Loops exist because reality is not fully knowable at planning time.

Correction must be continuous.

A loop is the mechanism that makes continuous correction possible.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is loop collapse.

Loop collapse occurs when:

- evaluation is skipped
- evidence is not collected
- or repair is deferred indefinitely

Loop collapse produces:

- silent failure accumulation
- credibility theater
- and eventual crisis

---

## Failure modes

### Output-only loops

Work is delivered without evaluation.

Failures appear later as incidents.

### Evidence-free loops

Work is evaluated through narrative.

Narrative becomes legitimacy.

### Repair-free loops

Failures are known but not repaired.

Debt accumulates.

Debt becomes coercion.

### Loop ownership collapse

No one owns the loop end-to-end.

This becomes blame diffusion.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit loops for high-impact work
2. evidence collection as part of execution
3. evaluation gates before completion
4. repair triggers when evaluation fails

If loops collapse, the organization becomes a production engine without correction.
