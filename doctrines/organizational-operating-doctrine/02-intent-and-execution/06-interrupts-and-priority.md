# Interrupts and Priority

Interrupts are normal.

Organizations fail when interrupts are treated as exceptions.

When interrupts are treated as exceptions:

- priority becomes informal
- constraints are erased under urgency
- and execution becomes capture-prone

This module defines interrupt handling as an interface.

---

## Interrupts are a legitimacy problem

Interrupts are not only scheduling.

Interrupts determine:

- what the organization treats as legitimate work
- what it treats as ignorable
- and who gets to override pipelines

If interrupts are unbounded, override becomes power.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is urgency exemptions.

Urgency exemptions occur when:

- evaluation gates are skipped
- permissions are expanded
- and accountability is deferred

Urgency exemptions become permanent.

Permanent urgency is capture.

---

## Failure modes

### Priority by status

Work is prioritized by seniority.

This produces factional capture.

### Incident theater

Incidents are used to justify unbounded action.

### Starvation of repair

Repair work is perpetually deprioritized.

Debt accumulates.

Debt becomes coercion.

### Interrupt exhaustion

Everything is urgent.

People stop distinguishing risk.

This produces drift.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit interrupt channels
2. bounded override authority
3. auditability of priority changes
4. protection of repair capacity under urgency

If interrupts cannot be bounded, urgency becomes the default legitimacy engine.
