# Planning vs Acting

Organizations collapse when planning and acting are mixed.

When planning and acting are mixed:

- execution becomes unbounded
- evaluation becomes impossible
- and accountability becomes symbolic

This module separates planning mode from acting mode.

---

## Planning mode

Planning mode exists to produce:

- bounded tasks
- evidence requirements
- evaluation gates
- consequence mapping
- and escalation triggers

Planning mode is not about predicting the future.

It is about making action corrigible.

---

## Acting mode

Acting mode exists to:

- execute bounded tasks
- produce evidence
- and trigger repair when needed

Acting mode is not the place to:

- redefine intent
- erase constraints
- or improvise legitimacy

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is urgency erasing planning.

Urgency is real.

Urgency is also the easiest justification for capture.

Organizations under urgency tend to:

- bypass intent declaration
- bypass evaluation gates
- and bypass accountability

This produces fast delusion.

---

## Failure modes

### Continuous planning

The organization never acts.

This produces paralysis.

### Continuous acting

The organization never plans.

This produces drift and incidents.

### Planning theater

Planning exists as documents.

Execution ignores them.

### Acting as improvisation

Tasks expand mid-execution.

Scope becomes unbounded.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit transition from planning to acting
2. bounded tasks before acting
3. evaluation gates defined before acting
4. escalation triggers when acting reveals new scope

If planning and acting cannot be separated, execution becomes unbounded.
