# Intent Declaration

Intent is the precondition to legitimate action.

Organizations often act without declared intent.

They call it “moving fast”.

Undeclared intent is not speed.

It is unbounded power.

---

## Why intent must be declared

Intent must be declared because it makes action:

- inspectable
- contestable
- and accountable

Without intent declaration:

- evaluation cannot occur
- consequence mapping is impossible
- and repair cannot be owned

---

## Intent is not justification

Intent is not a story.

Intent is a binding.

It binds:

- a decision
  to
- an action surface
  and
- a repair obligation

Declared intent does not make an action correct.

It makes the action corrigible.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is intent substitution.

Intent is substituted by:

- authority
- urgency
- and narrative

This produces legitimacy theater.

Theater stabilizes.

Theater destroys correction.

---

## Failure modes

### Retroactive intent

Intent is written after execution.

This is audit theater.

### Vague intent

Intent is declared but unbounded.

Unbounded intent is permission for anything.

### Intent monopoly

Only leaders may declare intent.

This produces bottlenecks and shadow work.

### Intent as loyalty ritual

Intent is used to enforce conformity.

Contradiction becomes disloyalty.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. intent declaration before high-impact action
2. bounded scope inside intent
3. explicit ownership of consequences and repair
4. contestability of intent claims

If intent cannot be declared, action becomes power.
