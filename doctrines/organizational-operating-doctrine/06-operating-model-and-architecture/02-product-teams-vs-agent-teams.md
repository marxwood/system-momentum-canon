# Domain Teams vs Execution Teams

Organizations often confuse:

- domain ownership
  with
- execution capability

This confusion produces either:

- chaos (everyone can do everything)
  or
- paralysis (only one group can do anything)

This module separates domain teams from execution teams.

---

## Domain teams

Domain teams own:

- intent in a domain
- consequence mapping
- and repair obligations

Domain teams are accountable for outcomes.

They are not defined by tools.

They are defined by responsibility.

---

## Execution teams

Execution teams own:

- execution infrastructure
- integration surfaces
- and shared capability constraints

Execution teams are accountable for:

- safe execution
- auditability
- and reversibility

They are not owners of domain intent.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is capability capture.

Capability capture occurs when:

- execution teams become intent owners
- or domain teams gain unbounded execution capability

Both outcomes destroy correction.

---

## Failure modes

### Platform domination

Execution teams dictate what is possible.

Domain intent becomes irrelevant.

### Domain anarchy

Domain teams bypass constraints.

Incidents become normal.

### Tool ownership confusion

Tools define power.

This becomes vendor-shaped authority.

### Responsibility laundering

Failures are blamed on “the platform” or “the business”.

No one repairs.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. domain intent ownership separated from execution capability
2. explicit interfaces between domain teams and execution teams
3. repair obligations assigned to domain owners
4. constraints enforced by execution owners

If capability and intent merge, power becomes unbounded.
