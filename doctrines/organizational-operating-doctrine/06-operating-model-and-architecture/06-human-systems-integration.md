# Human + Execution Integration

Organizations fail when they treat humans and execution units as separate worlds.

They are one system.

Work must flow across them without:

- truth loss
- accountability loss
- or repair loss

This module defines integration as a coordination interface.

---

## Integration is not adoption

Integration is not “getting people to use the tool”.

Integration is the preservation of:

- intent
- evidence
- evaluation
- and repair

across human and execution boundaries.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is handoff collapse.

Handoff collapse occurs when:

- humans delegate without contracts
- execution produces outputs without provenance
- or repair is not routed back to owners

Handoff collapse produces:

- blame diffusion
- and delegated delusion

---

## Failure modes

### Delegation without repair

Humans delegate and disappear.

No one repairs.

### Output without provenance

Outputs cannot be inspected.

Truth becomes narrative.

### Handoff overload

Humans receive too many approvals.

Approval becomes ritual.

### Override as humiliation

Humans override publicly.

Contradiction collapses.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. delegation contracts for high-impact work
2. provenance preservation across handoffs
3. explicit repair routing
4. override paths that do not humiliate

If integration collapses, humans become symbolic owners and execution becomes unbounded power.
