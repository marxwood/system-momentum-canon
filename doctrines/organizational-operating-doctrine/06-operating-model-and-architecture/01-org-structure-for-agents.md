# Organization Structure for Execution Units

Organizations fail when structure is optimized for hierarchy.

Hierarchy optimizes for control.

Correction requires:

- bounded delegation
- distributed ownership
- and survivable contradiction

Structure must serve these properties.

---

## Structure is not a chart

An org chart is not structure.

Structure is the routing of:

- decisions
- permissions
- and repair obligations

A flat org with centralized permissions is not flat.

A hierarchical org with distributed repair can be correction-capable.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is bottleneck structure.

Bottleneck structure occurs when:

- one group owns approvals
- one group owns permissions
- or one group owns repair

Bottlenecks produce:

- shadow work
- bypass behavior
- and eventual capture

---

## Failure modes

### Approval monopolies

Everything requires one leader.

Work routes through status.

### Permission monopolies

Access is centralized.

Exceptions become political.

### Repair monopolies

Only one team fixes systemic issues.

This becomes a choke point.

### Interface collapse

Teams cannot negotiate boundaries.

Conflict becomes factional.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. distributed ownership of repair
2. bounded delegation surfaces per domain
3. escalation paths that do not require personal loyalty
4. redundancy in critical coordination functions

If structure creates bottlenecks, the organization will route around them.

Routing around structure creates shadow systems.

Shadow systems create capture.
