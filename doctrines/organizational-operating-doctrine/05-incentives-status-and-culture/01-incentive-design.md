# Incentive Design

Organizations do not get what they intend.

They get what they reward.

Incentives are not a management tool.

They are the organization’s behavioral physics.

---

## Incentives are not only money

Incentives include:

- promotions
- attention
- prestige
- protection
- and immunity

In many organizations, immunity is the strongest incentive.

Immunity is capture.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is incentive inversion.

Incentive inversion occurs when:

- truth is punished
- repair is invisible
- and performance is rewarded

When this happens, the organization becomes a theater engine.

---

## Failure modes

### Goodhart pressure

Metrics become targets.

Gaming becomes rational.

### Output reward

Shipping is rewarded.

Repair is ignored.

### Loyalty reward

Agreement is rewarded.

Contradiction is punished.

### Immunity reward

Power protects itself.

This becomes stable capture.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. incentives that reward repair, not only output
2. incentives that reward contradiction, not only agreement
3. explicit penalties for immunity
4. metric design that anticipates gaming

If incentives reward theater, the organization will become theatrical.
