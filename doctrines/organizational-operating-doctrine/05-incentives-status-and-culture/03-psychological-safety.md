# Psychological Safety

Psychological safety is not comfort.

It is contradiction survivability.

An organization is not correction-capable unless people can:

- report evidence
- contradict authority
- and escalate risk
  without being punished.

---

## Safety is an operational requirement

Safety is not a moral virtue.

Safety is required because:

- contradiction is embarrassing
- error is inevitable
- and power resists correction

Without safety, people will choose silence.

Silence produces delusion.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is fear equilibrium.

Fear equilibrium occurs when:

- people expect humiliation
- or retaliation
- or career harm

Fear equilibrium is stable.

It produces long-term collapse.

---

## Failure modes

### Humiliation response

Contradiction triggers ridicule.

People stop speaking.

### Retaliation response

Contradiction triggers punishment.

People stop escalating.

### Career harm response

Truth becomes personally expensive.

People become strategic liars.

### Safety theater

The organization claims safety.

Reality shows silence.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. survivable contradiction
2. survivable escalation
3. protection for evidence reporting
4. consequences for retaliation

If safety collapses, truth becomes a personal risk.
