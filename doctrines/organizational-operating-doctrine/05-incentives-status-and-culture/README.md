# Incentives, Status, and Culture

Culture is not values on a wall.

Culture is incentive physics.

It is the system that determines:

- what behavior is rewarded
- what behavior is punished
- and what behavior is survivable

Organizations become capturable when culture becomes:

- status theater
- factional loyalty
- and contradiction suppression

This section treats culture as a stabilizer layer.

---

## Incentives are stronger than intent

Organizations often believe:

“If we have the right intent, we will behave well.”

This is false.

Incentives overpower intent.

Incentives shape:

- attention
- risk tolerance
- and truth handling

---

## Status is a control surface

Status determines who can speak.

Who can contradict.

Who can escalate.

Status is not a social detail.

Status is a power interface.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is credibility theater.

Credibility theater occurs when:

- appearance substitutes for evidence
- performance substitutes for repair
- and loyalty substitutes for truth

Credibility theater is how organizations lose correction capacity without noticing.

---

## What this section covers

This section defines:

- incentive design and Goodhart pressure
- status games and credibility theater
- psychological safety as contradiction survivability
- learning culture as repair discipline
- blame vs repair
- non-tribal culture as anti-capture engineering

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. incentives that reward contradiction and repair
2. status systems that do not punish truth
3. learning loops that survive embarrassment
4. accountability without scapegoating

If culture punishes contradiction, capture becomes stable.
