# Status Games and Credibility Theater

Organizations do not run on truth.

They run on credibility.

Credibility is not evidence.

Credibility is a social currency.

When credibility replaces evidence, organizations become capturable.

---

## Status is not a soft topic

Status determines:

- who gets believed
- who gets ignored
- and who gets punished for contradiction

Status is the organization’s internal legitimacy layer.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is credibility theater.

Credibility theater occurs when:

- confident speech is treated as competence
- narrative is treated as evidence
- and visibility is treated as value

Credibility theater produces:

- fake certainty
- suppressed contradiction
- and eventual collapse

---

## Failure modes

### Authority cosplay

People perform certainty.

Reality contact is lost.

### Meeting domination

Speaking time becomes power.

Silence becomes survival.

### Narrative capture

The story becomes the system.

Evidence becomes optional.

### Visibility monopoly

Work that is visible wins.

Repair work disappears.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. evidence over narrative in high-impact decisions
2. contradiction as a survivable act
3. protection for repair work
4. auditability of claims that drive action

If credibility becomes the operating system, truth becomes optional.
