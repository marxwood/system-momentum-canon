# Blame and Repair

Organizations always assign blame.

The question is whether blame produces repair.

Blame without repair is scapegoating.

Repair without accountability is drift.

This module treats blame as a dangerous force that must be bounded.

---

## Blame is an energy

Blame is a human energy.

Organizations often deny it.

Denied blame becomes:

- gossip
- factional war
- and covert sabotage

The goal is not to eliminate blame.

The goal is to route blame into repair.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is scapegoat equilibrium.

Scapegoat equilibrium occurs when:

- one person is sacrificed
- and the system is unchanged

This produces:

- fear
- silence
- and repeated failure

---

## Failure modes

### Public sacrifice

One person is punished.

The organization feels purified.

Nothing improves.

### Immunity zones

Power cannot be blamed.

This is capture.

### Blame diffusion

Everyone is blamed.

No one repairs.

### Repair without responsibility

Repair happens, but no one is accountable.

The same mistake returns.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. accountability that triggers repair
2. bounded consequences that do not humiliate
3. symmetric enforcement (no immunity)
4. repair ownership bindings for systemic failures

If blame becomes ritual, the organization will trade truth for fear.
