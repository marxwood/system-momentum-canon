# Internal Hostility

Organizations often deny internal hostility.

Internal hostility is not rare.

It is the predictable outcome of:

- scarcity
- status competition
- and factional capture

This module treats internal hostility as a stability risk.

---

## Hostility is not always explicit

Hostility can be:

- sabotage
- withholding information
- blocking repair
- weaponizing HR processes
- or weaponizing governance selectively

Organizations that deny this become naïve.

Naïveté is exploitable.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is factional sabotage equilibrium.

Sabotage equilibrium occurs when:

- trust collapses
- and retaliation becomes normal
- and truth becomes factional

At that point, governance becomes a weapon.

---

## Failure modes

### Withholding evidence

People hide information to win.

Truth collapses.

### Process weaponization

Rules are applied selectively.

This is capture.

### Reputation attacks

Credibility is destroyed to remove rivals.

This produces fear.

### Silent sabotage

Work is slowed or broken covertly.

Repair becomes impossible.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. symmetric governance enforcement (no selective punishment)
2. auditability of high-impact decisions
3. protection for contradiction and evidence reporting
4. escalation paths outside faction control

If internal hostility becomes normal, the organization becomes ungovernable.
