# Adversarial Reality

Organizations often treat adversarial behavior as exceptional.

It is not exceptional.

It is a normal property of environments with:

- money
- power
- and dependence

This section treats adversarial reality as baseline.

---

## Adversarial reality is not paranoia

Adversarial reality is not fear.

It is consequence realism.

If an organization assumes benevolence, it becomes:

- exploitable
- and capturable

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is hostile surprise.

Hostile surprise occurs when:

- trust assumptions are implicit
- threat models are absent
- and governance is selective

Hostile surprise produces catastrophic failure.

---

## What this section covers

This section defines:

- misuse and rogue execution scenarios
- data leakage and retention failures
- instruction hijacking and interface attacks
- supply chain compromise
- internal hostility and sabotage equilibria
- resilience under pressure

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. threat modeling as normal work
2. constraints that assume misuse
3. auditability of high-impact actions
4. resilience planning for pressure events

If adversarial reality is denied, the organization will learn through crisis.
