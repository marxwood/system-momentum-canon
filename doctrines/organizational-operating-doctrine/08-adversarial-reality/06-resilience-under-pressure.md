# Resilience Under Pressure

Organizations are tested under pressure.

Pressure is not exceptional.

Pressure is the normal environment of:

- competition
- incidents
- and constraint shocks

This module treats resilience as correction survival.

---

## Resilience is not toughness

Resilience is not morale slogans.

Resilience is the ability to:

- keep truth inspectable
- keep escalation survivable
- and keep repair possible

under pressure.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is pressure collapse.

Pressure collapse occurs when:

- urgency becomes legitimacy
- constraints are bypassed
- and accountability is suspended

Suspended accountability becomes permanent.

Permanent suspension is capture.

---

## Failure modes

### Permanent urgency

Everything becomes incident mode.

Evaluation collapses.

### Constraint abandonment

Policies exist but are ignored.

This produces unbounded capability.

### Scapegoat stabilization

Pressure is stabilized through blame.

Learning collapses.

### Silence equilibrium

People stop escalating.

The organization becomes blind.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. incident-mode constraints (what still cannot be done)
2. auditability of override decisions
3. repair capacity protected under pressure
4. escalation paths that survive fear

If pressure erases constraints, pressure becomes a capture engine.
