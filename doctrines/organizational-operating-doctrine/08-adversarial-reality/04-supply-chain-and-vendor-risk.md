# Supply Chain and Vendor Risk

Organizations are rarely compromised directly.

They are compromised through dependencies.

Dependencies are not neutral.

Dependencies are attack surfaces.

This module treats supply chain risk as default.

---

## Supply chain risk is dependence risk

Supply chain risk is not a security specialty.

It is the same mechanism as:

- vendor lock-in
- data captivity
- and capability erosion

Dependence becomes coercion.

Coercion becomes capture.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is blind trust in dependencies.

Blind trust occurs when:

- vendors are assumed safe
- integrations are assumed benign
- and auditability is absent

---

## Failure modes

### Dependency sprawl

Too many dependencies exist.

No one can audit them.

### Hidden transitive dependencies

Risk enters indirectly.

It is discovered only after harm.

### Vendor immunity

Vendors are not accountable.

This becomes asymmetric risk.

### Patch paralysis

Updates are too risky to apply.

Fragility increases.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. dependence maps for critical systems
2. auditability requirements for vendors
3. redundancy for critical dependencies
4. incident response plans for vendor compromise

If dependencies cannot be audited, the organization becomes capturable through procurement.
