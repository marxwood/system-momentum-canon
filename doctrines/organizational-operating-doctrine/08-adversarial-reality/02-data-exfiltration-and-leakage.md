# Data Exfiltration and Leakage

Organizations often treat leakage as an accident.

Leakage is often the primary objective.

Data is power.

Data without boundary is capture fuel.

---

## Leakage is not only theft

Leakage includes:

- accidental exposure
- negligent sharing
- internal misuse
- and coerced disclosure

Organizations that treat leakage as only “hackers” are blind.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is silent leakage.

Silent leakage occurs when:

- access is broad
- logging is weak
- and retention is uncontrolled

Silent leakage is discovered only after harm.

---

## Failure modes

### Overbroad access

Everyone can access everything.

This is not openness.

This is fragility.

### Unlogged access

Access occurs without audit trails.

Accountability collapses.

### Retention sprawl

Sensitive data persists indefinitely.

Risk accumulates.

### Vendor leakage

Data is exposed through dependencies.

This becomes supply-chain coercion.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. least privilege for sensitive data
2. auditability of access
3. retention discipline
4. explicit boundaries for external sharing

If leakage cannot be bounded, the organization becomes capturable through its own memory.
