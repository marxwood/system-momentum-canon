# Stabilizers and Meaning

Organizations require stabilizers.

Without stabilizers, coordination becomes:

- fear-based
- or faction-based

Both destroy correction.

This module treats meaning systems as stabilizer engineering.

---

## Meaning is not propaganda

Meaning is not enemy-making.

Meaning is the internal answer to:

- why we cooperate
- why repair matters
- and why truth is survivable

Organizations that cannot provide meaning tend to substitute:

- urgency
- loyalty
- or ideology

All three are capture vectors.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is manufactured meaning.

Manufactured meaning occurs when:

- narratives are imposed
- contradictions are suppressed
- and identity becomes mandatory

Manufactured meaning is internal propaganda.

---

## Failure modes

### Mission as immunity

The mission excuses harm.

This becomes moral licensing.

### Enemy-based meaning

Teams require enemies to bond.

This becomes internal war.

### Identity enforcement

Belonging requires ideological alignment.

This destroys contradiction.

### Cynicism equilibrium

Meaning collapses.

People become strategic.

Truth becomes optional.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. meaning that does not require enemies
2. dignity preservation as stabilizer
3. contradiction survivability inside meaning systems
4. separation between meaning and immunity claims

If meaning becomes propaganda, the organization becomes capturable from the inside.
