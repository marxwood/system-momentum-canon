# Media and Reputation

Reputation is not branding.

Reputation is external credibility.

Credibility is a power interface.

Organizations become capturable when they treat reputation as:

- PR performance
  rather than
- reality contact

---

## Reputation is fragile under contradiction

External audiences rarely have:

- full evidence
- provenance
- or context

They rely on:

- narratives
- symbols
- and trust signals

This creates a vulnerability.

Narratives can be weaponized.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is reputation capture.

Reputation capture occurs when:

- external narratives define internal reality
- and internal truth becomes subordinated to appearance

At that point, the organization becomes theatrical.

---

## Failure modes

### Crisis denial

Incidents are hidden.

Later exposure becomes catastrophic.

### Narrative substitution

PR replaces repair.

Reality eventually returns.

### External intimidation

The organization is manipulated through reputational threats.

This becomes coercion.

### Internal propaganda

Leadership uses external narratives to silence dissent.

This is internal capture.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. repair-first response to external narratives
2. provenance preservation for public claims
3. contradiction survivability under reputational pressure
4. separation between PR and truth handling

If reputation becomes the operating system, the organization becomes capturable through attention.
