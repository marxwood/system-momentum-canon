# Regulation and Compliance

Regulation is not morality.

Regulation is an external constraint layer.

Organizations fail when they treat regulation as:

- paperwork
- or hostility

Regulation is a power interface.

---

## Compliance is not safety

Compliance does not guarantee safety.

Compliance guarantees:

- auditability
- and accountability under external authority

A correction-capable organization treats compliance as:

- a boundary condition
- not a virtue

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is compliance theater.

Compliance theater occurs when:

- documents exist
- reality does not change
- and audits are gamed

Compliance theater produces catastrophic risk.

Because regulators respond with coercion.

---

## Failure modes

### Paper compliance

Policies exist, enforcement does not.

### Selective compliance

Some rules apply.

Others are bypassed.

This becomes capture.

### Compliance as weapon

Compliance is used internally to punish rivals.

This produces factional sabotage.

### Compliance denial

The organization ignores regulation.

External coercion follows.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. auditability of high-impact actions
2. symmetric enforcement of policy
3. evidence preservation for external scrutiny
4. compliance work tied to real constraints, not paperwork

If compliance becomes theater, external power will impose constraints violently.
