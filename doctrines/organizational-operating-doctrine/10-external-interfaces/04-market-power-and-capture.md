# Market Power and Capture

Organizations are not only economic actors.

They are power actors.

Market power creates:

- leverage
- dependency
- and coercion capacity

This module treats market dynamics as capture dynamics.

---

## Market power is not success

Market power is not moral.

Market power is the ability to:

- constrain others’ options
- and shape rules

Organizations that deny this become naïve.

Organizations that celebrate it become predatory.

Both outcomes increase capture risk.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is external capture.

External capture occurs when:

- the organization becomes dependent on one market
- one platform
- one regulator
- or one political faction

Dependence becomes coercion.

---

## Failure modes

### Platform dependence

A platform can destroy the business.

This is coercion risk.

### Monopsony labor power

The organization can punish workers.

This destroys legitimacy.

### Regulatory capture temptation

The organization shapes rules to block rivals.

This produces civilizational hostility.

### Customer coercion

The organization exploits lock-in.

Trust collapses.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. dependence maps for critical markets and platforms
2. exit paths where possible
3. internal constraints against predatory capture behavior
4. auditability of market-power decisions

If market power is unmanaged, the organization becomes a capture engine.
