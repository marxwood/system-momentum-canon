# Labor and Social Contract

Organizations do not survive by capability alone.

They survive by legitimacy.

Legitimacy includes the internal social contract:

- why people cooperate
- what they believe they are owed
- and what they believe is fair

This module treats labor legitimacy as a stability constraint.

---

## The social contract is not HR

The social contract is not benefits.

It is the perceived structure of:

- dignity
- security
- and reciprocity

When the social contract collapses, organizations become hostile internally.

Internal hostility produces sabotage equilibria.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is legitimacy rupture.

Legitimacy rupture occurs when:

- labor is treated as disposable
- promises are broken silently
- and accountability is asymmetric

Asymmetry produces resentment.

Resentment produces covert resistance.

---

## Failure modes

### Disposable labor reality

People stop caring.

Quality collapses.

### Two-class system

Some are protected.

Others are expendable.

This becomes internal authoritarianism.

### Trust collapse

Promises are not believed.

Coordination becomes expensive.

### Fear-based compliance

People comply to survive.

Truth reporting collapses.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. dignity preservation under change
2. symmetric accountability (no immunity)
3. credible repair of broken promises
4. survivable dissent inside labor relations

If the social contract collapses, the organization will stabilize through fear.

Fear destroys correction.
