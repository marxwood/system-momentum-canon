# Instability and Hostility

Organizations often assume stability.

Stability is not guaranteed.

Instability can come from:

- economic shocks
- political shifts
- supply chain disruption
- or legitimacy collapse

This module treats instability as a normal condition.

---

## Hostility is not personal

Hostility is structural.

It emerges when:

- dependence exists
- and incentives diverge
- and power is asymmetric

Organizations that assume benevolence become fragile.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is brittle legitimacy.

Brittle legitimacy occurs when:

- the organization cannot explain itself under pressure
- and cannot preserve truth under threat
- and cannot maintain repair obligations

Under pressure, brittle systems collapse into coercion.

---

## Failure modes

### Panic governance

Urgency becomes legitimacy.

Constraints are bypassed.

### External scapegoating

External enemies are manufactured.

This becomes propaganda.

### Internal purges

Instability is stabilized through blame.

Learning collapses.

### Denial

Signals are ignored.

Correction becomes too late.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. crisis-mode constraints (what still cannot be done)
2. truth preservation under external pressure
3. repair capacity protected during shocks
4. dependence management as stability work

If instability erases constraints, the organization becomes a capture engine.
