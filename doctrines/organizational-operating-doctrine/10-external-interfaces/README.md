# External Interfaces

Organizations do not operate in isolation.

They are constrained by:

- markets
- regulators
- vendors
- media
- and social legitimacy

External interfaces determine:

- what the organization can do
- what it must explain
- and what it can survive

This section treats external reality as part of the operating system.

---

## External pressure is not optional

External pressure is not “distraction”.

External pressure shapes:

- incentives
- governance
- and truth handling

Organizations that deny external pressure become naïve.

Naïveté becomes coercion later.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is interface blindness.

Interface blindness occurs when:

- dependencies are ignored
- legitimacy is treated as PR only
- and regulation is treated as paperwork

Interface blindness produces sudden constraint shocks.

---

## What this section covers

This section defines:

- regulation and audit pressures
- media and reputation dynamics
- labor legitimacy and social contract
- market power and capture dynamics
- instability and hostile environments
- stabilizers and meaning systems inside organizations

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. dependence maps beyond the organization
2. auditability under external scrutiny
3. legitimacy interfaces that do not rely on propaganda
4. resilience under external shocks

If external reality is ignored, the organization will be corrected by force.
