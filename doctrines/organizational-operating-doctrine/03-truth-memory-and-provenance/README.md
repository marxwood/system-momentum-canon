# Truth, Memory, and Provenance

Organizations do not coordinate on truth.

They coordinate on what they treat as true.

This is not a philosophical statement.

It is an operational reality.

When organizations lose shared truth, they do not become neutral.

They become:

- factional
- theatrical
- and capturable

This section defines truth as coordination substrate.

---

## Truth is not belief

Truth is not what people feel.

Truth is what survives inspection.

At organizational scale, truth requires:

- evidence primitives
- provenance integrity
- contradiction capacity
- and belief update protocols

Without these, “truth” becomes authority.

Authority becomes immunity.

Immunity becomes capture.

---

## Institutional memory is correction capacity

Organizations often treat memory as documentation.

That is insufficient.

Memory is what prevents:

- repeated failure
- personnel-loss collapse
- and narrative rewriting

Without memory, accountability cannot persist.

Without accountability, capture becomes stable.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is truth fragmentation.

Truth fragments when:

- teams maintain incompatible realities
- metrics become unquestionable
- provenance is lost
- and contradiction becomes dangerous

Fragmented truth makes coordination impossible.

Coercion becomes the substitute.

---

## What this section covers

This section defines:

- source-of-truth boundaries
- provenance and audit trails
- institutional memory as continuity infrastructure
- knowledge models as structure, not ideology
- epistemic regimes inside organizations
- hallucination containment as correction interface

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. inspectable evidence primitives
2. provenance integrity for claims and decisions
3. survivable contradiction
4. institutional memory that survives turnover

If truth cannot be inspected, coordination collapses into theater.
