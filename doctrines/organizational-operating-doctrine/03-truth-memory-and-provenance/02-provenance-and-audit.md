# Provenance and Audit

Organizations do not lose truth because people lie.

They lose truth because provenance becomes expensive.

When provenance is expensive, narrative becomes cheap.

Narrative becomes the default.

---

## Provenance is not compliance

Provenance is not paperwork.

Provenance is the ability to answer:

- where a claim came from
- what evidence supports it
- what transformations occurred
- and who touched it

Without provenance, accountability cannot exist.

Without accountability, repair cannot exist.

---

## Audit is not punishment

Audit is not policing.

Audit is the mechanism that keeps truth inspectable.

A correction-capable organization treats audit as:

- normal
- survivable
- and non-humiliating

Audit that humiliates produces silence.

Silence produces delusion.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is audit theater.

Audit theater occurs when:

- audit exists for appearance
- audit is selectively applied
- or audit is used as a weapon

Selective audit produces capture.

Weaponized audit produces factional war.

---

## Failure modes

### No provenance

Claims cannot be inspected.

Truth becomes authority.

### Provenance overload

Everything is logged.

Nothing is inspected.

This becomes ritual.

### Selective audit

Some actors are audited.

Others are immune.

This is capture.

### Audit as humiliation

Audit becomes public shaming.

People stop reporting.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. provenance for high-impact claims and actions
2. survivable audit paths
3. symmetric audit (no immunity)
4. audit that triggers repair, not humiliation

If provenance collapses, narrative becomes the organization’s operating system.
