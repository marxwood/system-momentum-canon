# Knowledge Models

Organizations inevitably build models.

Even when they pretend they do not.

A model is any structure that defines:

- what entities exist
- what relations matter
- and what counts as a valid state

Models are not ideology.

Models are interfaces.

---

## Models are not truth

A model is not truth.

A model is a projection.

A projection is a tool for coordination.

Treating projections as truth produces:

- rigidity
- and eventual collapse

Because reality does not match models.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is model capture.

Model capture occurs when:

- a model becomes sacred
- or a model becomes owned by a faction
- or a model becomes a vendor interface disguised as neutrality

Captured models produce:

- lock-in
- blindness
- and immunity

---

## Failure modes

### Sacred schemas

Models cannot be questioned.

Contradiction becomes disloyalty.

### Vendor-shaped reality

Models mirror vendor products.

This becomes dependence as power.

### Local forks

Teams fork the model.

Truth fragments.

### Model as politics

Model changes become factional war.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. models as inspectable projections
2. contestability of models
3. provenance for model changes
4. separation between models and factional identity

If models cannot be questioned, they become ideology.
