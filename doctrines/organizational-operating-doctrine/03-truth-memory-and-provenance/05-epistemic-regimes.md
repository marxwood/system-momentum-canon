# Epistemic Regimes

Organizations contain multiple truth regimes.

They rarely admit it.

A truth regime is a pattern that determines:

- what counts as evidence
- who is allowed to contradict
- and how belief updates occur

Epistemic regimes are not culture slogans.

They are operating mechanics.

---

## Why regimes collide

Regimes collide because they produce incompatible realities.

Examples:

- “metrics decide”
- “leaders decide”
- “customers decide”
- “engineers decide”
- “compliance decides”

Each regime has a different:

- evidence primitive
- authority boundary
- and update protocol

When regimes collide, the organization becomes political.

Politics becomes the substitute for truth.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is regime capture.

Regime capture occurs when:

- one regime becomes immune to contradiction
- and the others become symbolic

This produces:

- delusion stability
- and correction collapse

---

## Failure modes

### Leader regime

Truth is decided by seniority.

Contradiction becomes disloyalty.

### Metric regime

Metrics become sacred.

Gaming becomes inevitable.

### Customer regime

Short-term demand overrides consequence mapping.

### Compliance regime

Process replaces reality contact.

### Faction regime

Truth becomes group loyalty.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit identification of regimes
2. contradiction capacity between regimes
3. belief update protocols that survive status pressure
4. auditability of regime decisions

If regimes are invisible, regime war becomes the default coordination method.
