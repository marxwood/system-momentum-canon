# Organization as Coordinated Agency

An organization is not a collection of people.

It is a coordination system that produces agency.

Agency appears when:

- intent is declared
- decisions are made
- actions are executed
- consequences occur
- and repair is performed

Organizations are machines that transform intent into action.

Whether they preserve correction depends on the interfaces they use.

---

## Agency is not virtue

Agency is not moral goodness.

Agency is the ability to cause outcomes.

Organizations can have strong agency while being:

- delusional
- captured
- and coercive

This branch is concerned with agency that remains corrigible.

---

## Organizations have internal actors

Inside an organization, “actors” are not only people.

Actors include:

- roles
- teams
- committees
- systems
- and delegated execution units

An actor is anything that can:

- initiate action
- change state
- and produce consequences

The organizational hazard is that actors gain capability without accountability.

---

## The organization’s core loop

At minimum, an organization is a loop:

1. intent declaration
2. task decomposition
3. execution
4. evidence collection
5. evaluation
6. repair

When this loop breaks, the organization becomes:

- theater
- or coercion
- or chaos

---

## Ownership is the interface between agency and reality

Ownership is how agency becomes accountable.

Without ownership:

- errors cannot be repaired
- responsibility becomes blame diffusion
- and capture becomes stable

Ownership is not hierarchy.

Ownership is the binding between action and repair.

---

## Capture as organizational default

Capture is the default failure mode of organizational agency.

Capture occurs when:

- power becomes immune
- accountability becomes symbolic
- incentives reward appearance over truth
- exit paths disappear
- contradiction becomes dangerous

Capture produces order.

It destroys correction.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. a legible intent-to-action pipeline
2. ownership bindings for actions and outcomes
3. evidence and provenance for decisions
4. repair mechanisms that do not become purge
5. constraints that survive urgency

If these do not exist, the organization’s agency becomes unbounded.
