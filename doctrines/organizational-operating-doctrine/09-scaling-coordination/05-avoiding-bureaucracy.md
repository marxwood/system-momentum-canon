# Avoiding Bureaucracy

Bureaucracy is a coordination technology.

It is also a capture technology.

Organizations often oscillate between:

- chaos
  and
- bureaucracy

Both destroy correction.

This module defines bureaucracy as a failure mode.

---

## Bureaucracy is process without repair

Bureaucracy is not “many rules”.

Bureaucracy is:

- rules that do not improve correction
- gates that exist for appearance
- and procedures that protect immunity

Bureaucracy grows when trust collapses.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is process capture.

Process capture occurs when:

- rules are used to block rivals
- approvals are used as domination
- and compliance replaces evidence

This produces slow coercion.

---

## Failure modes

### Approval bloat

Approvals multiply.

Work slows.

Shadow work emerges.

### Ritual reporting

Reports exist without inspection.

Truth becomes performance.

### Process weaponization

Rules are applied selectively.

This is capture.

### Safety collapse

People stop escalating.

They use process as protection.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. process tied to correction outcomes
2. sunset clauses for process
3. auditability of approval bottlenecks
4. protection against selective enforcement

If bureaucracy grows unchecked, it becomes a substitute for truth.
