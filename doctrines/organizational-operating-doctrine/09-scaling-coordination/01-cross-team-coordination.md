# Cross-Team Coordination

Organizations fail when teams coordinate through relationships.

Relationship-based coordination does not scale.

It produces:

- favoritism
- shadow routing
- and factional capture

This module treats cross-team coordination as interface design.

---

## Coordination requires explicit interfaces

Teams must be able to answer:

- what we own
- what we do not own
- what we promise
- and how we repair

Without this, handoffs become political.

Politics becomes the substitute for truth.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is handoff collapse.

Handoff collapse occurs when:

- dependencies are implicit
- ownership is unclear
- and escalation paths are social

Handoff collapse produces:

- blame diffusion
- and slow sabotage

---

## Failure modes

### Relationship routing

Work goes to friends.

This becomes factional power.

### Unowned interfaces

No one owns the boundary.

Incidents repeat.

### Escalation by status

Disputes are resolved by seniority.

This becomes capture.

### Coordination theater

Meetings substitute for interfaces.

Work slows.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit team interfaces
2. ownership of boundary repair
3. escalation paths outside personal loyalty
4. visibility into dependency chains

If coordination is social, power becomes the routing layer.
