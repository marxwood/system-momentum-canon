# Decision Records and Coherence

Organizations lose coherence when decisions are not preserved.

Unpreserved decisions become:

- rumor
- reinterpretation
- and power rewriting

This module treats decision records as anti-capture infrastructure.

---

## Decision records are not documentation

Decision records are not for memory alone.

They exist to preserve:

- intent
- evidence
- constraints
- and repair obligations

Without this, decisions cannot be contested.

Uncontestable decisions become authority.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is narrative overwrite.

Narrative overwrite occurs when:

- decisions are not recorded
- or records are symbolic
- or records are selectively missing

Selective memory is capture.

---

## Failure modes

### Invisible decisions

People do not know why things exist.

Coordination becomes political.

### Status rewriting

Power rewrites history.

This creates immunity.

### Record theater

Records exist but contain no evidence.

This becomes ritual.

### Fragmented records

Records exist in many places.

Truth fragments.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. decision records for high-impact choices
2. provenance and evidence bindings in records
3. repair obligations linked to decisions
4. accessibility of records across teams

If decisions cannot be inspected, the organization becomes capturable through narrative.
