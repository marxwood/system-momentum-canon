# Distributed Ownership

Organizations collapse when ownership centralizes.

Centralized ownership produces:

- bottlenecks
- shadow routing
- and immunity zones

Distributed ownership is not a management preference.

It is a correction requirement.

---

## Ownership must be redundant

Redundancy is not inefficiency.

Redundancy is what prevents:

- continuity monopoly
- and single-point capture

If only one actor can repair a system, that actor becomes power.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is ownership monopoly.

Ownership monopoly occurs when:

- one team owns all repair
- or one leader owns all decisions
- or one system owns all truth

Monopoly produces capture.

---

## Failure modes

### Single maintainer

One person owns a critical system.

This becomes fragility.

### Central approval

All work requires one approval.

Shadow work emerges.

### Central truth system

One tool becomes unquestionable.

This becomes sacred infrastructure.

### Delegation collapse

Ownership exists on paper only.

No one repairs.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. redundancy for critical ownership
2. explicit delegation surfaces
3. cross-training for continuity
4. repair ownership that survives turnover

If ownership cannot be distributed, the organization will stabilize through hierarchy.

Hierarchy without correction becomes capture.
