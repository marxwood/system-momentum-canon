# Operational Doctrine

Organizations do not fail at strategy.

They fail at day-to-day living.

Operational doctrine is how an organization lives with:

- constraints
- delegation
- and correction

This module binds the branch into daily practice.

---

## Daily practice is where capture happens

Capture rarely happens through coups.

Capture happens through:

- exceptions
- urgency
- social routing
- and selective enforcement

Daily practice determines whether constraints survive.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is doctrine-as-document.

Doctrine-as-document occurs when:

- doctrine is written
- and ignored in execution
- and used only as rhetoric

Rhetoric is not correction.

---

## Failure modes

### Exception normalization

Every day has a special case.

Constraints decay.

### Social routing

Work is routed through status.

Queues become symbolic.

### Audit avoidance

Provenance is skipped.

Narrative replaces evidence.

### Repair starvation

Repair work is perpetually delayed.

Debt accumulates.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. daily review of exceptions and bypasses
2. repair capacity protected from output pressure
3. survivable contradiction in routine meetings
4. periodic re-validation of constraints and permissions

If daily practice does not preserve correction, doctrine becomes decoration.
