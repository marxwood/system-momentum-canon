# Scaling Coordination

Organizations do not scale by adding people.

They scale by stabilizing interfaces.

Interfaces determine:

- how work is handed off
- how truth is preserved
- how repair is routed
- and how power is constrained

This section treats scale as coordination, not hierarchy.

---

## Scale is not bureaucracy

Bureaucracy is one way to scale.

It is also a way to destroy correction.

The goal is not “no process”.

The goal is:

process that preserves correction capacity.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is scale capture.

Scale capture occurs when:

- coordination becomes political
- ownership becomes ambiguous
- and evaluation becomes symbolic

At scale, capture becomes cheap.

---

## What this section covers

This section defines:

- cross-team coordination interfaces
- decision records as coherence infrastructure
- distributed ownership and redundancy
- scaling evaluation and feedback loops
- avoiding bureaucracy bloat
- operational doctrine for day-to-day living

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. explicit interfaces between teams
2. decision continuity across time
3. distributed ownership (no single points)
4. evaluation and repair that scale

If coordination cannot scale, hierarchy becomes the substitute.

Hierarchy without correction becomes capture.
