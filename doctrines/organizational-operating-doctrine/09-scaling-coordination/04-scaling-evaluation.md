# Scaling Evaluation

Organizations fail when evaluation does not scale.

If evaluation does not scale:

- truth fragments
- drift accumulates
- and incident frequency increases

This module treats evaluation as an organizational organ.

---

## Evaluation is not centralized QA

Evaluation is not one team.

Evaluation is a distributed capability that must exist across:

- domains
- teams
- and interfaces

Centralized evaluation becomes a bottleneck.

Bottlenecks produce bypass behavior.

---

## The organizational hazard

The hazard is evaluation collapse.

Evaluation collapses when:

- teams ship without gates
- quality becomes narrative
- and accountability is symbolic

When evaluation collapses, drift becomes normal.

---

## Failure modes

### Local quality regimes

Each team defines quality differently.

Truth becomes incompatible.

### Evaluation bottlenecks

One group becomes gatekeeper.

Shadow work emerges.

### Metric substitution

Evaluation becomes one number.

Gaming becomes rational.

### Gate bypass culture

Urgency overrides evaluation.

Drift accelerates.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable organization must preserve:

1. evaluation gates tied to risk classes
2. distributed evaluation ownership
3. auditability of bypass events
4. repair triggers when evaluation fails

If evaluation cannot scale, the organization will scale drift instead.
