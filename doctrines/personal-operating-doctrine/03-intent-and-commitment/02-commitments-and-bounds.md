# Commitments and Bounds

Commitment is how intent becomes real.

But commitment without bounds becomes self-capture.

This module treats commitments as bounded contracts.

---

## Commitments create dependence

Every commitment creates dependence:

- others depend on you
- you depend on yourself
- institutions depend on your behavior

Dependence is not evil.

Dependence is how coordination works.

But dependence without exit becomes coercion.

---

## Bounds make commitments survivable

Bounds define:

- what is included
- what is excluded
- what happens if conditions change
- and what refusal looks like

Without bounds, commitment becomes a trap.

Traps create shame.

Shame creates concealment.

Concealment destroys trust.

---

## Refusal is part of commitment

A commitment system without refusal protocols becomes violence.

Not physical violence.

Constraint violence.

The actor becomes forced to:

- overpromise
- lie
- or collapse

A refusal protocol preserves dignity.

Dignity preserves repair.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is unbounded obligation.

Unbounded obligation occurs when:

- commitments have no limits
- exit is shameful
- and refusal is treated as betrayal

Then the actor becomes trapped.

Trapped actors become resentful.

Resentment produces hidden retaliation.

---

## Failure modes

### Overcommitment

Saying yes as identity.

### Silent withdrawal

Avoiding instead of refusing.

### Boundary collapse

No distinction between help and surrender.

### Commitment theater

Promises are used to gain status.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. explicit bounds for important commitments
2. refusal protocols without humiliation
3. exit rights when conditions change
4. separation between care and surrender

If commitments cannot be refused, they become coercion.
