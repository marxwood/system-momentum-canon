# Intent and Commitment

Personal agency is not desire.

Personal agency is the ability to bind oneself.

Binding is what makes coordination possible:

- with others
- with institutions
- and with your own future self

This section defines how intent becomes bounded action without self-capture.

---

## Intent is the precondition to legitimate action

Most personal harm is not caused by evil.

It is caused by:

- implicit intent
- drifting motives
- and post-hoc justification

When intent is implicit, action becomes untraceable.

When action is untraceable, repair becomes impossible.

---

## Commitment is the unit of personal reality

Commitments create consequences.

They create:

- obligations
- dependencies
- and trust

A person without commitments is uncoordinatable.

A person with unbounded commitments becomes trapped.

The doctrine treats commitments as bounded contracts.

---

## The goal is correction-capable agency

The goal is not discipline for its own sake.

The goal is:

- to act
- to revise
- and to repair

A commitment that cannot be revised becomes a prison.

A life without commitments becomes drift.

Correction requires both.

---

## Shame is the main distortion

Most commitment systems collapse through shame.

Shame produces:

- concealment
- self-deception
- and avoidance

Avoidance produces drift.

Drift produces more shame.

This loop destroys correction.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is implicit-binding.

Implicit-binding occurs when:

- the actor behaves as committed
- without explicit declaration
- and without explicit bounds
- and without explicit repair protocol

Then the actor becomes trapped in invisible obligations.

Invisible obligations are coercion.

---

## What this section covers

- intent declaration
- commitments and bounds
- accountability without guilt
- drift detection
- review cycles

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. explicit intent before commitments
2. bounded commitments with refusal protocols
3. accountability as repair, not shame
4. drift detection for rationalization and avoidance
5. review cycles as normal correction loops

If commitments become invisible, life becomes coercion.
