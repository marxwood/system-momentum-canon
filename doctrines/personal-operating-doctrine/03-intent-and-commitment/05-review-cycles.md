# Review Cycles

Correction requires periodic review.

Without review, drift becomes invisible.

This module defines review cycles as the maintenance layer of personal agency.

---

## Review is not productivity

Review is not planning.

Review is:

- checking projections against reality
- checking commitments against intent
- checking consequences against repair

The goal is correction.

Not output.

---

## Review prevents narrative hardening

Narratives harden when they are never inspected.

Inspection is uncomfortable.

Discomfort is not danger.

Avoiding discomfort creates long-term collapse.

Review is the ritual that prevents slow self-deception.

---

## Review must be bounded

Unbounded review becomes rumination.

Rumination is not correction.

Correction requires:

- bounded time
- bounded scope
- and explicit outcomes

The outcome is always one of:

- reaffirm
- revise
- refuse
- repair

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is review-to-theater conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- review becomes self-judgment
- and produces shame
- and produces no repair

Then review becomes punishment.

Punishment does not correct.

It corrodes.

---

## Failure modes

### Endless reflection

No decisions, no repair.

### Shame review

Review becomes moral self-attack.

### Avoidance review

Review is skipped until crisis.

### Projection worship

Projections are defended instead of revised.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. periodic review as non-negotiable maintenance
2. bounded scope to prevent rumination
3. explicit outcomes: reaffirm/revise/refuse/repair
4. review results written into projections and surfaces

If review becomes theater, drift becomes permanent.
