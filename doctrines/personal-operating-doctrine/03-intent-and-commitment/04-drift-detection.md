# Drift Detection

Drift is the silent enemy of personal agency.

Drift is not failure.

Drift is the default.

This module defines how drift is detected before it becomes collapse.

---

## Drift is usually rationalization

Most drift is not random.

It is rationalization.

Rationalization is when:

- the actor protects identity
- while pretending to protect intent

Rationalization feels like reason.

It is often defense.

---

## Drift signatures

Drift often has recognizable signatures:

- repeated “I had to”
- repeated “it’s not the right time”
- repeated “they made me”
- repeated “I’m just like this”
- growing resentment without explicit refusal
- commitments becoming vague and untraceable

These are not moral accusations.

They are signals.

---

## Drift must be caught early

Late drift is expensive.

Once drift is advanced:

- trust collapses
- repair debt grows
- and identity hardens

Early drift can be corrected with small revision.

Late drift requires conflict.

Conflict is costly.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is drift-to-self-capture conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- rationalization becomes identity
- avoidance becomes lifestyle
- and repair becomes impossible

Then the actor becomes trapped inside their own story.

---

## Failure modes

### Avoidance normalization

Not acting becomes normal.

### Commitment fog

Nothing is explicit.

### Resentment accumulation

Refusal is never spoken.

### Narrative hardening

Contradiction becomes unbearable.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. explicit drift signals and triggers
2. periodic review of commitments vs behavior
3. early revision before shame accumulates
4. refusal protocols to prevent resentment

If drift is not detected, the actor will slowly lose agency.
