# Integration

A single actor does not operate in isolation.

The actor is simultaneously:

- a personal being
- a role inside an organization
- and a participant inside civilizational reality

These scales overlap.

This overlap creates contamination risks.

This section treats integration as preventing cross-scale collapse.

---

## The integration problem

Each scale has different physics.

Personal life optimizes for:

- stability
- coherence
- and survivability

Organizational life optimizes for:

- coordination
- accountability
- and throughput

Civilizational life optimizes for:

- legitimacy
- power constraints
- and narrative survival

If these are mixed without discipline, the actor collapses.

---

## Role switching is not optional

Most people do not switch roles.

They leak.

Leakage produces:

- burnout
- confusion
- and identity warfare

Integration is therefore a switching discipline.

---

## Integration is not “balance”

Balance is a mood word.

Integration is an operational property:

- boundaries
- translation
- and coherence under constraint

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is cross-scale identity capture.

Capture occurs when:

- organizational role becomes identity
- civilizational narrative becomes identity
- and personal reality disappears

Then the actor becomes a reactive node in a larger machine.

---

## What this section covers

- personal ↔ organizational interface
- personal ↔ civilizational interface
- maintaining coherence across roles

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. explicit role boundaries
2. translation discipline between scales
3. refusal to become narrative machinery
4. coherence as a survivability property

If integration fails, the actor becomes consumed by external systems.
