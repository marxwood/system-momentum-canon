# Personal ↔ Civilizational Interface

Civilizational reality is not personal reality.

It is:

- mass narrative
- legitimacy warfare
- and attention capture

The personal actor must interface with it without becoming consumed.

---

## Media is a control surface

Media is not “information”.

Media is:

- arousal management
- narrative shaping
- and legitimacy signaling

Civilizational systems do not seek your clarity.

They seek your alignment.

Alignment is not truth.

---

## Political reality is high-coercion by default

Civilizational systems operate through:

- fear
- humiliation
- and tribe incentives

The personal actor must treat political reality as:

- hazardous
- but not optional

Ignoring it produces vulnerability.

Consuming it produces collapse.

---

## The interface must be bounded

A bounded interface includes:

- limited exposure windows
- reality contact discipline
- refusal of humiliation participation
- refusal of certainty theater

Without bounds, the actor becomes a reactive node.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is narrative addiction.

Addiction occurs when:

- the actor consumes outrage as meaning
- and treats alignment as morality
- and becomes incapable of local life

Then personal coordination collapses.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. bounded exposure to civilizational narratives
2. refusal of humiliation participation
3. reality contact anchors outside media
4. ability to act locally without needing global certainty

If civilizational reality becomes the self, the actor becomes a weapon.
