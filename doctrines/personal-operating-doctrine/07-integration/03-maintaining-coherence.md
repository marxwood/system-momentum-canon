# Maintaining Coherence

Coherence is not consistency of opinion.

Coherence is the ability to:

- preserve intent
- preserve commitments
- and preserve correction capacity
  across changing roles and contexts.

---

## Coherence is a survivability property

Without coherence, the actor becomes:

- fragmented
- reactive
- and manipulable

Fragmentation is a capture vector.

A fragmented actor can be steered by:

- fear
- status
- and narrative cues

---

## Coherence requires explicit translation

The actor must translate between:

- personal truth
- role obligations
- and civilizational narratives

Translation is not agreement.

Translation is boundary maintenance.

---

## Coherence requires pruning

Too many commitments destroy coherence.

Too many narratives destroy coherence.

Too many roles destroy coherence.

Coherence requires simplification.

Simplification is not laziness.

Simplification is survival.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is coherence-to-performance conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- coherence becomes “having the right identity”
- coherence becomes “being consistent for others”
- and the actor stops correcting

Then coherence becomes rigidity.

Rigidity is collapse under pressure.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. intent continuity across roles
2. bounded commitments and pruning
3. translation discipline without identity claims
4. correction as the core coherence mechanism

If coherence becomes performance, the actor will break under constraint.
