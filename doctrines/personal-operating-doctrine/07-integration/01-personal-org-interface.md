# Personal ↔ Organizational Interface

Organizations are coordination machines.

They do not love you.

They do not hate you.

They operate through:

- incentives
- role constraints
- and status dynamics

A person must interface with this reality without self-corruption.

---

## Roles are not identity

A role is a contract.

It defines:

- obligations
- permissions
- and expected outputs

When role becomes identity, the person becomes capturable.

Capture occurs through:

- praise
- humiliation
- and fear

---

## The interface must be explicit

A stable interface includes:

- explicit time boundaries
- explicit commitment boundaries
- explicit refusal protocols

Without refusal, the organization consumes the person.

Consumption is not productivity.

Consumption is collapse.

---

## Personal dignity must be protected

The organization will often attempt to:

- extract unpaid labor
- extract emotional compliance
- and extract narrative alignment

The actor must treat dignity as an operational stabilizer.

Not as pride.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is role-to-self conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- the actor internalizes organizational status as personal worth
- and treats organizational narratives as personal truth
- and loses refusal capability

Then personal coherence collapses.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. role as contract, not identity
2. explicit boundaries and refusal protocols
3. dignity preservation as stabilizer
4. separation between organizational incentives and personal truth

If the org becomes the self, the actor becomes disposable.
