# Conflict and De-escalation

Conflict is normal.

Avoiding conflict is not peace.

Avoiding conflict produces drift and resentment.

This module treats de-escalation as the method of preserving correction inside conflict.

---

## Conflict has escalation thresholds

Conflict escalates when:

- humiliation appears
- threats appear
- retaliation appears
- exit appears impossible

Escalation is not always intentional.

It is often emergent.

Once escalation begins, cognition narrows.

Repair becomes harder.

---

## De-escalation is not surrender

De-escalation is:

- reducing arousal
- restoring reality contact
- restoring boundaries

It is not agreement.

It is not appeasement.

It is preventing the conversion of conflict into war.

---

## De-escalation requires stance

Without stance, the actor escalates to feel safe.

Escalation is false safety.

Escalation creates future instability.

The actor must treat escalation impulse as danger signal.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is conflict-to-war conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- disagreement becomes humiliation
- retaliation becomes identity
- and repair becomes impossible

Then relationships become battlefields.

Battlefields do not correct.

---

## Failure modes

### Dominance theater

Winning becomes the goal.

### Retaliation loops

Each act demands response.

### Threat escalation

Safety is sought through fear.

### Freeze avoidance

Conflict is avoided until explosion.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. de-escalation as default under arousal
2. refusal to use humiliation as leverage
3. explicit boundaries without threats
4. post-conflict repair as normal practice

If conflict becomes war, the actor will lose coordination capacity.
