# Trust and Credibility

Trust is not liking.

Trust is predictability under constraint.

Credibility is not status.

Credibility is consistency between:

- intent
- action
- and repair

This module treats trust as coordination infrastructure.

---

## Trust is built through repair

Trust is not built through perfection.

Perfection is impossible.

Trust is built through:

- admitting error
- revising commitments
- and repairing consequences

Without repair, trust becomes fear-based.

Fear-based trust is not trust.

It is coercion.

---

## Credibility requires bounded speech

Credibility collapses when:

- speech is inflated
- commitments are vague
- and intent is implicit

A credible actor is not always correct.

A credible actor is correctable.

Correctability is the key.

---

## Trust collapses through humiliation

Humiliation destroys trust quickly.

Because humiliation signals:

- domination
- punishment
- and unsafe contradiction

Once contradiction is unsafe, truth disappears.

Without truth, trust cannot exist.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is credibility-to-performance conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- credibility becomes reputation theater
- repair becomes confession performance
- and truth becomes optional

Then trust collapses into cynicism.

Cynicism is not realism.

It is coordination failure.

---

## Failure modes

### Overpromising

Trust is purchased with future debt.

### Repair theater

Apologies replace correction.

### Status trust

Authority replaces evidence.

### Trust collapse denial

Trust is assumed until it is gone.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. bounded commitments and honest refusal
2. repair as standard practice
3. refusal of humiliation in conflict
4. credibility measured by correctability, not by status

If trust becomes performance, relationships become unstable.
