# Auditability

Auditability is the ability to answer:

- what happened
- why it happened
- who decided
- and what evidence was used

Without auditability, augmentation becomes magic.

Magic cannot be corrected.

---

## Auditability is not bureaucracy

Auditability is a correction mechanism.

It prevents:

- confabulation
- blame games
- and denial

Auditability is especially important when an agent is involved.

Because the agent will generate plausible narratives.

Plausibility is not truth.

---

## Auditability requires traceable artifacts

Auditability requires artifacts such as:

- intent declarations
- task lists
- decision notes
- drafts with provenance
- logs of what the agent produced

The actor does not need perfect logs.

The actor needs enough traceability to repair.

---

## Auditability protects the actor

Auditability is not only about controlling the agent.

It also protects the actor from:

- self-deception
- memory distortion
- and post-hoc rationalization

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is narrative replacement.

Narrative replacement occurs when:

- the actor forgets why something was done
- the agent supplies a plausible story
- and the actor accepts it

Then correction becomes impossible.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. traceable intent and decisions
2. artifacts that survive mood changes
3. logs sufficient for repair
4. refusal to accept “plausible story” as explanation

If auditability collapses, augmentation becomes delusion amplifier.
