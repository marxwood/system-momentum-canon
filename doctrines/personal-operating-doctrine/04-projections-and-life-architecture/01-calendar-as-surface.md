# Calendar as Surface

A calendar is not a schedule.

A calendar is a surface.

It is where commitments become visible and therefore real.

This module treats time as contract.

---

## Time is the scarce constraint

Time is not only a resource.

Time is the binding constraint that forces tradeoffs.

Most personal drift happens because tradeoffs are denied.

Denied tradeoffs become:

- overcommitment
- avoidance
- and hidden resentment

A calendar makes tradeoffs explicit.

---

## The calendar is not truth

The calendar is a projection.

It compresses reality.

It will be wrong.

If the calendar is treated as truth, it becomes tyranny.

The calendar must remain revisable.

Revision is correction.

---

## The calendar exposes hidden commitments

A calendar reveals:

- what you actually prioritize
- what you avoid
- and what you are willing to sacrifice

This can be uncomfortable.

Discomfort is not danger.

Discomfort is evidence.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is calendar-to-legitimacy conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- the calendar becomes moral authority
- revision becomes guilt
- and “busy” becomes identity

Then time becomes coercion.

The actor becomes unfree.

---

## Failure modes

### Overbooking

Commitments exceed reality.

### Ghost commitments

Unwritten obligations control behavior.

### Calendar shame

Revision is treated as failure.

### Performance scheduling

The calendar is used for status, not coordination.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. calendar as explicit commitment surface
2. revision rights without shame
3. bounded commitments with refusal protocols
4. periodic review: calendar vs reality

If the calendar becomes law, the actor will drift or collapse.
