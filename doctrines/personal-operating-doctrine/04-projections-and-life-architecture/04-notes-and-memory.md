# Notes and Memory

Notes are not productivity hacks.

Notes are externalized correction.

Personal memory is fallible.

A correction-capable actor uses notes to preserve provenance and continuity.

---

## Notes prevent confabulation

Confabulation is not lying.

It is reconstruction under pressure.

Notes prevent confabulation by creating:

- timestamps
- explicit commitments
- and evidence anchors

Without notes, the actor will rewrite the past to protect identity.

This is normal.

It is also dangerous.

---

## Notes preserve repair obligations

Repair requires:

- remembering what happened
- remembering what was promised
- and remembering what consequences occurred

Without notes, repair becomes narrative.

Narrative repair becomes theater.

Theater does not repair.

---

## Notes must be bounded

Notes can become noise.

Noise destroys retrieval.

Retrieval failure destroys continuity.

The goal is not maximal capture.

The goal is minimal records for:

- commitments
- conflicts
- and decisions

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is memory-to-story conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- notes are absent
- memory becomes narrative
- and narrative becomes certainty

Then the actor becomes uncorrectable.

---

## Failure modes

### No records

Everything relies on memory.

### Overcapture

Notes become unreadable noise.

### Selective recording

Only flattering facts are stored.

### Weaponized notes

Records are used to punish, not repair.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. external notes for high-impact commitments and conflicts
2. provenance: when and why a belief formed
3. bounded note-taking to preserve retrieval
4. refusal to use notes as punishment

If memory becomes story, correction will collapse.
