# Projects as Containers

Projects are bounded worlds.

They prevent dissolution.

Without containers, intent spreads into everything and becomes nothing.

This module treats projects as the unit of bounded agency.

---

## Projects prevent identity diffusion

Many people treat life as one project.

This produces:

- confusion
- drift
- and self-judgment

Projects allow separation:

- work
- relationships
- learning
- repair
- exploration

Separation is not fragmentation.

Separation is boundary hygiene.

---

## Projects create explicit scope

A project defines:

- what is included
- what is excluded
- what success means
- and what “done” means

Without scope, progress becomes narrative.

Narrative progress is often self-deception.

---

## Projects preserve commitments

Commitments without containers become invisible.

Invisible commitments become coercion.

Projects make commitments:

- visible
- revisitable
- and repairable

This is the core value.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is project-to-self-worth conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- projects become identity
- failure becomes shame
- and revision becomes humiliation

Then projects become self-punishment.

Self-punishment destroys correction.

---

## Failure modes

### Infinite projects

No closure, no repair.

### Scope drift

Everything becomes part of one project.

### Hidden commitments

Work happens without explicit containers.

### Status projects

Projects exist for reputation, not intent.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. projects as bounded containers for intent
2. explicit scope and closure conditions
3. separation between projects and self-worth
4. review cycles that revise projects without shame

If projects become identity, the actor will drift or collapse.
