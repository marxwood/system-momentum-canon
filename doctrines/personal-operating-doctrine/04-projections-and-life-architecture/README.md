# Projections and Life Architecture

A personal operating system requires structure.

Structure is not meaning.

Structure is scaffolding.

This section defines how projections are used to create stability without becoming authority.

---

## Life architecture is not control

Personal systems often fail because they promise control.

Control is not available.

Reality includes:

- uncertainty
- conflict
- and external constraints

Life architecture is not the removal of constraint.

It is the design of survivable coordination inside constraint.

---

## Projections are necessary

A person cannot coordinate without projections.

Projections include:

- calendars
- routines
- projects
- notes
- and summaries

The problem is not projection.

The problem is projection absolutism.

Projection absolutism turns scaffolding into prison.

---

## Architecture reduces cognitive coercion

Without structure, the actor must decide constantly.

Constant decision-making produces fatigue.

Fatigue produces:

- impulsive action
- avoidance
- and drift

Life architecture reduces this by:

- externalizing commitments
- reducing repeated decisions
- and making repair visible

---

## Architecture must preserve revision

A personal system that cannot be revised becomes coercion.

Revision is not failure.

Revision is correction.

This section treats revision rights as the central invariant.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is projection-to-coercion conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- structure becomes identity
- time becomes moral law
- and revision becomes shame

Then the actor becomes a servant of scaffolding.

---

## What this section covers

- calendar as surface
- routines as stabilizers
- projects as containers
- notes and memory
- simplification and pruning

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. projections as scaffolding, never as truth
2. surfaces where commitments become explicit
3. revision rights without shame
4. maintenance as normal (pruning, simplification)

If structure becomes authority, the system becomes coercive.
