# Contradiction Handling

Contradiction is not failure.

Contradiction is information.

A correction-capable actor can hold contradictions without collapsing into:

- denial
- panic
- or narrative war

This module treats contradiction as a stabilizer.

---

## Contradiction threatens identity

Most contradictions do not threaten logic.

They threaten identity.

Identity threat triggers:

- defense
- certainty
- and enemy-making

Enemy-making is a way to remove contradiction.

It also destroys correction.

---

## Contradiction is often unresolved

Many contradictions cannot be resolved quickly.

The actor must operate while:

- not knowing
- not deciding
- or holding multiple hypotheses

This is uncomfortable.

Discomfort is not danger.

---

## Contradiction can be operationalized

Contradiction handling is not philosophy.

It is a protocol:

- label contradiction
- prevent forced resolution
- preserve evidence
- and delay irreversible commitments

This preserves correction.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is contradiction-to-war conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- contradiction becomes humiliation
- disagreement becomes enemy
- and resolution becomes domination

Then the actor becomes unsafe.

To others.

To themselves.

---

## Failure modes

### Forced resolution

Picking a story to stop discomfort.

### Collapse into cynicism

“All is lies” as relief.

### Moral escalation

Contradiction becomes proof of evil.

### Avoidance

Refusing any contact with conflicting evidence.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. explicit labeling of contradictions
2. delayed irreversible commitments under contradiction
3. preservation of evidence and provenance
4. refusal of humiliation-based resolution

If contradiction becomes war, reality contact collapses.
