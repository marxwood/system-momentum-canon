# Uncertainty Tolerance

Uncertainty is permanent.

The actor cannot eliminate it.

The actor can only choose how to operate under it.

This module treats uncertainty tolerance as a core personal capability.

---

## Intolerance produces certainty theater

When uncertainty is intolerable, the actor creates:

- stories
- enemies
- or guarantees

These are not truth.

They are comfort objects.

Comfort objects often destroy repair capacity.

---

## Uncertainty is not indecision

Tolerance does not mean paralysis.

It means:

- acting with bounded commitments
- and explicit revision rights

This is the difference between:

- correction-capable action
- and compulsive action.

---

## Uncertainty is a coordination skill

Personal life includes:

- relationships
- institutions
- and unpredictable events

Uncertainty tolerance prevents:

- overreaction
- escalation
- and identity collapse

Without it, the actor becomes reactive.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is uncertainty-to-control conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- uncertainty triggers panic
- panic triggers domination attempts
- and domination triggers harm

Then the actor creates the instability they feared.

---

## Failure modes

### Certainty addiction

Always needing a final answer.

### Control escalation

Increasing coercion to reduce ambiguity.

### Narrative locking

A story becomes unchangeable.

### Shame avoidance

Avoiding unknowns because they imply imperfection.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. explicit “unknown” as allowed state
2. bounded commitments with revision rights
3. refusal of certainty theater under pressure
4. post-action review as normal practice

If uncertainty is intolerable, the actor will become coercive.
