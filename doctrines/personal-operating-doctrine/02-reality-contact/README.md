# Reality Contact

Reality contact is the personal equivalent of civilizational shared reality.

Without it, the actor cannot coordinate.

The actor can only narrate.

This section defines epistemic discipline for a single actor operating with an augmentation layer.

---

## Reality contact is not “being right”

Reality contact is not correctness.

It is the ability to:

- notice what is happening
- separate observation from interpretation
- and revise beliefs without collapse

A system can be wrong and still correction-capable.

A system that cannot revise is not wrong.

It is captured.

---

## Personal epistemic collapse is common

Most people do not collapse into delusion through insanity.

They collapse through normal mechanisms:

- shame avoidance
- fear management
- identity defense
- and narrative addiction

These mechanisms are socially rewarded.

They feel like strength.

They are correction failure.

---

## Reality contact is a survival mechanism

Reality contact is not intellectual.

It is practical.

Without reality contact:

- commitments drift
- relationships collapse
- and projections become tyrants

The actor becomes reactive.

The actor becomes easy to manipulate.

---

## Augmentation increases epistemic risk

An agentic layer increases:

- speed
- confidence
- and output

It also increases:

- confabulation risk
- narrative completion
- and projection absolutism

The doctrine treats augmentation as a power amplifier.

Power amplifiers require discipline.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is narrative substitution.

Narrative substitution occurs when:

- interpretation becomes fact
- memory becomes story
- and certainty becomes comfort

At that point, the actor loses correction.

The actor becomes uncorrectable.

---

## What this section covers

- observation vs interpretation
- evidence primitives
- provenance and memory discipline
- contradiction handling
- uncertainty tolerance

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. explicit separation of observation and interpretation
2. bounded evidence anchors
3. memory treated as fallible and auditable
4. contradiction survivability
5. uncertainty tolerance without panic

If reality contact collapses, all other modules become theater.
