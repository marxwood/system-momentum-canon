# Observation Before Action

The first act of agency is delay.

Not delay as avoidance.

Delay as interrupting compulsion.

This module defines observation as the precondition to action.

---

## Most harm is fast

Most personal harm is not strategic.

It is fast.

It comes from:

- impulse
- arousal
- humiliation
- and fear

Fast harm feels justified.

Fast harm creates long repair obligations.

---

## Observation is not analysis

Observation is not thinking harder.

Observation is:

- noticing sensation
- noticing impulse
- noticing interpretation forming

The goal is not to eliminate emotion.

The goal is to prevent emotion from becoming authority.

---

## The pause is a boundary

The pause is the smallest boundary.

Without it, the actor has no boundary.

The actor becomes:

- stimulus → narrative → action

This is not agency.

It is automation.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is compulsion conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- impulse becomes decision
- interpretation becomes truth
- and action becomes discharge

Then the actor cannot correct.

The actor can only justify.

---

## Failure modes

### “It was obvious”

Interpretation is treated as fact.

### “I had to”

Impulse is treated as necessity.

### “They deserved it”

Humiliation becomes permission.

### “I’m just like this”

Compulsion becomes identity.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. a pause between impulse and action
2. observation of interpretation forming
3. refusal to treat arousal as authority
4. post-action review and repair

If there is no pause, there is no agency.
