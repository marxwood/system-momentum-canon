# Identity as Interface

Identity is unavoidable.

But identity is not truth.

Identity is an interface.

This module treats identity as a coordination tool, not as a metaphysical claim.

---

## Identity has two functions

Identity has legitimate functions:

1. coordination
2. continuity

Coordination: others can predict you.

Continuity: you can predict yourself.

These functions are useful.

They are also dangerous.

---

## Identity becomes a trap through authority

Identity becomes a trap when it becomes authoritative.

Authoritative identity means:

- “this is what I am”
- therefore “this is what I must do”
- therefore “this is what cannot be questioned”

At that point, identity blocks correction.

---

## Identity is a capture surface

Identity can be captured by:

- groups
- status incentives
- and narratives

Once captured, the actor becomes predictable in the worst way:

- easy to trigger
- easy to recruit
- easy to shame

Captured identity is a control interface for others.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is identity-to-permission conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- identity grants moral permission
- identity excuses harm
- and contradiction becomes betrayal

Then identity becomes a weapon.

The actor becomes uncorrectable.

---

## Failure modes

### Identity rigidity

Revision feels like death.

### Group possession

Belonging requires alignment.

### Moral immunity

“I’m good” excuses harm.

### Shame lock-in

Past labels prevent repair.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. identity as interface, not truth
2. revision rights without humiliation
3. refusal of identity-based permission claims
4. separation between belonging and epistemics

If identity becomes authority, correction becomes impossible.
