# Anti-Moral Intoxication

Moral certainty is intoxicating.

It feels clean.

It feels powerful.

It is often a capture mechanism.

This module treats moral intoxication as a personal hazard.

---

## Moral intoxication produces permission

Moral intoxication creates permission to:

- humiliate
- punish
- and destroy

The actor feels righteous.

Righteousness hides consequences.

Consequences do not disappear.

They accumulate as repair debt.

---

## Moral intoxication destroys correction

Correction requires:

- admitting error
- revising beliefs
- and repairing harm

Moral intoxication makes all three impossible.

Because error becomes:

- shame
- and betrayal
- and loss of identity

The actor becomes uncorrectable.

---

## Moral intoxication is socially rewarded

Moral intoxication is not only internal.

It is reinforced by:

- group belonging
- attention incentives
- and status games

This makes it hard to detect.

It feels like virtue.

It is often addiction.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is righteousness-to-cruelty conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- certainty becomes identity
- disagreement becomes enemy-making
- and punishment becomes pleasure

Then the actor becomes unsafe.

Not only to others.

To themselves.

---

## Failure modes

### Purity spirals

Ever stricter standards, shrinking humanity.

### Certainty theater

Performing conviction to gain belonging.

### Humiliation as truth

Truth becomes degradation.

### Enemy addiction

Meaning requires enemies.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. suspicion of certainty as a signal
2. refusal of humiliation as truth method
3. repair priority over moral performance
4. deliberate exposure to contradiction

If righteousness becomes pleasure, cruelty will follow.
