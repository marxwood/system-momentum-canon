# Non-Vengeance

Vengeance is not justice.

Vengeance is escalation.

Escalation feels like strength.

Escalation produces self-corruption.

This module treats non-vengeance as a correction stabilizer.

---

## Vengeance is a loop

Vengeance is rarely a single act.

It is a loop:

- injury
- humiliation
- retaliation
- counter-retaliation
- identity hardening

Loops are attractive because they create certainty.

Certainty feels like relief.

Relief is not repair.

---

## Vengeance destroys reality contact

Vengeance requires narrative.

Narrative requires simplification.

Simplification produces:

- enemies
- certainty
- and permission to harm

At that point, the actor cannot see consequences.

The actor can only see justification.

---

## Non-vengeance is not weakness

Non-vengeance is refusal of loop logic.

It is the ability to:

- protect boundaries
- without retaliation addiction
- and without humiliation as weapon

This preserves dignity.

Dignity preserves correction.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is retaliation-to-identity conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- retaliation becomes “who I am”
- harm becomes “what they deserve”
- and repair becomes “betrayal”

Then the actor becomes a war machine.

War machines cannot coordinate.

---

## Failure modes

### Punishment obsession

The goal becomes suffering.

### Escalation addiction

More intensity is required over time.

### Revenge justification

Harm becomes morally clean.

### Self-corruption denial

The actor cannot see themselves changing.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable actor preserves:

1. boundary enforcement without retaliation loops
2. refusal to use humiliation as weapon
3. repair as higher priority than discharge
4. post-conflict review of self-corruption risk

If vengeance becomes normal, the actor will become unfree.
