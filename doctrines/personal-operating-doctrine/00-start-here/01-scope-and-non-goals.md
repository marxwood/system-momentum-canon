# Scope and Non-Goals

This branch defines how a single actor operates.

It treats the actor as:

- a decision-maker
- a coordinator
- and a repair agent

It assumes the actor may also operate with an agentic augmentation layer.

---

## Scope

This branch covers:

- how a single actor preserves reality contact
- how intent becomes bounded action
- how commitments are made and kept
- how drift is detected and corrected
- how projections (calendar, routines, projects) are used without becoming truth
- how speech is treated as coordination, not identity
- how augmentation is delegated safely

The focus is operational.

Not expressive.

Not metaphysical.

---

## What is excluded

This branch excludes:

- metaphysical claims about reality
- spiritual doctrine
- enlightenment narratives
- “life purpose” as a universal answer
- therapeutic protocols
- medical advice
- political prescriptions
- personality typing systems

It also excludes “optimization” as a primary goal.

Optimization is allowed only as a consequence of correction.

---

## Anti-self-help constraint

Most self-help fails because it:

- promises control
- sells certainty
- and manufactures narratives

This doctrine rejects all three.

It assumes:

- uncertainty is permanent
- constraints are real
- and failure is normal

The goal is not perfection.

The goal is survivable correction.

---

## Anti-metaphysics constraint

This branch uses terms like:

- posture
- projection
- reality contact
- repair

These are not metaphysical.

They are operational.

They describe:

- how decisions are formed
- how commitments create consequences
- how drift happens
- and how correction is preserved

If a concept cannot be operationalized, it does not belong here.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is narrative replacement.

Narrative replacement occurs when:

- the actor stops contacting reality
- and starts living inside explanations
- and treats technique as salvation

At that point, the doctrine becomes religion.

Religion cannot correct.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable personal doctrine preserves:

1. bounded scope and explicit non-goals
2. operational language instead of metaphysical language
3. correction and repair as primary objectives
4. refusal of certainty theater

If the doctrine becomes a story, it becomes a trap.
