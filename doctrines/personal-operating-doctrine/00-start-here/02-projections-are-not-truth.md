# Projections Are Not Truth

Personal life requires representations.

Representations are unavoidable.

You cannot coordinate without them.

But representations are not truth.

This doctrine treats projections as operational tools.

---

## Why projections are dangerous

A projection compresses reality.

Compression produces:

- blind spots
- distortion
- and false certainty

The danger is not that projections are wrong.

The danger is that projections become authoritative.

When projections become authoritative, correction becomes betrayal.

---

## Projection collapse modes

Personal projection collapse usually takes one of these forms:

### Calendar absolutism

Time becomes law.

Life becomes scheduling.

Reality contact collapses.

### Metrics absolutism

Numbers become meaning.

Human experience becomes noise.

### Narrative absolutism

A story explains everything.

Contradiction becomes intolerable.

### Task absolutism

Action becomes identity.

Rest becomes guilt.

---

## Projection discipline

Projection discipline is not “better planning”.

Projection discipline is:

- keeping projections correctable
- keeping them bounded
- and keeping them subordinate to reality contact

A projection must be allowed to be wrong.

It must be easy to revise.

It must not become moral authority.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is projection-to-reality inversion.

Inversion occurs when:

- the projection defines what is real
- and lived reality becomes “deviation”
- and correction becomes failure

At that point, the actor is governed by representations.

The actor is no longer free.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable personal system preserves:

1. projections as tools, never as truth
2. explicit revision rights (projections can be changed without shame)
3. reality contact that overrides projections
4. refusal of projection-based identity

If projections become truth, the system becomes a prison.
