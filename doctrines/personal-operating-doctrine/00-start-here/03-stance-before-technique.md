# Stance Before Technique

Technique without stance becomes compulsion.

Technique without stance becomes identity.

Technique without stance becomes theater.

This doctrine treats stance as the precondition for any personal system.

---

## Stance is not mood

Stance is not optimism.

Stance is not calm.

Stance is an operating posture toward:

- contradiction
- uncertainty
- and consequence

A stable stance prevents technique from becoming self-harm.

---

## The default failure

Most people do not lack technique.

They lack non-reactivity.

Under pressure, the actor becomes:

- a narrative machine
- a justification engine
- or a retaliation loop

Technique then becomes a weapon used by reaction.

---

## Stance makes correction possible

Correction requires:

- admitting error
- revising projections
- and repairing harm

Without stance, these become humiliating.

Humiliation produces defense.

Defense produces denial.

Denial destroys correction.

---

## The personal hazard

The hazard is technique-to-identity conversion.

Conversion occurs when:

- technique becomes moral status
- and failure becomes shame
- and revision becomes humiliation

Then the system becomes self-punishment.

Self-punishment does not produce correction.

It produces collapse.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable personal system preserves:

1. stance as precondition to technique
2. non-reactivity as the default skill
3. correction without humiliation
4. repair as normal, not exceptional

If stance collapses, technique becomes coercion.
