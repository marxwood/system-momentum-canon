# Reality Fragmentation

A civilization does not require a single narrative.

But it requires a minimal shared reality.

Reality fragmentation occurs when society loses shared anchors and splits into incompatible worlds.

These worlds do not merely disagree.

They become mutually illegible.

---

## Fragmentation is not diversity

Fragmentation is not pluralism.

Pluralism can preserve correction capacity.

Fragmentation destroys it.

In fragmentation:

- evidence primitives do not travel
- provenance is not trusted
- contradiction is treated as hostility
- belief update is identity death

Coordination becomes impossible.

Coercion becomes attractive.

---

## How fragmentation happens

Fragmentation is produced by stable mechanisms:

- attention capture
- narrative monopoly attempts
- credibility theater
- selective enforcement
- identity-coded evidence
- fear-based self-censorship

Fragmentation is not always accidental.

It is often profitable.

It is often useful to power.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is not disagreement.

The hazard is loss of translatability.

When actors cannot translate claims across worlds, they stop attempting.

This produces:

- contempt
- dehumanization
- and readiness for coercion

---

## Failure modes

### Parallel realities

Different groups live in incompatible epistemic regimes.

Each regime becomes self-sealing.

### Translation collapse

Even good-faith translators are punished.

Translation is treated as betrayal.

### Coordination collapse

Society cannot coordinate on:

- shared threats
- shared repairs
- shared accountability

The only remaining coordination mechanism becomes force.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. evidence primitives that can cross groups
2. provenance that can be inspected across boundaries
3. contradiction capacity that does not require humiliation

Without these, fragmentation becomes permanent.

Permanent fragmentation is an invitation to coercive closure.
