# Resistance Without Propaganda

In captured or collapsing epistemic environments, resistance emerges.

The civilizational problem is that resistance often becomes:

- counter-propaganda
- humiliation reversal
- moral intoxication
- tribal closure

These forms can mobilize.

They rarely preserve correction capacity.

They often reproduce the machinery they oppose.

---

## The core stance

The stance of this module is:

**Resistance must not destroy the substrate it depends on.**

If resistance destroys:

- evidence primitives
- provenance
- contradiction capacity
- uncertainty tolerance

then resistance becomes a capture accelerator.

It may win power.

It will not win correction.

---

## Why propaganda is attractive to resistance

Propaganda is attractive because it provides:

- speed
- emotional certainty
- mobilization
- enemy clarity
- loyalty cohesion

Correction is slower.

Correction feels weak under pressure.

This is why resistance often adopts the enemy’s tools.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is resistance that becomes:

- indistinguishable from propaganda
- immune to contradiction
- addicted to humiliation
- dependent on moral licensing

This produces:

- permanent enemies
- permanent emergency
- and eventual coercion

---

## Failure modes

### Humiliation reversal

Resistance adopts humiliation as justice.

Humiliation is a coercion engine.

It will be used again.

### Narrative closure

Resistance demands a single narrative.

Contradiction becomes betrayal.

This reproduces capture.

### Purity escalation

Resistance turns inward.

Purity becomes legitimacy.

This destroys coordination.

### Heroism addiction

Resistance depends on heroic individuals.

Heroism is not scalable.

Heroism collapses into martyrdom or burnout.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable resistance must preserve:

1. evidence primitives and provenance
2. contradiction capacity without humiliation
3. belief update without identity death

Resistance that cannot preserve these will either fail or become the next captured order.
