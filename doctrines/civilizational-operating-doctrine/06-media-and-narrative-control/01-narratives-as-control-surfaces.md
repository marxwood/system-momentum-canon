# Narratives as Control Surfaces

Narratives are not explanations.

Narratives are not literature.

Narratives are coordination mechanisms.

They shape:

- what is seen as real
- what is seen as moral
- what is seen as permitted
- what is seen as inevitable

This is why narrative control is one of the first capture moves.

---

## Why narratives work

Narratives work because they compress complexity.

They offer:

- a simplified causal model
- a moral orientation
- a stable enemy or hero
- a sense of inevitability

Compression is not always false.

But compression is always vulnerable to capture.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is when narratives become:

- immune to evidence primitives
- immune to provenance and integrity
- immune to contradiction capacity

When narratives become immune, they stop coordinating.

They start commanding.

Command is the language of coercion.

---

## Narratives and legitimacy

Narratives do not merely describe legitimacy.

They produce it.

They convert:

- interpretation into certainty
- certainty into morality
- morality into permission
- permission into action

This is how action becomes unbounded without explicit intent declaration.

---

## Failure modes

### Narrative monopoly

One narrative becomes the only permitted interface.

Contradiction becomes disloyalty.

Correction dies.

### Narrative fragmentation

Many narratives compete with no shared anchors.

This produces exhaustion.

Exhaustion produces longing for closure.

Closure is supplied by coercion.

### Narrative intoxication

Narratives become addictive.

Actors seek emotional certainty rather than correction.

This produces moral licensing.

Moral licensing erases boundaries.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. evidence primitives that narratives cannot erase
2. provenance that narratives cannot rewrite
3. contradiction capacity that narratives cannot criminalize

Without these, narratives become control surfaces for coercion.
