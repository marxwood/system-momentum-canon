# Epistemic Pollution

Epistemic pollution is not simply lying.

It is the degradation of the shared reality substrate.

It occurs when the environment becomes saturated with claims that cannot be:

- inspected
- attributed
- compared
- or corrected

A polluted epistemic environment can still coordinate society.

It coordinates through:

- fear
- identity
- and narrative dominance

It cannot coordinate through correction.

---

## Pollution is a system property

Epistemic pollution is not only produced by malicious actors.

It is produced by:

- incentives
- speed
- attention capture
- and status rewards for certainty

Even well-intentioned actors can pollute.

Because pollution is cheap.

Correction is expensive.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is not misinformation.

The hazard is loss of inspectability.

When inspectability collapses:

- credibility becomes inherited
- contradiction becomes hostility
- belief update becomes impossible

This is epistemic capture.

---

## Pollution vectors

This doctrine does not catalog platforms.

It catalogs vectors.

### 1) Claim flooding

The volume of claims exceeds inspection capacity.

### 2) Context stripping

Artifacts are detached from conditions.

### 3) Provenance erasure

Sources are hidden or blurred.

### 4) Manufactured ambiguity

Claims are made unfalsifiable.

### 5) Aesthetic evidence

Evidence is performed as style rather than provided as artifact.

---

## Failure modes

### Cynicism equilibrium

Actors stop believing evidence matters.

This produces apathy reality.

### Tribal immunity

Evidence is accepted or rejected based on identity.

This produces tribal reality.

### Authority closure

Inspection is replaced by authority decision.

This produces authority reality.

All three are stable.

None are correction-capable.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. inspectable evidence primitives
2. provenance and integrity
3. contradiction capacity without humiliation

If pollution overwhelms these, coercion becomes the stabilizer.
