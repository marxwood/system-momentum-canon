# Survival Under Coercion

This section is not about heroism.

It is about continuity.

Under coercion, the primary civilizational risk is not defeat.

It is epistemic death.

Epistemic death is the condition where:

- contradiction becomes suicidal
- inspection becomes impossible
- and correction capacity disappears

A civilization can survive coercion.

But only if correction capacity survives inside it.

---

## The core stance

The stance of this section is:

**Survival means preserving correction capacity under coercion.**

Not winning.

Not purity.

Not moral victory.

Continuity.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is survival being interpreted as:

- total silence
- total submission
- or total escalation

These are all stable equilibria.

None preserve correction.

Coercive orders are not defeated by arguments.

They are outlived by continuity of correction.

---

## What this section covers

This section defines:

- surveillance effects and paranoia collapse
- dependence and blackmail mechanics
- safe communication patterns
- distributed risk contribution
- refuge and exit as survival infrastructure
- continuity without surrender

This is not activism doctrine.

It is operational survival doctrine.

---

## Failure modes this section defends against

This section exists because coercion produces predictable collapses:

- paranoia destroys coordination
- silence destroys correction
- martyrdom destroys continuity
- centralized resistance becomes capturable
- blackmail destroys trust
- communication becomes lethal

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable society under coercion must preserve:

1. safe contradiction pockets
2. survivable exit paths
3. distributed contribution (no single point of failure)
4. communication patterns that do not collapse into paranoia

Without these, coercion becomes permanent.
