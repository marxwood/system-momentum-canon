# Refuge and Exit

Under coercion, exit is not a luxury.

Exit is a survival mechanism.

Exit makes coercion expensive.

Refuge preserves continuity.

A society that has no refuge will eventually accept silence as “peace”.

---

## Refuge is not abandonment

Refuge is not betrayal.

Refuge is the preservation of future correction.

When coercion rises, many people cannot act.

They can only survive.

Survival is not cowardice.

Survival is continuity.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is refuge being criminalized.

When refuge is criminalized:

- exit paths disappear
- dependence becomes permanent
- coercion becomes cheap
- and the society becomes trapped

A trapped society cannot correct.

It can only comply or explode.

---

## Failure modes

### Exit as treason

Leaving is framed as betrayal.

This produces loyalty capture.

### Refuge as humiliation

Refuge requires submission.

Submission becomes a punishment.

### Refuge monopoly

Only elites can exit.

This produces resentment and violence readiness.

### Refuge collapse into cynicism

People believe there is no refuge.

This produces despair.

Despair produces apathy.

Apathy produces capture.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable society under coercion must preserve:

1. at least one survivable refuge path
2. protection for those who exit
3. legitimacy for continuity outside the coercive center

If refuge disappears, coercion becomes permanent.
