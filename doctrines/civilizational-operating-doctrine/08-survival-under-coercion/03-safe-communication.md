# Safe Communication

Under coercion, communication becomes dangerous.

The primary risk is not that information is wrong.

The primary risk is that communication itself becomes punishable.

When communication becomes punishable:

- contradiction collapses
- coordination collapses
- and correction capacity dies

Safe communication is therefore civilizational infrastructure.

---

## Safe communication is not secrecy fetish

Safe communication is not the fantasy of perfect secrecy.

Perfect secrecy does not exist.

Safe communication is the discipline of:

- reducing unnecessary exposure
- avoiding predictable traps
- and preserving continuity of correction

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is communication collapsing into:

- total silence
  or
- reckless exposure

Silence destroys correction.

Reckless exposure produces martyrdom.

Martyrdom destroys continuity.

---

## Failure modes

### Over-encoding collapse

Communication becomes so encoded that coordination fails.

This produces isolation.

Isolation produces helplessness.

### Under-encoding collapse

Communication is naive.

Punishment becomes cheap.

This produces fear.

Fear produces silence.

### Paranoia contamination

People begin to suspect every message.

This destroys trust.

### Centralized channels

Communication depends on a single channel.

Single channels are capturable.

Capture collapses coordination.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable society under coercion must preserve:

1. redundancy of channels
2. low-risk communication patterns
3. shared assumptions that prevent paranoia inflation
4. separation between identity and content

If communication collapses, coercion becomes permanent.
