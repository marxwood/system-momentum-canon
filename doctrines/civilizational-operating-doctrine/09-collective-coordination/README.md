# Collective Coordination

Civilizations do not fail because people disagree.

They fail because coordination becomes impossible.

When coordination becomes impossible, societies stabilize through:

- coercion
- monopoly
- or collapse

This section treats coordination as infrastructure.

Not as moral unity.

Not as ideology.

Not as hope.

Infrastructure.

---

## The core stance

The stance of this section is:

**Coordination must preserve correction capacity.**

Coordination that requires:

- silence
- humiliation
- or obedience

is not coordination.

It is coercion.

---

## Coordination is not consensus

Consensus is not scalable.

Civilizations coordinate through:

- bounded procedures
- shared anchors
- incentives
- and distributed enforcement

The question is not whether people agree.

The question is whether correction remains possible.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is coordination being replaced by:

- tribal cohesion
- propaganda alignment
- elite bargains immune to accountability
- or fear-based obedience

These can all create order.

They do not create correction-capable order.

---

## What this section covers

This section defines:

- collective action equilibria
- free rider mechanics
- distributed oversight
- parallel legitimacy building
- reform sequencing
- accountability without purge
- and the target correction-capable order

This is not utopian design.

It is coordination realism.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. coordination mechanisms that do not require ideological unity
2. oversight that cannot be monopolized
3. reform that is reversible and staged
4. accountability that does not become revenge

Without these, coordination collapses into coercion.
