# Parallel Legitimacy

When legitimacy collapses, societies face a dangerous temptation:

replace the system entirely.

Replacement fantasies are emotionally satisfying.

They are also capture accelerators.

Because collapse panic produces demand for closure.

Closure is supplied by coercion.

Parallel legitimacy is the alternative.

It is the process of rebuilding legitimacy without requiring collapse.

---

## Legitimacy is not declared

Legitimacy is not produced by slogans.

Legitimacy is produced by:

- bounded action
- accountability ledgers
- repair mechanisms
- and survivable contradiction

If these cannot be demonstrated, legitimacy cannot be rebuilt.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is legitimacy being rebuilt through:

- propaganda
- humiliation reversal
- purity tests
- and enemy inflation

These methods can mobilize.

They cannot preserve correction capacity.

They reproduce capture.

---

## What parallel legitimacy means

Parallel legitimacy means:

- building correction-capable procedures
- building inspectable accountability
- building survivable repair
- building bounded enforcement

in parallel with existing captured structures.

Not as utopia.

As redundancy.

Redundancy is what makes correction survivable.

---

## Failure modes

### Parallel legitimacy as revolution theater

Parallel structures are treated as symbolic protest.

This produces burnout and cynicism.

### Parallel legitimacy as cult

Parallel structures become identity objects.

Contradiction becomes betrayal.

This reproduces capture.

### Parallel legitimacy without boundaries

Parallel structures imitate power without constraints.

This becomes coercion in miniature.

### Parallel legitimacy without continuity

Parallel structures require heroism.

Heroism is not scalable.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. legitimacy built from procedures, not narratives
2. bounded action and accountability
3. survivable participation without martyrdom
4. redundancy that cannot be monopolized

If legitimacy cannot be rebuilt without humiliation, the order will stabilize into coercion.
