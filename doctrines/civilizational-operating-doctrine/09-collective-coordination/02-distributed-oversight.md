# Distributed Oversight

Oversight is not bureaucracy.

Oversight is the ability to punish misuse of power.

If oversight is monopolized, it will be captured.

If oversight is absent, power becomes unbounded.

This module treats oversight as redundancy.

Redundancy is what makes correction survivable.

---

## Oversight is a power function

Oversight is not moral critique.

Oversight is operational.

It answers:

- who can inspect whom
- who can contest decisions
- who can punish misuse
- and who is protected when they contradict

Without these, accountability becomes theater.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is oversight being centralized.

Centralized oversight creates:

- single points of capture
- single points of intimidation
- and single points of failure

Captured oversight is worse than no oversight.

Because it produces legitimacy theater.

Legitimacy theater stabilizes capture.

---

## Why oversight must be distributed

Distributed oversight matters because:

- power is layered
- dependence is layered
- capture is adaptive
- and fear is contagious

A single oversight organ cannot survive pressure.

Redundancy is the only scalable defense.

---

## Failure modes

### Oversight monopoly

One institution owns oversight.

Capture of that institution ends correction.

### Oversight as faction weapon

Oversight is used selectively.

Friends are immune.

Enemies are punished.

This produces capture.

### Oversight intimidation

Oversight actors are punished.

This produces silence equilibria.

### Oversight ritualization

Oversight exists as procedure theater.

This produces cynicism.

Cynicism produces apathy.

Apathy produces capture.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. multiple independent oversight paths
2. protection for contradictors and inspectors
3. accountability ledgers for oversight itself

If oversight cannot punish misuse safely, the order is already coercive.
