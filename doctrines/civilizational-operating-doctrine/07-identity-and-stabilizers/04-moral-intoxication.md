# Moral Intoxication

Moral intoxication is not morality.

It is the addiction to moral certainty.

It produces:

- righteousness as identity
- purity as legitimacy
- and punishment as virtue

Moral intoxication is one of the fastest paths from disagreement to coercion.

---

## Why moral intoxication is powerful

Moral intoxication is powerful because it solves coordination problems cheaply.

It provides:

- certainty
- cohesion
- enemy clarity
- permission for unbounded action

It makes contradiction feel like evil.

This is why it is a capture accelerant.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is moral intoxication becoming the legitimacy layer.

When moral intoxication becomes legitimacy:

- intent declaration becomes unnecessary
- action boundaries are erased
- accountability becomes purge
- consequence mapping collapses into slogans

This is how coercive orders are built while believing they are virtuous.

---

## Purity spirals

Moral intoxication produces purity spirals.

Purity spirals are the dynamic where:

- belonging requires increasing conformity
- contradiction becomes betrayal
- and legitimacy requires escalation

Purity spirals do not stabilize.

They radicalize.

Radicalization increases violence readiness.

---

## Failure modes

### Purity tests

People are evaluated by loyalty rituals.

This produces fear and pre-compliance.

### Enemy inflation

Enemies are required to maintain moral certainty.

Permanent enemies stabilize coercion.

### Humiliation as justice

Humiliation becomes virtue.

This normalizes violence thresholds.

### Moral immunity

Certain actors become morally immune.

Immune actors become power.

Power becomes capture.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. contradiction capacity against moral certainty
2. boundaries that survive virtue framing
3. accountability that does not become purge
4. dignity protections against humiliation justice

If moral intoxication cannot be contradicted safely, coercion becomes inevitable.
