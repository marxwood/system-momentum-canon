# Anti-Fragile Constraints

Most constraints fail under pressure.

A correction-capable order cannot rely on constraints that only work in calm conditions.

Constraints must survive:

- fear
- conflict
- emergency logic
- factional competition
- elite coordination

This section defines the properties of constraints that do not collapse when tested.

---

## Anti-fragile is not perfect

Anti-fragile does not mean unbreakable.

It means:

- constraints degrade gracefully
- correction remains possible
- capture does not become irreversible

Anti-fragile constraints are not ideals.

They are survivable designs.

---

## Why constraints fail

Constraints fail for predictable reasons:

### 1) They depend on virtue

Virtue is not a stable input.

### 2) They depend on a single organ

Single organs are capturable.

### 3) They depend on secrecy

Secrecy is a capture accelerator.

### 4) They depend on calm

Calm is not guaranteed.

---

## Properties of anti-fragile constraints

This doctrine does not prescribe specific institutions.

It defines constraint properties.

### 1) Redundancy

More than one path must exist to:

- inspect
- contest
- and punish misuse

Redundancy prevents monopoly.

### 2) Reversibility

Actions must be designed to allow rollback.

Reversibility is the opposite of coercive closure.

### 3) Local survivability

Constraints must function at local levels even if higher levels are captured.

Local correction pockets are civilizational lifeboats.

### 4) Low dependency on heroism

Constraints that require heroic actors will fail.

Heroism is not scalable.

### 5) Auditability

Constraints must be inspectable.

If inspection is punished, constraints are decorative.

---

## Failure modes

### Constraint brittleness

Constraints work until the first real crisis.

Then they vanish.

### Constraint centralization

Constraints are concentrated in one institution.

Capture of that institution ends correction.

### Constraint ritualization

Constraints become rituals.

Rituals can be performed while power ignores them.

This produces legitimacy theater.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve three anti-fragile properties:

1. redundancy (no single point of correction)
2. reversibility (no permanent closure)
3. local survivability (correction pockets can persist)

Without these, constraints will collapse under pressure.

And coercion will become the stabilizer.
