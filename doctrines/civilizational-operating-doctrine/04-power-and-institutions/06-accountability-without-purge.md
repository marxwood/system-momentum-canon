# Accountability Without Purge

Civilizations cannot correct without accountability.

Civilizations cannot survive if accountability becomes purge.

This is the central institutional dilemma.

If misconduct is never punished, capture stabilizes.

If punishment becomes annihilation, fear stabilizes.

Both outcomes destroy correction capacity.

---

## Purge is not accountability

Purge is:

- collective punishment
- guilt by association
- humiliation as governance
- revenge as legitimacy
- fear as stability

Purge produces order.

But it produces an order that cannot correct itself.

Because contradiction becomes suicidal.

---

## Why purge becomes attractive

Purge becomes attractive when:

- institutions are visibly captured
- accountability systems are distrusted
- consequences feel unbearable
- humiliation has accumulated

Purge feels like cleansing.

It is not.

It is capture with new owners.

---

## The doctrine rule

The doctrine rule is:

**Accountability must be ledgered and bounded.**

Ledgered:

- responsibility is traceable
- evidence is inspectable
- consequences are mapped

Bounded:

- punishment is limited
- repair is required
- contradiction is protected

---

## Failure modes

### Immunity collapse

Friends are immune.

Enemies are punished.

This produces capture.

### Revenge substitution

Accountability is replaced by revenge.

Revenge is a stable order.

It is not correction-capable.

### Collective guilt

Accountability spreads by association.

This produces fear and silence.

### Ritual punishment

Punishment becomes a performance.

Performance produces legitimacy theater.

Legitimacy theater produces cynicism.

Cynicism produces apathy.

Apathy produces capture.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve three accountability properties:

1. evidence before punishment
2. bounded consequences (no annihilation logic)
3. repair as requirement, not humiliation as spectacle

Without these, accountability becomes purge.

And purge becomes governance.
