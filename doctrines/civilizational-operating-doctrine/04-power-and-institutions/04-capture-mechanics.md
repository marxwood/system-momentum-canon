# Capture Mechanics

Capture is not corruption.

Capture is not a scandal.

Capture is a stable operating mode where institutions stop serving correction and start serving power.

Captured institutions can look functional.

They can even be efficient.

They are simply efficient at producing obedience.

---

## Capture is structural

Capture is not caused by one bad leader.

It is caused by:

- incentives
- dependence networks
- fear gradients
- and control of punishment

If these conditions exist, capture is not a risk.

It is an attractor.

---

## How capture works

Capture works through a small number of mechanisms.

This doctrine does not moralize them.

It treats them as machinery.

### 1) Dependence capture

Actors become dependent on:

- jobs
- permits
- protection
- access
- resources
- exit paths

Dependence makes contradiction expensive.

### 2) Loyalty capture

Actors are promoted based on loyalty, not competence.

Institutions become machines for loyalty distribution.

### 3) Fear capture

Punishment becomes unpredictable.

Actors learn to pre-comply.

Pre-compliance is the signature of capture.

### 4) Selective enforcement

Rules exist, but are applied asymmetrically.

Friends become immune.
Enemies become targets.

This converts law into factional weaponry.

### 5) Narrative capture

Legitimacy is shifted from evidence to story.

Contradiction becomes “enemy messaging”.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is that capture is misdiagnosed.

Societies often diagnose capture as:

- lack of ideology
- wrong ideology
- insufficient purity
- insufficient loyalty

These diagnoses accelerate capture.

Because they demand closure rather than correction.

---

## Failure modes

### Purity escalation

Society responds to capture by demanding purges.

Purges are capture tools.

They do not restore correction.

### Cynicism stabilization

Society concludes capture is permanent.

This produces apathy reality.

Apathy reality is stable.

It is also capturable.

### Institutional worship

Society treats institutions as sacred.

Sacred institutions cannot be corrected.

They can only be defended or destroyed.

Both outcomes serve capture.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve one discipline:

**Track dependence and punishment pathways.**

If dependence becomes unescapable and punishment becomes asymmetric, capture is already present.
