# Institutions as Machines

Institutions are not moral objects.

They are machines that coordinate action.

They do this by producing:

- permissions
- punishments
- incentives
- and predictable procedures

A society that treats institutions as sacred loses the ability to correct them.

A society that treats institutions as illegitimate by definition loses the ability to use them.

This doctrine treats institutions as machinery.

---

## Why “machine” is the correct abstraction

A machine has:

- inputs
- outputs
- failure modes
- maintenance requirements
- and attack surfaces

Institutions have the same.

They process:

- claims
- conflicts
- resources
- and authority

They output:

- decisions
- enforcement
- legitimacy signals
- and stability

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is not institutional imperfection.

The hazard is institutional capture.

Capture occurs when institutions stop processing reality and start processing loyalty.

A captured institution is still functional.

It is simply functional for power, not for correction.

---

## Machines drift

Machines drift.

Institutions drift through:

- incentive shifts
- personnel replacement
- dependence networks
- fear compression
- procedural erosion

Institutional drift is not always visible.

Often, institutions appear intact while their function is inverted.

---

## Failure modes

### Symbolic institution

The institution exists as a symbol.

The real machine operates elsewhere.

This produces governance by invisible power.

### Punishment inversion

The institution punishes contradiction rather than misconduct.

This produces fear-based compliance.

### Procedure theater

Procedures exist but are selectively applied.

This produces factional immunity and enemy punishment.

### Maintenance collapse

The institution cannot repair itself.

It becomes brittle.

Brittle institutions invite coercive replacement.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve one stance:

**Institutions are machines, therefore they must be inspectable.**

If institutions cannot be inspected, they will be captured.

And captured machines will produce coercion as stability.
