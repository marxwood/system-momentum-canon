# Checks and Constraints

Power expands.

This is not ideology.

It is a structural property of power.

Checks and constraints exist because without them, institutions become machines of unbounded action.

Unbounded action is not strength.

It is the end of correction.

---

## Constraints are not trust

Constraints are not built on trust.

They are built on expectation of failure.

A correction-capable order does not assume:

- leaders are good
- factions are moral
- institutions are stable

It assumes drift.

Constraints exist to survive drift.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is that constraints are treated as optional.

Constraints become optional when:

- emergency logic dominates
- enemies are invoked
- fear becomes governance
- loyalty becomes legitimacy

Optional constraints are not constraints.

They are decoration.

---

## What constraints do

Constraints preserve:

- bounded action
- accountability
- contradiction capacity
- and reform possibility

Without constraints, the system becomes:

- power acting
- narrative justifying
- fear stabilizing

This is capture.

---

## Constraint types

This doctrine does not prescribe specific constitutions.

It defines operational constraint types.

### 1) Procedural constraints

Action must follow declared intent and bounded procedure.

### 2) Oversight constraints

Action must be inspectable by actors who can punish misuse.

### 3) Redundancy constraints

No single organ is allowed to be the only enforcement path.

### 4) Reversibility constraints

Actions must be designed to allow rollback and repair.

### 5) Exit constraints

Actors must have ways to exit dependence without annihilation.

Exit is a constraint on coercion.

---

## Failure modes

### Constraint erosion through emergency

Emergency becomes permanent governance.

Constraints are suspended “temporarily”.

Temporary becomes forever.

### Constraint erosion through selective enforcement

Constraints apply only to enemies.

Friends receive immunity.

This produces capture.

### Constraint erosion through complexity

Constraints exist on paper but are too complex to use.

This produces governance by insiders.

### Constraint erosion through fear

Actors who enforce constraints are punished.

This produces silence equilibria.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve three constraint properties:

1. constraints that survive emergency framing
2. constraints that apply regardless of faction
3. constraints that preserve contradiction capacity

Without these, institutions will produce coercion as stability.
