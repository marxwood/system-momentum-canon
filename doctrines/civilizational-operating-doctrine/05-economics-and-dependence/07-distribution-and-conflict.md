# Distribution and Conflict

Distribution is not only economics.

Distribution is conflict substrate.

When distribution feels unfair, societies do not merely argue.

They polarize.

Polarization shifts conflict primitives from:

- interest conflict
  to
- identity conflict

Identity conflict accelerates violence thresholds.

---

## Distribution is about perception and survival

At civilizational scale, distribution conflict is rarely only about numbers.

It is about:

- dignity
- humiliation
- future security
- and perceived capture

When people believe distribution is captured, legitimacy collapses.

When legitimacy collapses, coercion becomes attractive.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is distribution conflict being processed through:

- humiliation
- scapegoating
- and propaganda

Instead of through:

- bounded repair
- consequence mapping
- and accountability

When distribution conflict becomes identity war, correction becomes impossible.

---

## Why distribution conflict escalates

Distribution conflict escalates when:

- exit paths collapse
- safety nets collapse
- monopoly and lock-in expand
- elites coordinate for immunity
- accountability becomes theater

In this condition, distribution is not a policy debate.

It is a legitimacy crisis.

---

## Failure modes

### Scapegoat distribution

Groups are blamed as parasites.

This produces dehumanization.

Dehumanization accelerates violence.

### Resentment accumulation

Injuries accumulate without repair.

Resentment becomes political fuel.

### Redistribution as humiliation

Repair is framed as humiliation of one side.

Humiliation creates permanent enemies.

Permanent enemies stabilize coercive order.

### Redistribution as capture

Repair mechanisms are captured by elites.

This produces cynicism.

Cynicism produces apathy.

Apathy produces capture.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. repair mechanisms that reduce humiliation
2. safety nets that reduce fear
3. accountability for capture and gaming
4. exit paths that prevent desperation

Without these, distribution conflict becomes a capture engine.
