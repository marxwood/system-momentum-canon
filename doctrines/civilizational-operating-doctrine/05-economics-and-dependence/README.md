# Economics and Dependence

Civilizations do not run on ideas.

They run on constraints.

Economics is the substrate of constraints.

It determines:

- who depends on whom
- who can be punished through deprivation
- who can exit
- who must comply
- who can take risk
- who can refuse

This section treats economics as the civilizational layer where coercion becomes structurally cheap.

---

## The core stance

The stance of this section is:

**Dependence is power.**

Not metaphorically.

Operationally.

If an actor can remove your livelihood, your safety, or your ability to exit, they can govern you without argument.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is not inequality as a moral concept.

The hazard is dependence becoming:

- unescapable
- monopolized
- and weaponizable

When dependence becomes weaponizable, the society does not need ideology to be coercive.

Coercion becomes an economic default.

---

## What this section covers

This section defines:

- dependence maps
- incentive design and gaming
- monopoly and lock-in as coercion
- safety nets as fear reduction substrate
- exit paths as anti-coercion infrastructure
- credit and liquidity fear as capture engines
- distribution conflict as escalation fuel
- trust as economic infrastructure

This is not an economic manifesto.

It is an operational doctrine for correction-capable order.

---

## Failure modes this section defends against

This section exists because economic systems fail in predictable ways:

- dependence becomes permanent
- exit becomes impossible
- monopoly becomes governance
- scarcity is weaponized
- incentives reward pollution and capture
- liquidity shocks trigger fear closure
- distribution conflict becomes identity conflict
- trust collapses into cynicism

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. exit paths that are survivable
2. safety nets that reduce fear
3. constraints on monopoly and lock-in
4. incentives that do not reward epistemic pollution

Without these, coercion becomes economically cheap.

And economically cheap coercion becomes the stabilizer.
