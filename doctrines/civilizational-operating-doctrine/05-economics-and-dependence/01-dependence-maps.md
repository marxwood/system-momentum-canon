# Dependence Maps

Power is often misread as ideology.

At civilizational scale, power is usually dependence.

Dependence is the ability to govern through deprivation.

If you can remove:

- income
- housing
- food
- healthcare
- legal safety
- mobility
- or access to markets

you can enforce compliance without argument.

This is why dependence maps are as important as power maps.

---

## What a dependence map is

A dependence map answers:

- who depends on whom
- for what
- under what conditions
- with what exit options
- and with what punishment pathways

Dependence maps are not moral.

They are operational.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is dependence becoming invisible.

When dependence is invisible:

- coercion is misdiagnosed as “consent”
- compliance is misdiagnosed as “agreement”
- fear is misdiagnosed as “culture”
- capture is misdiagnosed as “stability”

Invisible dependence is one of the most stable forms of power.

---

## Dependence layers

Dependence is layered.

A society can have:

- formal freedoms
- and total dependence

Dependence layers include:

- employment dependence
- housing dependence
- debt dependence
- licensing and permit dependence
- supply-chain dependence
- platform dependence
- institutional access dependence
- protection dependence

The more layers overlap, the more coercion becomes cheap.

---

## Failure modes

### Single-point dependence

A small number of actors control a critical resource.

This produces monopoly coercion.

### Dependency cascades

Losing one resource causes loss of others.

This makes dissent suicidal.

### Dependence disguised as merit

Dependence is framed as deserved outcome.

This prevents correction.

### Dependence weaponization

Dependence is used explicitly to punish contradiction.

This produces fear governance.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve one discipline:

**Always ask: who can starve whom?**

Not only through food.

Through livelihood and exit.

If this question cannot be asked safely, the order is already coercive.
