# Belief Update Protocols

Civilizations do not update beliefs the way individuals imagine.

They do not “learn” as a single mind.

They update through:

- institutions
- media systems
- credibility hierarchies
- social permission
- punishment and reward
- fear gradients

This is why truth alone is insufficient.

A claim can be true and still fail to update society.

A claim can be false and still become coordination.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is not that people believe false things.

The hazard is that belief update becomes:

- humiliating
- dangerous
- identity-threatening
- socially illegal

When belief update becomes costly, societies freeze.

When societies freeze, they cannot correct.

When they cannot correct, coercion becomes the stabilizer.

---

## Belief update is not persuasion

Most belief updates at scale are not caused by persuasion.

They are caused by:

- changes in incentives
- changes in legitimacy
- changes in credibility signals
- changes in fear
- changes in what can be publicly said

This is not cynicism.

It is operational realism.

---

## The doctrine rule

The doctrine rule is:

**Treat belief update as a system function, not a moral event.**

A correction-capable order must preserve belief update pathways.

Not by demanding virtue.

By maintaining conditions under which updating is survivable.

---

## The three layers of belief update

### 1) Private belief

What actors privately think.

Private belief can be sophisticated and still irrelevant.

### 2) Public belief

What actors can safely say.

Public belief determines coordination.

### 3) Institutional belief

What institutions encode in:

- law
- policy
- education
- enforcement
- media framing

Institutional belief determines long-term reality.

A society collapses when these three layers become permanently separated.

---

## Failure modes

### Humiliation lock

Updating becomes an admission of inferiority.

Actors defend false beliefs because the social cost of updating is higher than the cost of being wrong.

### Identity lock

Updating is treated as betrayal of tribe.

Actors defend false beliefs because updating threatens belonging.

### Fear lock

Updating is punished.

Actors defend false beliefs because correction is dangerous.

### Cynicism lock

Actors stop believing that truth matters at all.

This produces apathy reality.

Apathy reality is stable.

It is also capturable.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable society must preserve:

1. safe paths to revise beliefs
2. credibility systems that can be corrected
3. disagreement without annihilation

Belief update does not require universal agreement.

It requires correction capacity to remain socially possible.
