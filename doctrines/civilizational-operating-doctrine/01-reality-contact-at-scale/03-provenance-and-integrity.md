# Provenance and Integrity

At civilizational scale, evidence is not scarce.

Integrity is scarce.

Most societies do not collapse because they lack facts.

They collapse because they lose the ability to trust anchors.

When anchors become untrustable, coordination shifts to:

- tribe
- regime
- cult
- fear
- apathy

These are stable orders.

But they are not correction-capable.

---

## Provenance is the spine of reality contact

Provenance is not a luxury.

It is the spine of correction capacity.

Without provenance:

- evidence cannot be compared
- claims cannot be audited
- responsibility cannot be assigned
- belief updates cannot be justified

A society without provenance becomes a society of:

- rumors
- performances
- punishments

---

## What provenance is

Provenance is the chain that answers:

- who produced the claim
- how it was produced
- what was observed vs inferred
- what was modified
- who touched it
- what was omitted
- what incentives were present

This is not about perfection.

It is about traceability.

---

## Integrity is not truth

Integrity is not the same as truth.

Integrity means:

- the artifact is what it claims to be
- the source is what it claims to be
- the record has not been silently altered
- the context has not been erased

A society can be wrong with integrity.

It can correct.

A society can be “right” without integrity.

It cannot correct.

---

## Why integrity collapses

Integrity collapses for predictable reasons:

### 1) Incentives reward distortion

Actors gain status and power by:

- exaggerating
- omitting
- reframing
- weaponizing ambiguity

### 2) Complexity blocks inspection

Systems become too complex for most actors to verify.

This creates a dependence on:

- authority
- interpreters
- gatekeepers

### 3) Fear makes inspection dangerous

When inspection is punished, correction dies.

### 4) Speed outcompetes verification

Interpretation travels faster than inspection.

This is not an accident.

It is a structural property of scale.

---

## The capture vector

Provenance is the first thing captured in a coercive order.

Not because it is morally bad.

Because it is operationally dangerous to power.

Power prefers:

- claims without sources
- sources without accountability
- accountability without evidence
- evidence without inspectability

---

## Failure modes

### Anonymous authority

Claims circulate without responsibility.

This is how rumor becomes governance.

### Selective provenance

Provenance exists only for enemies.

Friends get narrative immunity.

This produces factional reality.

### Context stripping

Artifacts are cut from their conditions.

This produces outrage engines and humiliation rituals.

### Integrity collapse through repetition

Falsehood becomes stable through repetition, not through persuasion.

Repetition is not evidence.

But it becomes coordination.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve three things:

1. the ability to attribute claims
2. the ability to inspect artifacts
3. the ability to contest without annihilation

This is not an epistemic ideal.

It is the minimum survival requirement for non-coercive coordination.
