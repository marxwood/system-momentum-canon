# Negotiation Under Hostility

Negotiation is not harmony.

Negotiation is the operational alternative to coercion.

Civilizations negotiate even when they hate each other.

The question is whether negotiation is possible without collapse into:

- humiliation
- propaganda
- and retaliation loops

---

## Negotiation is not moral agreement

Negotiation does not require shared values.

It requires:

- bounded action
- inspectable commitments
- and survivable exits

When negotiation is framed as moral agreement, it fails.

Because moral agreement is not achievable under hostility.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is negotiation being replaced by:

- loyalty tests
- propaganda performances
- and symbolic surrender rituals

These are not negotiation.

They are dominance theater.

Dominance theater produces short-term closure.

It produces long-term escalation.

---

## What negotiation requires

Negotiation requires conditions.

This doctrine defines operational conditions.

### 1) Commitment visibility

Actors must be able to see whether commitments are kept.

Without this, negotiation becomes narrative.

### 2) Exit paths

Actors must be able to leave without annihilation.

Without exit paths, negotiation becomes coercion.

### 3) Bounded demands

Demands must be bounded.

Unbounded demands are humiliation disguised as negotiation.

### 4) Repair mechanisms

Injuries must be repairable.

If injuries are not repairable, resentment becomes permanent fuel.

### 5) Non-humiliation posture

Negotiation must not require humiliation.

Humiliation is retaliation fuel.

---

## Failure modes

### Negotiation as surrender

One side demands symbolic submission.

Submission produces resentment.

Resentment produces future conflict.

### Negotiation as delay tactic

Negotiation is used to reorganize power.

This destroys trust in negotiation.

### Negotiation without power maps

Negotiation ignores punishment asymmetries.

This makes agreements unstable.

### Negotiation without consequence mapping

Negotiation commits to actions that normalize coercion.

This produces capture through agreement.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. negotiation legitimacy even under hostility
2. agreements that are inspectable
3. boundaries that survive conflict

If negotiation becomes illegitimate, coercion becomes the only coordination tool.
