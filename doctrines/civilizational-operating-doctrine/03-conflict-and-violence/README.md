# Conflict and Violence

Conflict is normal.

It is not a moral failure.

It is the default condition of scale.

Civilizations contain:

- competing interests
- competing identities
- competing legitimacy claims
- competing power coalitions

This section does not attempt to eliminate conflict.

It defines how conflict becomes:

- bounded
- survivable
- and non-capturable

---

## Violence is a threshold crossing

Violence is not only physical harm.

Violence is the threshold where:

- correction becomes impossible
- fear becomes governance
- and coercion becomes normal

Violence can stabilize order.

But it stabilizes through closure.

Closure destroys correction capacity.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is not conflict.

The hazard is conflict being used as:

- a capture engine
- a boundary eraser
- a legitimacy weapon
- and a permission slip for coercion

This is how societies drift into coercive orders while believing they are defending themselves.

---

## What this section covers

This section defines:

- conflict primitives
- violence thresholds
- retaliation loops
- de-escalation protocols
- negotiation under hostility
- non-violent power
- coercion thresholds
- capture via conflict

This is not pacifism.

It is not war theory.

It is the operational doctrine for preserving correction capacity under conflict.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. de-escalation as a legitimate act
2. boundaries that survive conflict
3. accountability that does not become revenge

If these fail, conflict will be resolved by coercive closure.
