# Violence Thresholds

Violence is not a binary.

Violence is a gradient.

Societies rarely wake up in violence.

They slide into it through threshold crossings.

Each crossing becomes a precedent.

Precedent becomes permission.

Permission becomes normalization.

Normalization becomes order.

---

## Violence is broader than physical harm

Physical violence is the clearest form.

But civilizational violence includes:

- deprivation as punishment
- coercion through dependence
- humiliation as governance
- selective enforcement
- disappearance of safe contradiction

These forms are often treated as “not violence”.

They are violence because they destroy correction capacity.

---

## Why thresholds matter

Thresholds matter because:

- once crossed, they are difficult to uncross
- actors adapt to the new normal
- institutions reconfigure around enforcement
- fear becomes coordination infrastructure

A correction-capable order must treat thresholds as irreversible risks.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is violence being reframed as:

- necessity
- justice
- defense
- cleansing
- destiny

These frames may contain partial truth.

But they also function as permission engines.

They erase boundaries.

---

## Common threshold crossings

This doctrine does not list historical examples.

It lists structural crossings.

### 1) Permission to humiliate publicly

Humiliation becomes enforcement.

### 2) Permission to punish by association

Collective guilt becomes normal.

### 3) Permission to silence contradiction

Contradiction becomes dangerous.

### 4) Permission to treat opponents as non-persons

Dehumanization becomes legitimate.

### 5) Permission to remove exit paths

Dependence becomes unescapable.

### 6) Permission to use fear as default governance

Fear becomes infrastructure.

Each crossing reduces correction capacity.

---

## Failure modes

### Threshold blindness

Society believes violence begins only when blood is visible.

By the time blood is visible, correction is already dead.

### Threshold acceleration

Each side justifies escalation as response.

Retaliation loops become inevitable.

### Threshold normalization

People adapt.

They stop noticing.

This is how coercive order becomes normal.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve one discipline:

**Treat threshold crossings as the primary risk, not “winning”.**

If the system rewards escalation, violence becomes the stabilizer.
