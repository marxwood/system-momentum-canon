# Non-Violent Power

Civilizations do not choose between violence and peace.

They choose between:

- violent coercion
- and non-violent power

Non-violent power is not weakness.

It is the ability to impose constraints without crossing violence thresholds.

This is how correction capacity survives conflict.

---

## Power does not disappear

Power is always present.

If a society pretends power can be removed, it becomes blind.

Blind societies do not become peaceful.

They become capturable.

Non-violent power is the attempt to keep power bounded.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is the belief that:

- only violence is real power
- only domination produces stability
- only humiliation produces deterrence

This belief is a capture engine.

It makes coercion feel inevitable.

---

## Forms of non-violent power

This doctrine does not prescribe tactics.

It defines structural forms.

### 1) Economic constraint

The ability to deny resources without annihilation.

### 2) Institutional constraint

The ability to enforce boundaries through procedures.

### 3) Social constraint

The ability to punish misconduct without humiliation rituals.

### 4) Narrative constraint

The ability to resist propaganda without becoming propaganda.

### 5) Exit constraint

The ability to leave dependence networks without destruction.

Exit is a power form.

It is also a coercion limiter.

---

## Failure modes

### Non-violent power collapse into humiliation

Social constraint becomes shaming.

Shaming escalates conflict.

### Non-violent power collapse into monopoly

Constraints are centralized.

Centralization becomes capturable.

### Non-violent power collapse into moral licensing

Actors believe their intent sanctifies their constraints.

This erases boundaries.

### Non-violent power collapse into impotence

Constraints cannot be enforced.

This produces longing for violence.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. constraint mechanisms that do not require violence
2. accountability ledgers for constraint use
3. boundaries that prevent constraint escalation into annihilation

If non-violent power fails, violence becomes the stabilizer.
