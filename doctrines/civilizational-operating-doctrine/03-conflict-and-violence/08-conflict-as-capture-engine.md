# Conflict as a Capture Engine

Conflict is not only a threat.

Conflict is also a tool.

Captured orders use conflict to:

- erase boundaries
- suspend constraints
- disable accountability
- and justify coercion

This is why conflict is one of the most reliable capture engines.

---

## Why conflict enables capture

Conflict enables capture because it changes what feels legitimate.

Under conflict:

- intent declaration is skipped
- action boundaries feel intolerable
- consequence mapping feels slow
- contradiction feels dangerous
- accountability feels like sabotage

This is not irrational.

It is the psychology of survival under uncertainty.

Capture exploits it.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is not conflict.

The hazard is conflict being cultivated as permanent condition.

Permanent conflict produces:

- permanent emergency
- permanent loyalty tests
- permanent enemy inflation
- permanent suspension of constraints

This is how coercive orders become stable.

---

## Capture patterns through conflict

This doctrine defines common patterns.

### 1) Emergency normalization

Crisis measures become default governance.

### 2) Enemy inflation

The enemy category expands until contradiction itself is enemy behavior.

### 3) Constraint suspension

Checks and constraints are framed as weakness.

### 4) Accountability inversion

Misconduct is protected if it serves the conflict.

### 5) Purge justification

Purges are framed as defense.

Purges destroy correction capacity.

---

## Failure modes

### Endless mobilization

Society becomes permanently mobilized.

Mobilization consumes resources and attention.

Exhaustion produces longing for closure.

Closure is supplied by coercion.

### Internal enemy shift

Conflict shifts inward.

Opponents become traitors.

This produces civilizational fragmentation.

### Capture through fatigue

People accept coercion to end exhaustion.

This is one of the most common endings.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. constraints that survive conflict framing
2. legitimacy for de-escalation
3. accountability that does not become purge

If conflict suspends correction capacity, conflict will produce capture.
