# Conflict Primitives

Most civilizational conflict is not one thing.

It is a mixture of different conflict primitives.

A society collapses when it misclassifies the conflict it is in.

Misclassification produces wrong tools.

Wrong tools produce escalation.

Escalation produces coercion.

---

## The core conflict primitives

This doctrine does not classify conflicts by ideology.

It classifies them by operational structure.

### 1) Interest conflict

Actors want incompatible outcomes.

This is the most negotiable form.

### 2) Resource conflict

Actors compete for scarce resources.

This can be negotiated if scarcity is managed.

If scarcity is weaponized, coercion becomes likely.

### 3) Power conflict

Actors compete over who can punish whom.

This conflict is often hidden.

It is rarely resolved by argument.

### 4) Legitimacy conflict

Actors disagree on what actions are permitted.

This conflict becomes dangerous when legitimacy becomes identity.

### 5) Identity conflict

Actors treat the conflict as:

- existential
- sacred
- and non-negotiable

Identity conflict is the fastest path to violence.

### 6) Narrative conflict

Actors fight over the control surface of meaning.

Narrative conflict can be resolved without violence.

But it is often used to justify violence.

---

## Why primitives matter

Different primitives require different constraints.

If you treat interest conflict as identity conflict, you escalate.

If you treat identity conflict as interest conflict, you are naive.

A correction-capable order must preserve the ability to distinguish primitives.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is conflict collapse into a single primitive:

**enemy conflict.**

When all conflict is classified as enemy conflict:

- contradiction becomes treason
- boundaries become betrayal
- accountability becomes purge
- violence becomes attractive

Enemy conflict is a capture engine.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve one discipline:

**Name the conflict primitive before acting.**

If the primitive cannot be named safely, conflict is already being used as a capture tool.
