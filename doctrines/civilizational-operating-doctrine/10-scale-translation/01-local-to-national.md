# Local to National

Local coordination is often real.

National coordination is often symbolic.

The transition from local to national is where legitimacy frequently becomes theater.

This module defines what breaks when mechanisms are scaled.

---

## Why scaling breaks

Local systems scale poorly because:

- trust is local
- oversight is personal
- accountability is visible
- and consequences are felt directly

National systems invert these properties.

At national scale:

- trust becomes mediated
- oversight becomes abstract
- accountability becomes delayed
- and consequences become disputable

This makes capture easier.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is national systems pretending to be local.

This produces:

- credibility theater
- false participation
- and legitimacy simulation

Simulation stabilizes.

Simulation also hides capture.

---

## Failure modes

### Oversight collapse

Oversight mechanisms that work locally cannot inspect national power.

National power becomes immune.

### Accountability delay

Accountability becomes too slow to matter.

This produces cynicism.

Cynicism produces apathy.

Apathy produces capture.

### Participation theater

Participation becomes ritual.

People stop believing correction is possible.

### Identity substitution

Identity becomes the shortcut for national coordination.

This produces tribal legitimacy.

Tribal legitimacy erases evidence.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable national order must preserve:

1. distributed oversight that scales beyond personal trust
2. accountability ledgers that remain inspectable
3. exit paths that prevent dependence monopoly
4. evidence primitives that travel across identity boundaries

If local mechanisms cannot translate, national order will stabilize through coercion.
