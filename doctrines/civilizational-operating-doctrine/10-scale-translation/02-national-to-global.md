# National to Global

Global coordination is not national coordination at higher scale.

It is coordination between entities that:

- do not share legitimacy
- do not share oversight
- and do not share enforcement

This makes global order structurally fragile.

Fragile order often stabilizes through coercion.

Not necessarily military coercion.

Economic and narrative coercion are often enough.

---

## Global coordination is dependence coordination

At global scale, power is mostly exercised through:

- dependence
- exit denial
- and asymmetric punishment

Global coordination is therefore inseparable from economics.

This is why global legitimacy debates often fail.

They are not legitimacy debates.

They are dependence negotiations.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is global order being framed as:

- universal morality
- ideological destiny
- or civilizational superiority

These framings produce humiliation.

Humiliation produces backlash.

Backlash produces identity hardening.

Identity hardening produces conflict.

Conflict produces coercive closure.

---

## Failure modes

### Global legitimacy theater

Institutions exist without enforcement.

They become performance objects.

Performance produces cynicism.

### Dependence weaponization

Trade, energy, and finance become punishment tools.

This produces fear and pre-compliance.

### Narrative imperialism

One narrative is exported as universal.

This produces humiliation and resistance.

### Exit denial between states

States cannot exit dependence networks.

This produces permanent conflict.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable global order must preserve:

1. dependence maps that are visible
2. exit paths that reduce punishment monopoly
3. translation discipline that avoids humiliation
4. bounded agreements rather than universalist replacement

If global coordination cannot preserve exit, it will stabilize through coercion.
