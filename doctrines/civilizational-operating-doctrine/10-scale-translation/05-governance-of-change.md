# Governance of Change

Doctrines drift.

Drift is inevitable.

The problem is not drift.

The problem is drift becoming capture.

This module defines how doctrine evolves without turning into:

- ideology
- propaganda
- or institutional theater

---

## Change is a power surface

Every change process creates:

- gatekeepers
- legitimacy claims
- and exclusion mechanics

If change governance is not explicit, it will be captured.

Captured change governance produces:

- stagnation
- cynicism
- and eventual coercive stabilization

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is doctrine governance becoming:

- a priesthood
- a faction
- or a brand

Brands need loyalty.

Loyalty destroys contradiction.

Priesthoods need immunity.

Immunity produces capture.

---

## What governance of change requires

Governance of change requires:

- contradiction as core function
- provenance and integrity of edits
- explicit non-goals and boundaries
- mechanisms-first translation discipline
- anti-rationalization discipline

This is not “community management”.

It is correction preservation.

---

## Failure modes

### Priesthood governance

Only insiders can change doctrine.

This produces capture.

### Popularity governance

Change follows attention.

Attention is capturable.

### Fear governance

Change is avoided to prevent conflict.

Avoidance produces stagnation.

Stagnation produces collapse panic.

### Ideology drift

Doctrine becomes belief system.

Belief systems require enemies.

Enemies stabilize coercion.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable doctrine evolution process must preserve:

1. inspectable provenance of changes
2. contradiction capacity against insiders
3. explicit anti-ideology guardrails
4. translation discipline across contexts

If doctrine cannot be changed safely, it becomes ideology.

If it becomes ideology, it becomes capture.
