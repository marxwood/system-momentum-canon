# Intent Declaration

At civilizational scale, most harm is not caused by lack of capability.

It is caused by action without declared intent.

When intent is not declared, action is justified after the fact.

After-the-fact justification is the engine of coercion.

---

## Intent is not motivation

Intent is not emotion.

Intent is not moral posture.

Intent is not a story.

Intent is a declaration of:

- what action is being taken
- what outcome is being pursued
- what constraints apply
- what accountability is accepted

Intent is the precondition for legitimate action.

---

## Why intent declaration matters

Without intent declaration:

- actions cannot be bounded
- consequences cannot be mapped
- accountability cannot be assigned
- repair cannot be demanded

The system becomes:

- power acting
- narrative justifying
- fear stabilizing

This is capture.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is that intent is replaced by:

- slogans
- moral panic
- enemy labeling
- emergency framing
- historical destiny narratives

These substitutes feel like intent.

They are not.

They are permission engines.

---

## Intent as an anti-rationalization tool

Declared intent prevents the most common coercive pattern:

1. act
2. deny
3. justify
4. escalate
5. punish contradiction

When intent is declared before action, the system can ask:

- “Did you do what you said?”
- “Did you exceed bounds?”
- “Did you accept consequences?”
- “Did you map second-order effects?”

This is how correction survives.

---

## Failure modes

### Implicit intent

Intent is assumed through identity.

“If you are one of us, your intent is good.”

This produces immunity and corruption.

### Retrospective intent

Intent is invented after action.

This produces permanent rationalization.

### Sacred intent

Intent is treated as unquestionable.

This produces unbounded coercion.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve one discipline:

**No action without declared intent.**

This does not prevent harm.

But it makes harm accountable.

And accountability is the only alternative to fear.
