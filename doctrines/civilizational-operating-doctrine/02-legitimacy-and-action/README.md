# Legitimacy and Action

At civilizational scale, the central question is not:

“What is true?”

The central question is:

**“What actions become permitted?”**

Legitimacy is the permission layer for action.

It determines:

- what can be done
- by whom
- against whom
- with what justification
- with what consequences
- with what repair

A society can survive disagreement.

It cannot survive unbounded action.

---

## Legitimacy is not truth

Legitimacy is not truth.

Legitimacy is a coordination interface.

It stabilizes action by making action feel:

- justified
- normal
- required
- inevitable

This is why legitimacy is a primary target of capture.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is not that legitimacy exists.

Legitimacy must exist.

The hazard is when legitimacy becomes:

- detached from evidence primitives
- detached from accountability
- detached from consequence mapping
- detached from repair

When this happens, legitimacy becomes a license.

And licenses become coercion.

---

## What this section covers

This section defines how action becomes:

- permitted
- bounded
- accountable
- repairable

It does not do this through ideology.

It does it through operational constraints.

The modules cover:

- intent declaration as precondition
- action boundaries
- accountability ledgers
- consequence mapping
- decision under conflict
- anti-rationalization discipline

---

## The stance

The stance of this section is:

**Legitimacy must preserve correction capacity.**

Not by moral purity.

By maintaining:

- bounded action
- inspectable accountability
- repair without purge
- and constraints on coercion

---

## Failure modes this section defends against

This section exists because civilizational legitimacy fails in predictable ways:

- action becomes unbounded under “emergency”
- accountability becomes revenge
- consequence mapping collapses into slogans
- rationalization becomes a weapon
- legitimacy becomes identity
- correction becomes treason

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve:

1. declared intent before action
2. boundaries on what can be done
3. accountability without purge
4. consequence mapping beyond slogans
5. repair mechanisms that do not require annihilation

Without these, legitimacy becomes a tool for coercion.

And coercion becomes the stabilizer.
