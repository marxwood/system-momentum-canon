# Decision Under Conflict

Civilizations do not make decisions in calm conditions.

They make decisions under conflict.

Conflict compresses attention.

It compresses nuance.

It compresses tolerance.

It creates a demand for closure.

This is the condition under which coercion becomes attractive.

---

## Conflict changes what feels legitimate

Under conflict, societies reclassify actions.

Actions that were unthinkable become:

- necessary
- justified
- demanded

This is not because people become evil.

It is because conflict makes bounded action feel insufficient.

Boundaries feel like weakness.

Contradiction feels like betrayal.

Uncertainty feels like danger.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is not conflict itself.

Conflict is normal.

The hazard is conflict becoming the mechanism that:

- removes boundaries
- disables contradiction
- collapses belief update
- grants legitimacy to coercion

This is how captured orders are manufactured.

---

## Decision is not choice

Under conflict, decision is not choice.

Decision becomes:

- a public loyalty signal
- a test of belonging
- a performance of certainty

This shifts decision-making away from:

- evidence primitives
- consequence mapping
- repair mechanisms

and toward:

- identity
- humiliation
- dominance

---

## The doctrine rule

The doctrine rule is:

**Conflict must not be allowed to suspend correction capacity.**

Not because correction is morally good.

Because without correction, conflict has only one exit:

coercive closure.

---

## Failure modes

### Closure escalation

Conflict creates demand for immediate closure.

Closure is supplied by:

- censorship
- purges
- emergency powers
- violence normalization

### Loyalty substitution

Decision becomes a loyalty ritual.

This destroys accountability ledgers.

### Enemy inflation

The enemy category expands.

Everything becomes “enemy behavior”.

This makes boundaries impossible.

### Permanent emergency

Conflict becomes permanent governance.

Permanent emergency is capture.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve three disciplines under conflict:

1. intent must still be declared
2. boundaries must still apply
3. accountability must still be ledgered

If these fail under conflict, they are not real.

They are decoration.

And coercion becomes the stabilizer.
