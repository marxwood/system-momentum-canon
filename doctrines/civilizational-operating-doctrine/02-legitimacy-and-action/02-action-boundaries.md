# Action Boundaries

Intent declaration is meaningless without boundaries.

Boundaries are what prevent legitimacy from becoming a blank check.

A society can tolerate many competing intents.

It cannot tolerate unbounded action.

---

## Boundaries are not moral purity

Boundaries are not virtue.

They are constraints that preserve correction capacity.

They exist because:

- power expands
- fear justifies excess
- emergencies become permanent
- rationalization is unlimited

Boundaries are the only defense against legitimacy becoming coercion.

---

## The civilizational hazard

The hazard is that boundaries are replaced by:

- emergency logic
- enemy logic
- purity logic
- destiny logic

These logics have one property in common:

They make boundaries feel like betrayal.

This is how coercion becomes normal.

---

## What action boundaries do

Action boundaries create:

- predictability
- reversibility
- inspectability
- and survivable dissent

They prevent the coordination system from collapsing into:

- brute force
- humiliation
- and revenge

---

## Boundary types

This doctrine does not prescribe specific laws.

It defines operational boundary types.

### 1) Prohibited actions

Actions that cannot be justified by outcome claims.

These are actions that destroy correction capacity itself.

### 2) Bounded actions

Actions allowed only under explicit constraints:

- time limits
- scope limits
- oversight limits
- evidence requirements

### 3) Reversible actions

Actions designed to allow repair and rollback.

A correction-capable order must preserve reversibility.

### 4) Accountability-bound actions

Actions that require pre-committed accountability mechanisms.

Not after-the-fact.

Pre-committed.

---

## Failure modes

### Boundary erosion through emergency

“Temporary” measures become permanent.

Emergency becomes a governance style.

### Boundary erosion through identity

Boundaries apply only to enemies.

Friends receive immunity.

This produces capture.

### Boundary erosion through moral licensing

Actors believe their intent is sacred.

Sacred intent is how boundaries die.

---

## Minimal implementation

A correction-capable order must preserve three boundary properties:

1. constraints that do not depend on who is acting
2. constraints that survive emergency framing
3. constraints that preserve contradiction capacity

Without these, legitimacy becomes a license.

And license becomes coercion.
