# Cognitive / Reasoning Domain — Doctrinal View

This document explains the Cognitive / Reasoning Domain
from a human and practical perspective.

It clarifies what cognition does, what it must never do,
and why its **ephemeral nature** is essential for system integrity.

---

## 1. What Cognition Is

Cognition is the domain where the system **thinks**.

It operates over:

- percepts
- contextual references
- hypothetical structures

Cognition is where:

- patterns are formed
- possibilities are explored
- candidates are generated

Cognition does **not** decide.
It **prepares**.

---

## 2. Ephemeral by Design

Cognition is intentionally **non-persistent**.

Its products:

- hypotheses
- interpretations
- simulations
- candidate actions

exist only long enough to be evaluated or acted upon.

This ephemerality is not a limitation.
It is a safeguard.

If cognition were persistent by default,
the system would accumulate speculation as memory.

---

## 3. What Cognition Must Not Become

Cognition must never be treated as:

- knowledge
- authority
- commitment
- memory
- truth

When reasoning artifacts are stored as facts,
systems begin to **believe their own thoughts**.

This is one of the most common sources of institutional delusion.

---

## 4. Relationship to LLMs and Simulations

Large language models and simulations
belong **naturally** in the Cognitive Domain.

They are:

- generative
- probabilistic
- speculative

Their outputs are **useful**
precisely because they are **not authoritative**.

Treating LLM output as knowledge
is a category error.

Cognition may generate insight.
Only other domains may assign meaning, validity, or commitment.

---

## 5. Boundary Toward Knowledge and Semantics

Cognition may **consult** semantic authority,
but it may not redefine it.

Meaning flows:

- from Knowledge / Semantic Authority
- into cognition
- never the other way around

If cognition is allowed to invent meaning,
semantic drift becomes inevitable.

---

## 6. Boundary Toward Action

Cognition may propose actions.
It may not perform them.

The moment a proposal becomes a commitment,
cognition must exit.

This boundary protects the system
from acting on unexamined reasoning.

---

## 7. Non-Linear Reasoning

Cognition is not a single chain of thought.

It may:

- branch
- backtrack
- contradict itself
- run in parallel

Contradiction at this stage is healthy.

Suppressing contradiction too early
forces premature certainty.

---

## 8. Typical Failure Modes

Common cognitive failures include:

- persisting reasoning outputs
- treating confidence as correctness
- collapsing speculation into belief
- optimizing for coherence instead of truth
- skipping evaluation due to urgency

Most systems fail not because they reason poorly,
but because they **trust reasoning too much**.

---

## 9. Practical Examples

Examples of cognition include:

- drafting hypotheses
- simulating outcomes
- generating options
- interpreting ambiguous input

None of these are final.
All of them are disposable.

---

## Closing Note

Cognition is where creativity lives.

But creativity without discipline
quickly becomes fiction.

A healthy system allows cognition to be bold,
precisely because it is never final.
