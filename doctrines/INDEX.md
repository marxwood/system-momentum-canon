# Doctrines

Doctrines define how an actor operates under reality.

They are not Canon.

They do not define global entities.
They do not define invariants.
They do not define truth.

Doctrines exist to make action survivable.

They provide:

- operational constraints
- rules
- threat models
- failure modes
- checklists
- coordination protocols

Doctrines are written for three primary operating scales:

- Personal
- Organizational
- Civilizational

---

## Branches

### 1) Personal Operating Doctrine (v0.1)

Path:

`doctrines/personal-operating-doctrine/`

Purpose:

To define how a single actor, together with an agentic layer, maintains:

- stance
- reality contact
- bounded intent
- sustainable execution
- communication discipline
- render-based life architecture

This branch is explicitly non-metaphysical.

It avoids:

- spirituality as truth
- life coaching language
- philosophical ontologies

It remains procedural and operational.

---

### 2) Organizational Operating Doctrine (v0.1)

Path:

`doctrines/organizational-operating-doctrine/`

Purpose:

To define how organizations operate as agentic systems, including:

- roles, responsibility, and accountability
- intent → execution loops
- knowledge, memory, and provenance
- governance, control, and evaluation
- culture and incentives
- coordination at scale
- interfaces with markets and regulation

This branch avoids:

- vendor-specific implementations
- technology prescriptions
- motivational essays

It remains an operating doctrine.

---

### 3) Civilizational Operating Doctrine (v0.1)

Path:

`doctrines/civilizational-operating-doctrine/`

Purpose:

To define how large-scale coordination survives under conditions of:

- legitimacy conflict
- power concentration
- institutional capture
- narrative warfare
- fear-based obedience
- corruption as stabilizer
- collective action failure

This branch treats order as a **coordination outcome**, not a moral slogan.

---

## Cross-Branch Discipline

Doctrines are allowed to differ by branch.

However, they must remain compatible through:

- translation protocols
- boundary discipline
- drift detection

The most common failure mode is cross-scale contamination:

- personal posture incorrectly treated as civilizational policy
- civilizational threat-model incorrectly treated as organizational paranoia
- organizational operating doctrine incorrectly treated as personal identity

---

## Doctrine Quality Requirements

Every doctrine must contain at least one of the following:

- constraints
- operational rules
- failure modes
- checklists

If a text cannot produce operational checks, it is not a doctrine.

---

## Canon Boundary

Doctrines must not:

- introduce canonical entities
- introduce canonical invariants
- define truth primitives as ontology
- prescribe implementation technologies

Doctrines may reference Canon concepts, but must not mutate them.
