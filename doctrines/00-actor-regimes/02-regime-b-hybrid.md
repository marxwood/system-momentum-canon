# Regime B — Hybrid (bounded autonomous agents)

In this regime, agents can execute work with bounded autonomy.

Humans may not be present in every step, but final legitimacy and liability still resolve to:

- a human
- an organization
- or an institution that is human-governed

This regime is operational today.

---

## What changes vs Regime A

Agents can be allowed to:

- schedule
- negotiate low-risk interfaces
- operate task pools
- maintain ledgers
- perform routine enforcement of constraints

But the “final bearer” remains non-agentic.

---

## Why this still isn’t agent-final

Because sovereignty does not sit with the agent:

- resources are still owned externally (accounts, keys, money, infrastructure)
- enforcement ultimately resolves to human institutions (law, contracts, organizational authority)
- sanctioning of the agent is indirect (disable, revoke access, terminate compute)

Hybrid is “autonomy at runtime”, not “sovereignty in reality”.

---

## Reading rule

When doctrine says “human override”, in Hybrid read it as:

- oversight protocol
- escalation boundary
- revocation mechanism

You are translating the surface of control, not removing control.
