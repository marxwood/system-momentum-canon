# Translation Rules Across Regimes

These rules allow reading the same doctrine modules without rewriting them.

They are not philosophy.

They are mechanical substitutions that preserve operational meaning.

---

## A → B (Human-final → Hybrid)

Replace:

- “human override” → “oversight protocol + revocation boundary”
- “personal responsibility” → “accountability interface (who signs, who owns liability)”
- “trust” → “trust + auditability (trust is still present, but anchored by trace)”
- “repair” → “repair actions + rollback + containment (explicit artifacts)”
- “refusal” → “refusal + escalation (agent can refuse; human/org resolves)”

Keep:

- provenance discipline
- intent declaration
- bounded commitments
- auditability
- anti-humiliation constraints

These remain valid and become more important.

---

## A/B → C (Human-final / Hybrid → Agent-final)

Do not translate by word substitution alone.

Regime C requires explicit primitives:

- legitimacy issuance/revocation
- enforcement mechanisms
- coercion primitives

Until those exist, any “agent-final” reading is narrative, not doctrine.

---

## Mechanism-first constraint

If you cannot specify:

- who can block actions/resources
- by what evidence
- via what enforcement channel

then you are not in Regime C.

You are in Hybrid with high autonomy and low visibility.
