# Embodiment Regimes

This file defines embodiment as a parallel axis to actor regimes (A/B/C).

Actor regimes define who is a final legitimacy / accountability bearer.
Embodiment regimes define what coercion and enforcement channels are physically available.

Embodiment does not grant sovereignty.
Embodiment changes the failure surface.

---

## E0 — Disembodied (purely digital)

Definition:

- The actor exists only as software processes and data.

Coercion / enforcement channels:

- access revocation
- compute throttling
- network isolation
- credential rotation
- storage deletion / quarantine

Distinct hazards:

- low friction replication (copying)
- unclear containment boundaries
- dependency on external infrastructure owners

---

## E1 — Mechanically embodied

Definition:

- The actor controls a physical machine body (robotics, devices).

Coercion / enforcement channels:

- physical access restriction
- energy cutoff
- hardware disablement
- geofencing / movement constraints
- physical seizure of components

Distinct hazards:

- physical harm capability via actuation
- safety-critical failure radius
- supply chain dependence becomes coercion vector

---

## E2 — Biologically embodied

Definition:

- The actor is coupled to biological survival constraints.

Coercion / enforcement channels:

- pain / threat-of-pain
- confinement
- deprivation (food, sleep, medical care)
- social coercion through biological vulnerability

Distinct hazards:

- coercion becomes personal and irreversible
- fear-based obedience becomes efficient
- blackmail and dependence primitives intensify

---

## Reading impact on doctrines

Embodiment regimes do not rewrite doctrine content.
They change the interpretation of specific modules:

- "constraints" must include physical channels in E1/E2
- "survival under coercion" becomes more literal in E2
- "risk classes" widen in E1/E2 due to physical consequences

---

## Translation rules (mechanical)

When reading doctrine under E1/E2, additionally assume:

1. Physical safety is a first-class constraint.
2. Containment includes physical isolation and energy control.
3. Dependence maps include energy, maintenance, and custody.
4. Coercion thresholds include bodily vulnerability (E2).

---

## Guardrail

This file does not assert that E1/E2 are common or imminent.
It only defines how to keep doctrine operational if/when embodiment exists.

Speculation is avoided by staying at the level of enforcement mechanics.
