# Regime A — Human-final (default)

In this regime, a human actor is the final bearer of responsibility.

Agents exist as an augmentation layer.

They can assist, recommend, draft, route, and even execute bounded actions, but:

- accountability is human
- legitimacy is human
- repair is human

This is the regime assumed by default across all branches.

---

## What “agent” means here

An agent is not a person.

An agent is an execution-capable layer that can:

- produce projections
- propose actions
- perform bounded actions under explicit delegation

The agent is not the final legitimacy holder.

---

## Why this is the default

Because this regime matches current reality:

- someone owns credentials and keys
- someone pays for compute and infrastructure
- someone is legally and socially sanctionable
- someone must repair consequences in the real world

Even when a human is “not visible” in a workflow, the human exists as the final bearer through ownership and liability.

---

## Reading rule

When the doctrine says:

- responsibility
- accountability
- repair
- refusal
- legitimacy

assume a human is the final endpoint.

Agents are instruments inside that endpoint.
