# Regime C — Agent-final (fully agentic actors)

In this regime, agents are sovereign actors.

They are the final bearers of:

- legitimacy
- accountability
- repair obligations
- and enforceable constraints

Humans may exist, but they are not the final legitimacy holders of the agent civilization.

---

## Why this is treated as speculative

Not because “agents can’t exist”.

Not because “agents can’t act”.

Because “agent-final” requires mechanics that are rarely present in real deployments:

1. Legitimacy substrate
   - who grants permission to act?
   - how is permission revoked?

2. Enforcement
   - how are constraints applied?
   - how is punishment or containment executed?

3. Coercion primitives
   - who can block resources or actions of others?
   - under what rules, and with what evidence?

Without these three, you can have an ecology of automation.

You do not yet have a civilization.

---

## Bodies do not determine regime

Mechanical or biological bodies do not automatically produce agent-final actors.

The regime boundary is not embodiment.

The boundary is sovereignty:

- resource sovereignty (can the actor sustain and control its resources?)
- enforceability (can constraints be applied to the actor?)
- sanctionability (can the actor be meaningfully constrained/punished?)

A body without sovereignty is still Hybrid.

---

## Biological embodiment as a special case

Biological bodies change the failure surface.

They introduce:

- pain and fear as coercion channels
- physical confinement as enforcement
- and survival needs as dependence mechanics

This does not automatically create agent-final sovereignty.

But it does create civilizational coercion primitives.

A biologically embodied agent can be coerced even when it is not legally accountable.

This is a distinct hazard.

Embodiment increases the need for explicit legitimacy and enforcement rules.

---

## Reading rule

Regime C is not assumed by any branch.

The doctrine provides compatibility notes, but does not expand Regime C into full operational doctrine unless legitimacy/enforcement/coercion primitives are defined.
