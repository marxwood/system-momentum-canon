# Operational Calendar Surface

This document defines the **Operational Calendar** as an
**interactive operational projection surface**.

The calendar is a coordination surface.
It exists to align actions, commitments, and temporal dependencies
without introducing authority or truth.

---

## 1. Purpose

The Operational Calendar exists to:

- represent planned and committed actions in time
- coordinate work between people and agents
- surface temporal conflicts and dependencies
- provide a shared operational reference

The calendar does **not** define meaning, validity, or priority by itself.

---

## 2. Canonical Dependencies

This surface depends on:

- **Core / Projections**  
  (permission for temporal projections to exist)

- **Derivatives / Projections**  
  (classification of operational projections)

- **Brain**  
  (events, commitments, actions as canonical objects)

No calendar element has authority outside these constraints.

---

## 3. Visible Elements

The calendar may render:

- time slots
- events
- tasks or commitments
- deadlines
- dependencies between entries
- ownership or responsibility markers

Rendered elements are **views**, not canonical objects themselves.

---

## 4. Permitted Interactions

Depending on governance and role, the surface may allow:

- create draft entries
- modify time allocation
- link existing canonical objects
- propose rescheduling
- mark completion or cancellation

All interactions are **requests**, not final actions,
unless explicitly governed upstream.

---

## 5. Constraints

The calendar surface must enforce:

- regime-based permission checks
- validity constraints from Brain
- immutability of finalized decisions
- separation between draft and committed states

The surface must never:

- bypass governance
- override authority
- fabricate canonical objects
- reinterpret meaning

---

## 6. People and Agents

Both people and agents may interact with the calendar.

Agents may:

- propose entries
- detect conflicts
- suggest optimizations

Agents may **not**:

- unilaterally commit actions
- alter governed decisions
- infer authority from visibility

---

## 7. Non-Equivalence Rules

- Calendar ≠ plan
- Calendar ≠ decision
- Calendar ≠ authority
- Calendar ≠ truth

The calendar is an **operational coordination surface**, nothing else.

---

## 8. Scope Discipline

This document defines:

- interaction affordances
- visible structure
- operational constraints

Narrative explanation belongs to **Doctrines**.  
Canonical rules belong to **Core**.  
Structural models belong to **Derivatives**.
