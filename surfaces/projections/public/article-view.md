# Public Article View Surface

This document defines the **Public Article View** as a
**public, read-only projection surface** for individual published artifacts.

The article view exists to present a single finalized output
in a stable, inspectable form,
without enabling interaction that could affect meaning, authority, or validity.

---

## 1. Purpose

The Public Article View exists to:

- present a finalized published artifact
- provide stable reference and citation
- expose provenance and context markers
- support public inspection without participation

The article view does **not** invite response, correction, or negotiation.

---

## 2. Canonical Dependencies

This surface depends on:

- **Core / Projections**  
  (permission for public artifact projections)

- **Derivatives / Projections**  
  (constraints on public artifact exposure)

- **Brain**  
  (canonical objects representing published outputs and their relations)

All rendered content is finalized upstream.

---

## 3. Visible Elements

The article view may render:

- primary content body
- title and identifiers
- publication timestamp
- provenance and attribution
- linked references
- immutable metadata

Elements are rendered as **final state**.

---

## 4. Permitted Interactions

The article view may allow:

- navigation between sections
- navigation to related public artifacts
- copying or referencing identifiers
- linking for citation

The surface must remain **strictly read-only**.

---

## 5. Constraints

The article view must enforce:

- immutability of published content
- clear separation from drafts or analysis
- absence of interactive authority cues
- consistency with public feed entries

The surface must never:

- allow inline modification
- expose internal deliberation
- imply validation beyond publication
- collapse visibility into endorsement

---

## 6. People and Agents

Both people and agents may consume article views.

Agents may:

- index content
- analyze text externally
- reference artifacts in downstream processes

Agents may **not**:

- treat publication as validation
- infer authority from presentation
- interact with content state

---

## 7. Non-Equivalence Rules

- Article View ≠ truth
- Article View ≠ authority
- Article View ≠ validation
- Article View ≠ deliberation

The article view is a **public presentation surface**, nothing else.

---

## 8. Scope Discipline

This document defines:

- how a single artifact is presented publicly
- what interactions are permitted
- what constraints apply

Narrative explanation belongs to **Doctrines**.  
Canonical rules belong to **Core**.  
Structural models belong to **Derivatives**.
