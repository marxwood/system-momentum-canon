# Public Feed Surface

This document defines the **Public Feed** as a
**read-oriented public projection surface**.

The public feed exists to expose selected system outputs
to broad visibility without enabling interaction
that could alter system state, meaning, or authority.

---

## 1. Purpose

The Public Feed exists to:

- provide public visibility into published outputs
- surface updates, statements, or artifacts
- support transparency without participation
- establish a stable public reference layer

The feed does **not** invite response, correction, or negotiation.

---

## 2. Canonical Dependencies

This surface depends on:

- **Core / Projections**  
  (permission for public-facing projections)

- **Derivatives / Projections**  
  (constraints on public visibility)

- **Brain**  
  (canonical objects representing published outputs)

Visibility is always selective and governed upstream.

---

## 3. Visible Elements

The public feed may render:

- published items
- timestamps and provenance markers
- source attribution
- categorization or tags
- immutable identifiers
- ordering or grouping

Rendered elements are **finalized views**, not drafts.

---

## 4. Permitted Interactions

The public feed may allow:

- navigation
- filtering
- sorting
- linking to related public surfaces

The feed must remain **read-only**.

No mutation, submission, or feedback is permitted.

---

## 5. Constraints

The public feed must enforce:

- immutability of published items
- regime-based publication gates
- separation between publication and evaluation
- absence of interactive authority cues

The surface must never:

- imply endorsement beyond publication
- expose internal state
- allow inference of internal processes
- collapse visibility into legitimacy

---

## 6. People and Agents

Both people and agents may consume the public feed.

Agents may:

- monitor publications
- index or reference items
- trigger downstream analysis

Agents may **not**:

- interact with or modify feed content
- infer authority from visibility
- treat publication as validation

---

## 7. Non-Equivalence Rules

- Public Feed ≠ authority
- Public Feed ≠ endorsement
- Public Feed ≠ truth
- Public Feed ≠ decision

The public feed is a **visibility surface**, nothing else.

---

## 8. Scope Discipline

This document defines:

- what is publicly visible
- how it may be consumed
- what constraints apply

Narrative explanation belongs to **Doctrines**.  
Canonical rules belong to **Core**.  
Structural models belong to **Derivatives**.
