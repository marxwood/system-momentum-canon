# Analytical Analysis View Surface

This document defines the **Analysis View** as an
**analytical projection surface** for structured inspection,
interpretation, and reasoning over system outputs.

The analysis view exists to support understanding and evaluation
without asserting authority, validity, or decision power.

---

## 1. Purpose

The Analysis View exists to:

- enable structured inspection of artifacts and outcomes
- support comparison, reasoning, and hypothesis formation
- connect analytical signals to their sources
- make analytical work explicit and inspectable

The analysis view does **not** decide, approve, or authorize.

---

## 2. Canonical Dependencies

This surface depends on:

- **Core / Projections**  
  (permission for analytical interpretation projections)

- **Derivatives / Projections**  
  (constraints on analytical inspection)

- **Brain**  
  (canonical objects, relations, and states under analysis)

All analysis operates on **derived representations**.

---

## 3. Visible Elements

The analysis view may render:

- selected artifacts or datasets
- analytical annotations
- comparative views
- hypotheses or interpretive notes
- links to source objects
- analytical provenance markers

Rendered elements are **analytical constructs**, not canonical facts.

---

## 4. Permitted Interactions

Depending on governance and role, the surface may allow:

- selection and grouping of objects
- creation of analytical notes
- formulation of hypotheses
- comparison across perspectives
- navigation to metrics or source surfaces

No canonical mutation occurs here.

---

## 5. Constraints

The analysis view must enforce:

- explicit separation between analysis and decision
- visibility of analytical context
- traceability to source objects
- regime-based access control

The surface must never:

- present analysis as truth
- collapse interpretation into authority
- bypass canonical evaluation
- act as a decision mechanism

---

## 6. People and Agents

Both people and agents may use the analysis view.

Agents may:

- assist with pattern detection
- generate analytical suggestions
- annotate findings

Agents may **not**:

- assert conclusions as binding
- escalate analysis into action
- infer authority from analytical position

---

## 7. Non-Equivalence Rules

- Analysis View ≠ truth
- Analysis View ≠ authority
- Analysis View ≠ decision
- Analysis View ≠ governance

The analysis view is an **interpretive surface**, nothing else.

---

## 8. Scope Discipline

This document defines:

- how analysis is performed
- what may be expressed analytically
- what constraints apply

Narrative explanation belongs to **Doctrines**.  
Canonical rules belong to **Core**.  
Structural models belong to **Derivatives**.
