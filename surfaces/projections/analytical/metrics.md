# Analytical Metrics Surface

This document defines the **Analytical Metrics** surface as an
**analytical projection surface** for quantitative inspection
of system behavior and outcomes.

The metrics surface exists to expose measurements and indicators
derived from canonical objects,
without asserting interpretation, causality, or judgment.

---

## 1. Purpose

The Analytical Metrics surface exists to:

- expose quantitative indicators
- support inspection and comparison
- enable trend detection
- provide inputs for analysis

Metrics do **not** decide, explain, or justify outcomes.

---

## 2. Canonical Dependencies

This surface depends on:

- **Core / Projections**  
  (permission for analytical projections)

- **Derivatives / Projections**  
  (constraints on analytical aggregation)

- **Brain**  
  (canonical objects and states from which metrics are derived)

Metrics are always derived, never primary.

---

## 3. Visible Elements

The metrics surface may render:

- counters and ratios
- distributions and aggregates
- time-series indicators
- thresholds and bands
- provenance of measurement
- update intervals

All values are **contextual indicators**, not conclusions.

---

## 4. Permitted Interactions

Depending on governance and role, the surface may allow:

- filtering and grouping
- time-range selection
- comparison across segments
- export for external analysis
- navigation to related analytical surfaces

No interpretation or decision is executed here.

---

## 5. Constraints

The metrics surface must enforce:

- explicit definition of measurement scope
- separation between metric and meaning
- visibility of derivation boundaries
- regime-based access control

The surface must never:

- imply causality
- substitute metrics for judgment
- collapse indicators into mandates
- override canonical evaluation

---

## 6. People and Agents

Both people and agents may consume metrics.

Agents may:

- monitor indicators
- detect anomalies
- generate analytical signals

Agents may **not**:

- treat metrics as decisions
- infer authority from thresholds
- execute actions based solely on metrics

---

## 7. Non-Equivalence Rules

- Metrics ≠ truth
- Metrics ≠ explanation
- Metrics ≠ decision
- Metrics ≠ authority

Metrics are **analytical indicators**, nothing else.

---

## 8. Scope Discipline

This document defines:

- what may be measured
- how metrics are surfaced
- what constraints apply

Interpretation belongs to **Doctrines**.  
Canonical rules belong to **Core**.  
Structural models belong to **Derivatives**.
