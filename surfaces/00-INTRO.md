# Surfaces

This document defines **Surfaces** as the **operational interaction layer**
of the System Momentum system.

Surfaces are **interactive semantic projections**
through which people and agents interact with the system.

They are not canonical.
They do not define truth, authority, or validity.
They expose **constrained interaction contracts** derived from canonical structures.

---

## 1. What Surfaces Are

Surfaces are:

- interactive interfaces
- semantic projections
- operational contracts of interaction

They define:

- what is visible
- what is selectable
- what actions are permitted
- under which constraints those actions occur

Surfaces exist to make the system **usable**, not authoritative.

---

## 2. What Surfaces Are Not

Surfaces are **not**:

- sources of truth
- holders of semantic authority
- canonical definitions
- derivatives
- doctrines
- representations of the Brain

No surface may:

- introduce new meaning
- reinterpret canonical objects
- bypass regimes or validity
- act as authority

---

## 3. Relationship to Canonical Layers

Surfaces are **derived indirectly** from the Canon.

They depend on:

- **Core / Projections**  
  (what projections are allowed to exist)

- **Derivatives / Projections**  
  (structural types and constraints)

Surfaces are **not derived from Doctrine**.
Doctrine informs _how_ surfaces should be used,
but does not define their structure or limits.

---

## 4. Projections as Interactive Surfaces

Within the Surfaces layer, **Projections** are treated as
**interactive semantic surfaces**.

A projection surface:

- renders a partial, purpose-specific view of the system
- exposes a limited set of interactions
- enforces constraints defined upstream

Projections here are **operational**, not epistemic.

---

## 5. Types of Projection Surfaces

Projection surfaces are grouped by **interaction intent**, not audience:

- **Public**  
  Read-only or minimally interactive surfaces intended for broad visibility.

- **Operational**  
  Surfaces used for coordination, planning, execution, and system operation.

- **Analytical**  
  Surfaces used for inspection, analysis, metrics, and evaluation.

Each type may have multiple concrete surfaces.

---

## 6. People and Agents

By default, both **people and agents** interact with the system
through Surfaces.

Direct interaction with canonical layers
(Core, Derivatives, Brain)
is exceptional and governance-controlled.

Surfaces are the **primary interface boundary** of the system.

---

## 7. Scope Discipline

Surfaces documentation must remain:

- concrete
- operational
- interaction-focused

Narrative explanation, failure modes, and civilizational analysis
belong to **Doctrines**, not to Surfaces.

Canonical rules belong to **Core**.
Structural models belong to **Derivatives**.

Surfaces describe **how interaction is performed**, nothing more.
