# Interpretation

Interpretation defines canonical constraints on how terms, claims, and evaluations may be interpreted.

Interpretation does not redefine meaning (KSA).  
Interpretation does not decide validity (Validity).  
Interpretation does not prescribe lived workflows (Doctrine).

Interpretation exists to prevent covert semantic or evaluative manipulation.

---

## 1. Purpose

Interpretation answers:

> What forms of interpretation are admissible without violating the Constitution or bypassing Authority?

It provides guardrails against:

- motivated reinterpretation
- selective context omission
- retroactive semantic rewriting
- collapsing uncertainty into certainty

---

## 2. Interpretation Boundaries

Interpretation must respect:

- **KSA boundaries**  
  Terms must be interpreted within declared semantic context.

- **Regime boundaries**  
  Claims must be read within their regime constraints.

- **Provenance boundaries**  
  Claims must be interpreted with their origin, chain, and evidence visibility.

---

## 3. Allowed Interpretation Forms

The Canon recognizes these admissible forms:

- **Literal interpretation**  
  Read the claim under its explicit semantics and context.

- **Contextual interpretation**  
  Read the claim with explicit additional context, without changing meaning.

- **Comparative interpretation**  
  Compare claims across regimes or semantic versions, with explicit mapping declarations.

- **Conservative interpretation**  
  Prefer minimal extrapolation when evidence is weak or contested.

Any other interpretation form must be explicitly declared as non-canonical or doctrine-specific.

---

## 4. Prohibited Interpretation Forms

- **Silent regime switching**  
  Re-reading a claim under a stricter or looser regime without declaring it.

- **Silent semantic switching**  
  Reinterpreting terms under a different KSA version without declaring it.

- **Outcome-driven reinterpretation**  
  Changing interpretation to force a desired validity outcome.

- **Projection-as-authority**  
  Treating a projection’s representation as more authoritative than the underlying claim object.

---

## 5. Interpretation as a Discipline (not a UI behavior)

Interpretation rules apply equally to:

- humans
- agents
- tools
- projections
- downstream systems

UI affordances may assist interpretation, but cannot override canonical constraints.

---

## 6. Minimal Interpretation Invariants

- Interpretation must declare semantic and regime context when non-trivial.
- No silent switching of semantics or regimes.
- Projections cannot act as authority sources.
- Interpretive additions must be explicit and traceable.
