# Mindframe

This document defines **Mindframe** as the **primary canonical derivative**
through which the System Momentum Canon is **embodied** and **enacted**
in contact with reality.

Mindframe is a **canonical construct**, not a technical system.
It exists to ensure the Canon is not merely declarative,
but operable as a **meaning-based normative order**.

Mindframe is the Canon’s **bridge to reality**:
the minimal canonical structure that allows
signals, reasoning, meaning, and commitment
to coexist without collapsing authority boundaries.

---

## 1. Canonical Status

Mindframe is part of the **Canon** and occupies a **derivative position** within it.

It is:

- mandatory
- unique
- non-optional
- non-variant

There is exactly **one Mindframe**.

All canonical systems derive from and operate through the same Mindframe.
Any system not conforming to Mindframe is non-canonical by definition.

---

## 2. Why Mindframe Exists

Mindframe exists to prevent two systemic failures:

1. **Normative abstraction collapse**  
   (The Canon exists as text, but cannot be enacted against reality.)

2. **Implementation-driven meaning**  
   (Concrete systems invent structures and meanings first, then justify them retroactively.)

Mindframe defines **what must be distinguished**
for the Canon to remain coherent when embodied:

- observation vs meaning
- meaning vs truth
- truth vs commitment
- commitment vs action
- critique vs authority

Mindframe does not define how systems implement these distinctions.
It only defines that the distinctions **must exist** and **must hold**.

---

## 3. Mindframe Is Not "Brain"

The term **Brain** implies technical runtime, machinery, or physical substrate.
This Canon does not define such a substrate.

Mindframe is **not**:

- a runtime
- a scheduler
- an event loop
- an agent framework
- a storage model
- a database schema
- a toolchain
- a deployment architecture

Mindframe is purely **epistemic**: a canonical separation of responsibility
required for meaning-based institutional coherence.

---

## 4. Mindframe as Canonical Embodiment Bridge

Mindframe is the Canon’s **embodiment bridge**:

- it enables contact with reality (signals, events, observations)
- it enables internal cognition (reasoning without authority)
- it enables semantic authority (stable meaning and invariants)
- it enables institutional memory (commitment and accountability over time)
- it enables reflective critique (evaluation without rewriting commitments)
- it defines the boundary where obligation may begin

Mindframe is not "how the system runs".
Mindframe is **what the system must never confuse**.

---

## 5. Canonical Epistemic Domains

Mindframe defines a **closed set of Canonical Epistemic Domains**.

Domains are **non-negotiable separations of epistemic responsibility**.
They do not define execution order, workflows, or technical behavior.

Each domain establishes:

- what epistemic activity may occur within it,
- what it is explicitly prohibited from doing,
- and where binding authority begins and ends.

### 5.1 Perceptual Domain

Responsible for intake of signals and observations.

Permitted:

- registering observations
- recording signals as received
- attaching provenance metadata

Prohibited:

- asserting truth
- assigning authoritative meaning
- creating obligation or commitment

Perception can be wrong.
Perception is never binding.

---

### 5.2 Cognitive Domain

Responsible for reasoning, exploration, inference, and hypothesis formation.

Permitted:

- generating hypotheses
- exploring alternatives
- deriving tentative conclusions
- proposing interpretations and candidate projections

Prohibited:

- asserting institutional truth
- changing authoritative meaning
- writing institutional commitments

Cognition is non-binding by design.

---

### 5.3 Interpretive Domain

Responsible for producing **Projections**: derived representations intended for perception, coordination, or analysis.

Permitted:

- creating projections from available state
- presenting views
- summarizing, formatting, and transforming for readability

Prohibited:

- introducing new meaning
- asserting truth
- creating obligation
- acting as authority

A Projection is revocable, regenerable, and non-authoritative.

---

### 5.4 Knowledge / Semantic Authority Domain (KSA)

Responsible for authoritative meaning, classification, and invariants.

Permitted:

- defining terms and canonical meanings
- maintaining stable semantic identity of canonical objects
- establishing classification rules consistent with Core Authority

Prohibited:

- inventing new authority outside Core
- creating institutional commitments by itself
- overriding regimes or validity rules

KSA is the sole authority over meaning,
but meaning alone is not commitment.

---

### 5.5 Institutional / Epistemic Memory Domain (IEM)

Responsible for institutional commitment, provenance, and accountability over time.

Permitted:

- recording claims and commitments
- tracking provenance, responsibility, and status over time
- preserving validity states and regime outcomes

Prohibited:

- redefining meaning (KSA territory)
- generating truth from reasoning alone
- rewriting commitments silently

IEM is where institutional memory begins.
Without IEM, there is no institution—only conversation.

---

### 5.6 Reflective Domain

Responsible for critique and evaluation of institutional state.

Permitted:

- auditing commitments
- identifying inconsistencies
- producing assessments and critiques
- proposing amendments (without enacting them)

Prohibited:

- altering commitments directly
- redefining meaning
- asserting authority by critique alone

Reflection is necessary, but never binding.

---

### 5.7 Binding / Governance Domain

Responsible for the boundary where thinking ends and institutional obligation may begin.

Permitted:

- authorizing commitments
- marking decisions as binding
- acknowledging that obligation now exists
- initiating institutionally admissible change mechanisms (as defined by Authority)

Prohibited:

- redefining meaning (KSA territory)
- rewriting history (IEM territory)
- laundering cognition into truth without admissibility checks

Binding is not execution.
Binding is the creation of institutional obligation.

---

## 6. Relationship to Core Authority

Mindframe is constrained by Core and introduces no new authority.

- **Constitution** constrains what may exist as canonical norm.
- **Authority** constrains who/what may bind and how meaning is governed.
- **Regimes** constrain admissibility and evaluation.
- **Validity** operates over institutional objects and commitments.
- **Interpretation** constrains how canonical objects are read.

Mindframe does not override Authority.  
Authority does not redefine Mindframe.  
They are mutually constraining.

---

## 7. Canonical Primitives and Vocabulary

Mindframe does not define implementation schemas or technical object models.

Mindframe operates over Canon-defined **epistemic primitives** (KSI scope)
and requires that:

- external ontologies map onto canonical primitives via declared mappings,
- mappings do not alter canonical meaning,
- failures to map imply non-canonical status.

The canonical primitive set is defined and versioned at Canon level,
not invented inside Mindframe.

---

## 8. Prohibitions

At the Canon and Mindframe level:

- technical runtime descriptions are prohibited
- execution flow definitions are prohibited
- storage and database schemas are prohibited
- toolchains and infrastructure are prohibited
- implementation object models are prohibited

Any reference to implementation-level concerns
at this level constitutes a canonical violation.

---

## 9. Stability Clause

Mindframe is stable by default.

Changes to Mindframe:

- require explicit Canon amendment
- are versioned at Canon level
- are globally breaking by definition

Silent evolution of Mindframe is forbidden.

---

## 10. Non-Equivalence Rules

- Mindframe ≠ Core
- Mindframe ≠ technical runtime
- Mindframe ≠ implementation
- Mindframe ≠ agent
- Mindframe ≠ intelligence
- Mindframe ≠ UI or projection

Mindframe is the Canon’s **epistemic embodiment bridge**—nothing else.
