# Canonical Hierarchy

This document defines the canonical hierarchy of the System Momentum Canon.

It establishes **what exists**, **at which level**, and **under what authority**, while explicitly separating
normative structure from execution, implementation, and use.

This hierarchy is **non-negotiable**. All derivations, systems, agents, and implementations must conform to it.

---

## 1. Canon (the Canonical Layer)

The **Canon** is the highest-order normative layer.

It defines:

- meaning
- boundaries
- authority
- invariants
- prohibitions
- interpretation rules

The Canon **does not act**, **does not execute**, and **does not decide**.

The Canon is composed of three internal layers:

1. **Core**
2. **Derivatives**
3. **Doctrines**

Anything outside these layers is **not canonical**.

---

## 2. Core

**Core** is the constitutional nucleus of the Canon.

It defines the **conditions of legitimacy** for all derived systems.

Core is split into two strictly separated layers:

### 2.1 Constitution

The **Constitution** defines:

- civilizational foundations
- axioms and definitions
- constraints and non-negotiables
- scope and non-goals
- interpretation boundaries
- misuse and alignment conditions

The Constitution answers the question:

> _“What must be true for any system claiming canonical alignment to exist at all?”_

The Constitution is **non-operational** and **non-executable**.

---

### 2.2 Authority

The **Authority** layer defines:

- semantic authority
- regimes of validity
- rules of interpretation
- conditions for claim acceptance and rejection
- boundaries between truth, validity, and decision

Authority answers the question:

> _“Who is allowed to define meaning, under which conditions, and with what consequences?”_

Authority governs **claims**, not actions.

---

## 3. Derivatives

**Derivatives** are canonical **capability families** derived from Core.

They:

- implement _how_ the system can act
- package capabilities under canonical constraints
- may be instantiated in multiple ways

Derivatives **must not**:

- redefine meaning
- override authority
- introduce new norms
- collapse doctrine into core

### 3.1 Derivative Families

A derivative family is a canonical grouping of capabilities.

The default derivative family is referred to as **Brain**.

---

### 3.2 Brain (Canonical Runtime Model)

**Brain** is a **canonical runtime model** (conceptual), not a technical runtime.

It represents:

- an abstract packaging of capabilities
- a reference structure for derived systems
- a convergence point for canonical constraints

Brain **is not**:

- an implementation
- an agent
- a technical runtime
- a decision-maker

All Brain realizations remain subordinate to:

- Core Constitution
- Core Authority
- Applied Doctrine

---

## 4. Doctrines

**Doctrines** define **how derivatives are used in practice**.

They:

- establish operational patterns
- define acceptable modes of use
- bind capabilities to real-world contexts

Doctrine answers the question:

> _“How is this system lived, operated, and applied?”_

Doctrines may vary without altering the Canon.

---

## 5. Implementations

**Implementations** are concrete technical realizations.

They:

- instantiate derivatives
- apply doctrines
- execute within real constraints

Implementations:

- are replaceable
- are not canonical
- carry no authority by themselves

An implementation may be aligned — but never authoritative.

---

## 6. Projections

**Projections** are operational views derived from system state.

They:

- render information for agents or humans
- do not define validity
- do not alter authority
- may be regenerated at any time

Examples include (non-exhaustive):

- dashboards
- calendars
- reports
- interfaces

Projections are **artifacts**, not capabilities.

---

## 7. Hierarchy Summary

Canon
├─ Core
│ ├─ Constitution
│ └─ Authority
├─ Derivatives
│ └─ Brain (derivation target)
├─ Doctrines
└─ Implementations
└─ Projections

---

## 8. Non-Equivalence Rule

No layer may be collapsed into another.

In particular:

- Canon ≠ System
- Core ≠ Runtime
- Authority ≠ Intelligence
- Derivative ≠ Implementation
- Doctrine ≠ Policy
- Projection ≠ Capability

Any system violating this hierarchy **ceases to be canonically aligned**.
