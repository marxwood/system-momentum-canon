# Intent

This document defines **Intent** as the canonical concept
through which institutional agency is declared and epistemic flows are activated.

Intent is a **semantic precondition** to any legitimate activity within Brain.
Without declared Intent, no flow through Canonical Functional Domains may occur.

Intent is **pre-Canon by definition**: it must exist conceptually
before Brain can be enacted as a normative system.

---

## 1. Canonical Status

Intent is part of the **Canon** and exists at a **foundational semantic level**.

It is:

- mandatory for all institutional acts
- semantically closed
- non-negotiable
- non-automatable

Intent is not a layer, domain, or object.
Intent is the **declaration of institutional agency** that enables flows.

---

## 2. What Intent Is

Intent is an **explicit institutional declaration**
that selects which flows through Brain domains are permitted for a given act.

Intent:

- precedes all domain activity
- enables specific flows
- disables all non-declared flows
- carries no execution semantics

Intent does not execute anything.
Intent permits or forbids flows.

---

## 3. What Intent Is Not

Intent is not:

- an event
- a user action
- a heuristic
- an automatism
- a system inference
- a task or workflow

If something occurs without a declared Intent, it is not an institutional act.

---

## 4. Relationship to Governance

Intent and Governance are distinct but bound:

- **Intent (semantics)**: what is possible to intend within this system
- **Governance (operations)**: who may activate which Intent, when, and under what conditions

Intent is defined at Canon level.
Governance operationalizes Intent within institutional constraints.

Intent without Governance is inert.
Governance without Intent has nothing to activate.

---

## 5. Canonical Intent Taxonomy

The Canon recognizes the following **closed set of Intents**:

### I1. Exploratory

**Purpose**: Thinking, exploration, hypothesis generation.

**Permitted flows**:
- Perceptual → Cognitive

**Prohibited**:
- IEM
- Action / Governance decisions

No obligation. No memory. No institutional consequence.

---

### I2. Semantic Inquiry

**Purpose**: Understanding meaning, classification, definition.

**Permitted flows**:
- Cognitive → Knowledge (read-only)

**Prohibited**:
- IEM
- Action / Governance decisions

Pure meaning. No world. No commitment.

---

### I3. Archival

**Purpose**: Preserving input without conclusion or commitment.

**Permitted flows**:
- Perceptual → Percept Store

**Prohibited**:
- IEM
- Governance decisions

Evidence without truth. Record without obligation.

---

### I4. Epistemic Commitment

**Purpose**: Institutional assumption of responsibility for a claim.

**Permitted flows**:
- Perceptual → Cognitive → Governance → IEM

**Required**:
- Named authority
- Provenance
- Validity conditions

This is the most consequential Intent.
It creates institutional memory and accountability.

---

### I5. Reflection

**Purpose**: Learning from institutional behavior over time.

**Permitted flows**:
- IEM → Reflective → (recommendations)

**Prohibited**:
- Direct modification of IEM

Meta-knowledge about institutional patterns.
Not truth about the world.

---

## 6. Intent Rules

The following rules are **non-negotiable**:

1. **Intent precedes flow**
   If there is no Intent, there is no legitimate flow.

2. **One Intent per institutional act**
   Mixing Intents within a single act is forbidden.

3. **Intent selects flows, not the reverse**
   Domains do not activate themselves.

4. **Only Governance activates Intent**
   Even when delegated to an agent, activation authority remains with Governance.

5. **Intent is auditable**
   Every institutional act must have a recorded Intent.

---

## 7. Closure Rule

The set of canonical Intents is **closed**.

No Intent may be added, removed, or redefined
without an explicit Canon amendment.

Any flow activated by an undeclared or non-canonical Intent
is non-canonical by definition.

---

## 8. Non-Equivalence Rules

- Intent ≠ event
- Intent ≠ task
- Intent ≠ workflow
- Intent ≠ user action
- Intent ≠ system trigger
- Intent ≠ automation rule

Intent is a **canonical semantic declaration**, nothing else.

---

## 9. Prohibition of Implementation Concerns

At the Canon level:

- Intent routing mechanisms are prohibited
- Intent detection algorithms are prohibited
- Automatic Intent inference is prohibited
- Intent-to-workflow mappings are prohibited

Any reference to **technical implementation of Intent**
at this level constitutes a **canonical violation**.

Implementation-level concerns belong exclusively to operational specifications.

---

## 10. Stability Clause

Intent is **stable by default**.

Changes to Intent:

- require explicit Canon amendment
- are versioned at Canon level
- are globally breaking by definition

Silent evolution of Intent is forbidden.
